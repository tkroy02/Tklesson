Section 8.6: Bernoulli’s Principle

Bernoulli’s principle describes the relationship between pressure, velocity, and height in a flowing fluid. It is derived from energy conservation for incompressible, non-viscous fluids.

Bernoulli’s Equation: \[ P + \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 + \rho g h = \text{constant} \] Where:
  • \(P\) = fluid pressure
  • \(\rho\) = fluid density
  • \(v\) = fluid velocity
  • \(g\) = acceleration due to gravity
  • \(h\) = height above reference
Key Idea: Increase in fluid speed corresponds to a decrease in pressure, and vice versa.

Example 1

Water flows horizontally through a pipe, speed increases from 2 m/s to 5 m/s. Initial pressure 200 kPa. Find final pressure. Assume \(\rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m³}\).

Using Bernoulli: \(P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 = P_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2\)

\(P_2 = 200000 + 0.5*1000*(2^2) - 0.5*1000*(5^2) = 187500 \text{ Pa}\)

Example 2

A horizontal jet of fluid exits a pipe at 3 m/s with pressure 150 kPa. If cross-section narrows and speed becomes 6 m/s, find pressure.

\(P_2 = 150000 + 0.5*1000*(3^2) - 0.5*1000*(6^2) = 127500 \text{ Pa}\)

Practice Problems

  1. Fluid flows horizontally: speed doubles, initial pressure 120 kPa. Find final pressure.
  2. Water flows through pipe rising 2 m vertically. Initial pressure 100 kPa, speed 4 m/s. Find pressure at top.
  3. Horizontal flow: speed triples, pressure drops 50 kPa. Find initial speed if \(\rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m³}\).
  4. Venturi tube: narrow section 0.05 m², wide 0.2 m². Flow speed in wide section 2 m/s. Find pressure difference.
  5. Pipe with height difference 5 m. Speed constant 3 m/s. Initial pressure 200 kPa. Find pressure at higher point.
  6. Fluid flows from 1 m² to 0.25 m². Initial pressure 250 kPa, speed 1 m/s. Find final pressure.
  7. Horizontal water flow. Pressure decreases 20 kPa. Speed change? Assume \(\rho = 1000\) kg/m³.
  8. Pipe horizontal, pressure 100 kPa, speed 3 m/s. Diameter halves. Find speed and pressure at narrow section.
  9. Water speed 5 m/s, pressure 200 kPa. Horizontal flow, pressure drops to 150 kPa. Find speed.
  10. Flow along sloped pipe. Height difference 3 m. Initial pressure 120 kPa. Speed change 2 m/s. Find final pressure.
