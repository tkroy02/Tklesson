Section 8.6: Bernoulli’s Principle

Bernoulli’s principle relates the pressure, velocity, and height of an incompressible, non-viscous fluid in steady flow along a streamline.

Bernoulli’s Equation: \[ P + \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 + \rho g h = \text{constant} \] where \(P\) is fluid pressure, \(\rho\) density, \(v\) velocity, \(h\) height, and \(g\) gravity.
Implications:
  • Where speed increases, pressure decreases.
  • Useful for lift, fluid flow, and pipe systems.

Example 1

Water flows horizontally through a pipe with speed 2 m/s and pressure 120 kPa. It narrows and speed becomes 4 m/s. Find new pressure (density 1000 kg/m³).

\(P_2 = P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho(v_1^2 - v_2^2) = 120000 + 0.5*1000*(4^2-2^2) = 120000 - 6000 = 114000 \text{ Pa}\)

Example 2

Air flows over a venturi tube: velocity increases from 5 m/s to 15 m/s. Find pressure drop. Air density 1.2 kg/m³.

\(\Delta P = 0.5 \rho (v_2^2 - v_1^2) = 0.5*1.2*(15^2 - 5^2) = 120 \text{ Pa drop}\)
