Section 8.8: Superposition of Fields

Magnetic fields from multiple sources can be added vectorially. This is the principle of superposition:

\[ \vec{B}_{\text{total}} = \sum_i \vec{B}_i \]

Each \(\vec{B}_i\) represents the magnetic field contribution from a single current distribution.

Key Concept:

Superposition allows complex magnetic field problems to be simplified into sums of fields from individual sources.
