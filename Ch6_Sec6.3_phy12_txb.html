Section 6.3: Impedance & Reactance

In AC circuits, the opposition to current flow is not just resistance but also includes reactance. Together, they form impedance.

  • Resistance (R): Opposition due to resistors (independent of frequency).
  • Inductive Reactance (XL): \( X_L = \omega L \).
  • Capacitive Reactance (XC): \( X_C = \dfrac{1}{\omega C} \).
  • Impedance (Z): For a series RLC circuit:

    \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \).

Impedance determines the RMS current in the circuit according to Ohmâ€™s law for AC: \( I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{Z} \).

Example

An AC circuit has \( R = 10 \,\Omega \), \( L = 0.1 \,\text{H} \), and \( C = 100 \,\mu\text{F} \). It is connected to a source of frequency 50 Hz. Find the impedance.

Solution:

Angular frequency: \( \omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi (50) = 314 \,\text{rad/s} \).

\( X_L = \omega L = 314 \times 0.1 = 31.4 \,\Omega \).

\( X_C = \dfrac{1}{\omega C} = \dfrac{1}{314 \times 100 \times 10^{-6}} \approx 31.8 \,\Omega \).

\( Z = \sqrt{10^2 + (31.4 - 31.8)^2} \approx \sqrt{100 + 0.16} \approx 10.0 \,\Omega \).
