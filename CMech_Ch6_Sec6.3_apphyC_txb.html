Section 6.3: Pendulums

Pendulums exhibit simple harmonic motion for small angular displacements. The restoring force is due to gravity, and energy oscillates between kinetic and potential forms.

Simple Pendulum:

Restoring force: \( F = -mg \sin\theta \approx -mg \theta \) for small angles

Angular frequency: \( \omega = \sqrt{\frac{g}{L}} \)

Period: \( T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} \)

Total mechanical energy: \( E = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 + m g h \)

Example 1

A pendulum of length 2 m swings with small amplitude. Find its period.

\( T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{2}{9.8}} \approx 2.84 \text{ s} \)

Example 2

Compute the speed of a 0.5 kg pendulum bob at the lowest point if released from height 0.1 m.

Using energy conservation: \( m g h = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \)

\( v = \sqrt{2 g h} = \sqrt{2*9.8*0.1} \approx 1.4 \text{ m/s} \)

Practice Problems

  1. A 1.2 m pendulum is released from 10° angle. Find approximate period.
  2. A bob of 0.3 kg swings from 0.05 m height. Find its speed at the bottom.
  3. Compute period of a pendulum on the Moon where \( g = 1.62 \text{ m/s²} \) and L = 1 m.
  4. A pendulum with length 0.5 m has period 1.42 s. Verify the local g.
  5. Find maximum kinetic energy of a 0.2 kg pendulum swinging from 0.1 m height.
