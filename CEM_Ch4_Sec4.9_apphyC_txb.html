Section 4.9: Nonuniform Field Calculations

Capacitors with nonuniform fields require calculus to determine the potential difference and stored energy.

Field & Potential:

For a spatially varying electric field \( E(x) \):

\[ V = - \int E(x) \, dx \]

Energy:

The energy stored can be expressed as:

\[ U = \frac{1}{2} \int \epsilon(x) E^2(x) \, dV \]

Example 1

A parallel-plate capacitor has a dielectric with permittivity varying linearly from \( \epsilon_0 \) to \( 3\epsilon_0 \) along the plates separated by \( d \). Compute approximate energy stored for voltage \( V \).

Assume \( \epsilon(x) = \epsilon_0 \left(1 + \frac{2x}{d}\right) \), then

\[ U = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^d \epsilon(x) \frac{V^2}{d^2} A \, dx = \frac{1}{2} \frac{V^2 A}{d^2} \int_0^d \epsilon_0 \left(1 + \frac{2x}{d}\right) dx = \frac{1}{2} \frac{V^2 A}{d^2} \epsilon_0 \left[ x + \frac{x^2}{d} \right]_0^d = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 V^2 A \cdot \frac{2}{d} = \frac{\epsilon_0 V^2 A}{d} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Compute potential difference for a capacitor with linear dielectric variation.
  2. Calculate energy stored for a radial dielectric distribution in a cylindrical capacitor.
  3. Explain why nonuniform dielectrics require integral calculations.
  4. Given \( \epsilon(x) = \epsilon_0 e^{x/d} \), compute total capacitance.
  5. Discuss physical implications of varying permittivity on stored energy.
