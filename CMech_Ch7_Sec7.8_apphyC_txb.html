Section 7.8: Advanced Problems

Advanced gravitation problems that integrate multiple concepts: Newton's Law, orbital mechanics, energy, and Kepler's laws.

Advanced Problems

  1. Calculate the gravitational force between Earth and Moon. Use \( M_E = 5.97\times10^{24} \) kg, \( M_M = 7.35\times10^{22} \) kg, \( r = 3.84\times10^8 \) m.
  2. A satellite orbits Earth at 300 km altitude. Compute its orbital speed and period.
  3. Find the height for a geostationary orbit around Earth.
  4. Given a planet's mass and radius, calculate the escape velocity.
  5. Determine the potential energy of a spacecraft at 1000 km above Earth.
  6. Using Kepler’s third law, compute the period of a satellite 42,000 km from Earth’s center.
  7. A comet moves in an elliptical orbit. Find its speed at perihelion.
  8. Two satellites orbiting at different altitudes. Compare their orbital velocities.
  9. Determine the centripetal acceleration of a satellite in circular orbit at 15,000 km from Earth.
  10. Compute the total mechanical energy of a satellite in low Earth orbit.
