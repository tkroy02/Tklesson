Section 7.6: Helical Motion

If a charged particle has a velocity component parallel to the magnetic field, it undergoes helical motion: circular motion in the plane perpendicular to B and linear motion along B.

Velocity Components:

\[ v_\perp = \text{perpendicular to B}, \quad v_\parallel = \text{parallel to B}, \quad r = \frac{mv_\perp}{qB}, \quad \lambda = v_\parallel T \] where \( r \) is radius, \( \lambda \) is pitch of helix, \( T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB} \).

Example 1

A proton enters a 0.2 T field with \( v_\perp = 2\times10^5\,\text{m/s} \) and \( v_\parallel = 1\times10^5\,\text{m/s} \). Find the radius and pitch.

\( r = \frac{mv_\perp}{qB} = \frac{1.67\times10^{-27}\cdot 2\times10^5}{1.6\times10^{-19}\cdot 0.2} \approx 0.0104\,\text{m} \)

\( T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB} = \frac{2\pi \cdot 1.67\times10^{-27}}{1.6\times10^{-19} \cdot 0.2} \approx 3.28\times10^{-7}\,\text{s} \)

\( \lambda = v_\parallel T \approx 1\times10^5 \cdot 3.28\times10^{-7} \approx 0.0328\,\text{m} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Determine the pitch of an electron moving in a 0.1 T field with given \( v_\parallel \) and \( v_\perp \).
  2. Compare the radius for electrons and protons at same \( v_\perp \) and B.
  3. Explain why the motion is helical instead of circular.
  4. Calculate the time period of one loop.
  5. Describe the motion when \( v_\parallel = 0 \).
