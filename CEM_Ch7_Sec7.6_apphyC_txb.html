Section 7.6: Helical Motion

When a charged particle enters a uniform magnetic field at an angle other than 0° or 90°, its velocity has both parallel and perpendicular components. The particle follows a helical path around the magnetic field lines.

Velocity Components:

\[ v_\parallel = v \cos\theta, \quad v_\perp = v \sin\theta \]

Where \( \theta \) is the angle between the velocity and magnetic field.

Helical Radius:

\[ r = \frac{mv_\perp}{qB} \]

Determined by the perpendicular component of velocity.

Pitch of Helix:

\[ p = v_\parallel T = \frac{2\pi m v_\parallel}{qB} \]

Distance traveled along the field direction in one complete turn.

Example 1

An alpha particle (\(m = 6.64 \times 10^{-27}\, \text{kg}, q = 3.2 \times 10^{-19}\, \text{C}\)) enters a 0.1 T uniform magnetic field at 45° with speed \(2 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\). Calculate the radius of the helical path and the pitch.

Perpendicular component: \[ v_\perp = v \sin 45^\circ = 2 \times 10^6 \times 0.707 \approx 1.414 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s} \]

Radius: \[ r = \frac{mv_\perp}{qB} = \frac{6.64 \times 10^{-27} \times 1.414 \times 10^6}{3.2 \times 10^{-19} \times 0.1} \approx 0.293\,\text{m} \]

Parallel component: \[ v_\parallel = v \cos 45^\circ \approx 1.414 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s} \]

Period: \[ T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB} = \frac{2\pi \times 6.64 \times 10^{-27}}{3.2 \times 10^{-19} \times 0.1} \approx 1.303 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{s} \]

Pitch: \[ p = v_\parallel T \approx 1.414 \times 10^6 \times 1.303 \times 10^{-6} \approx 1.84\,\text{m} \]

Practice Problems

  1. A proton enters a 0.2 T field at 60° with speed \(1 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\). Find the radius and pitch of its helical path.
  2. An electron moves at 30° to a uniform 0.05 T field with speed \(5 \times 10^5\,\text{m/s}\). Calculate the period of revolution and pitch.
  3. Explain why the helix radius depends only on the perpendicular component of velocity.
  4. Derive the expression for the pitch of the helix in terms of \(v, m, q, B\) and \(\theta\).
  5. A charged particle moves in a helical path. Discuss how changing the magnetic field strength affects the radius and pitch.
