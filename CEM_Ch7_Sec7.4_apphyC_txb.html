Section 7.4: Motion in Uniform Magnetic Field

A charged particle moving in a uniform magnetic field experiences a Lorentz force perpendicular to both its velocity and the magnetic field. This causes circular or helical motion.

Equations:

\[ \vec{F} = q \vec{v} \times \vec{B}, \quad r = \frac{mv}{qB}, \quad \omega = \frac{qB}{m} \] where \( r \) is the radius of circular motion, \( \omega \) is the angular frequency, \( q \) is the particle charge, \( m \) is its mass, and \( v \) is the speed perpendicular to \( \vec{B} \).

Example 1

An electron moves perpendicular to a 0.01 T magnetic field with speed \( 2 \times 10^6 \, \text{m/s} \). Find the radius of its circular path.

Radius: \( r = \frac{mv}{qB} = \frac{9.11\times10^{-31} \cdot 2\times10^6}{1.6\times10^{-19} \cdot 0.01} \approx 0.114 \, \text{m} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the frequency of circular motion for a proton in a 0.02 T field.
  2. Describe the trajectory of a charged particle entering the field at an angle.
  3. Explain why the speed of the particle remains constant in uniform B.
  4. Derive the equation for the pitch of a helical motion.
  5. Compare the motion of electrons and protons in the same magnetic field.
