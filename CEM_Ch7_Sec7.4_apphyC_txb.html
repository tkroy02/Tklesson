Section 7.4: Motion in Uniform Magnetic Field

A charged particle entering a uniform magnetic field experiences a force perpendicular to its velocity. The resulting motion depends on the angle between the velocity and the field.

Velocity Perpendicular to Field:

Particle moves in a circular path with radius:

\[ r = \frac{mv}{qB} \]

Velocity Parallel to Field:

Particle moves in a straight line along the field; no magnetic force acts along this direction.

Oblique Entry (Helical Motion):

Particle velocity has components parallel and perpendicular to field, producing a helical trajectory.

\[ v_\parallel = v \cos\theta, \quad v_\perp = v \sin\theta \]

Example 1

An electron (\(m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\, \text{kg}, q = -1.6 \times 10^{-19}\, \text{C}\)) enters a uniform magnetic field of 0.01 T with velocity \(2 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\) at 90° to the field. Find the radius of its circular path.

\[ r = \frac{mv}{qB} = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(2 \times 10^6)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.01)} \approx 1.14 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m} \]

Practice Problems

  1. A proton enters a 0.5 T uniform field at 30° with velocity \(10^6\,\text{m/s}\). Describe the motion and compute circular radius of perpendicular component.
  2. An electron moves parallel to a 0.1 T field. Explain the path and compute acceleration.
  3. Calculate the pitch (distance between turns) of a helical path for a proton with velocity components \(v_\parallel = 2 \times 10^5\,\text{m/s}\) and \(v_\perp = 1 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\) in 0.2 T field.
  4. Discuss how period of revolution depends on charge and mass.
  5. Derive general expression for helical radius and pitch for oblique motion in uniform magnetic field.
