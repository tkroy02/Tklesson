Section 8.3: Buoyancy and Archimedes' Principle

Objects submerged in a fluid experience an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced (Archimedes’ Principle):

\[ F_b = \rho_\text{fluid} g V_\text{submerged} \]

Where \(F_b\) is the buoyant force, \(\rho_\text{fluid}\) is the fluid density, \(g\) is gravitational acceleration, and \(V_\text{submerged}\) is the submerged volume.

Applications

  • Floating objects and equilibrium.
  • Determining whether objects sink or float.
  • Designing ships, submarines, and balloons.

Example 1

A cube of volume 0.02 m³ and density 600 kg/m³ is placed in water. Find the buoyant force and whether it floats.

Buoyant force: \( F_b = \rho_\text{water} g V = 1000*9.8*0.02 = 196 \, \text{N} \)

Weight of cube: \( W = \rho_\text{cube} g V = 600*9.8*0.02 = 117.6 \, \text{N} \)

Since \(F_b > W\), the cube floats.

Example 2

A sphere of volume 0.005 m³ and density 1200 kg/m³ is submerged in water. Determine buoyant force and sinking behavior.

Buoyant force: \( F_b = 1000*9.8*0.005 = 49 \, \text{N} \)

Weight: \( W = 1200*9.8*0.005 = 58.8 \, \text{N} \)

Since \(W > F_b\), the sphere sinks.

Practice Problems

  1. A block of density 500 kg/m³ floats in water. Find the fraction submerged.
  2. A cylinder 0.1 m³ is fully submerged in oil (\(\rho=800\) kg/m³). Calculate buoyant force.
  3. Determine if an iron cube (density 7800 kg/m³) of 0.001 m³ sinks or floats in water.
  4. A balloon displaces 0.02 m³ of air (\(\rho=1.2\) kg/m³). Find buoyant force.
  5. Wooden plank floats in water. Plank volume 0.05 m³, density 600 kg/m³. Fraction submerged?
  6. A ship displaces 500 m³ of seawater. Buoyant force acting on the ship?
  7. Submarine submerged 100 m, water density 1025 kg/m³. Buoyant force per m³?
  8. Floating cube, weight 50 N, water density 1000 kg/m³. Volume submerged?
  9. Find upward force on a 0.02 m³ block of aluminum (\(\rho=2700\) kg/m³) in water.
  10. A raft floats with 30% of its volume submerged. What is the raft density?
