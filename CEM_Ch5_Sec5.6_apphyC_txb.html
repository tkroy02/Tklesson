Section 5.6: Series Resistor Circuits

In a series circuit, resistors are connected end-to-end, so the same current flows through all components. The total resistance is the sum of individual resistances:

\[ R_{total} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + \dots \]

The voltage divides among resistors proportionally to their resistances:

\[ V_n = I R_n \]

Key Points:
  • Current is the same through all resistors.
  • Voltage divides according to resistance.
  • Series circuits fail if any resistor is removed.

Example 1

Three resistors, 2 Ω, 3 Ω, and 5 Ω, are connected in series to a 10 V battery. Find the current and voltage drop across each resistor.

Total resistance: \( R_{total} = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 \, \Omega \)

Current: \( I = V / R_{total} = 10 / 10 = 1 \, A \)

Voltage drops: \( V_1 = 2 \cdot 1 = 2 V, \, V_2 = 3 \cdot 1 = 3 V, \, V_3 = 5 \cdot 1 = 5 V \)

Practice Problems

  1. Find the total resistance and current for a series circuit with resistors 4 Ω, 6 Ω, 10 Ω, powered by a 12 V source.
  2. Calculate voltage drops across each resistor if the total current is 2 A.
  3. Explain what happens to the current if one resistor in the series burns out.
  4. Derive the formula for series resistance from Ohm’s Law.
  5. A 24 V battery is connected to a series circuit of 3 resistors: 2 Ω, 3 Ω, 5 Ω. Compute power dissipated by each resistor.
