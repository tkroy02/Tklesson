Section 10.8: Boundary Conditions for Fields

This section explains how electric and magnetic fields behave at the interface of different materials.

Electric Field Boundary Conditions:

The tangential components of \(\vec{E}\) are continuous across the boundary: \[ E_{1t} = E_{2t} \] The normal component discontinuity is proportional to surface charge density \(\sigma\): \[ E_{2n} - E_{1n} = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} \]

Magnetic Field Boundary Conditions:

The tangential components of \(\vec{B}\) have a discontinuity proportional to surface current density \(\vec{K}\): \[ B_{2t} - B_{1t} = \mu_0 \vec{K} \times \hat{n} \] The normal components are continuous: \[ B_{1n} = B_{2n} \]

Example Problem

Find the electric field just above a conducting sheet with surface charge density \(\sigma = 5 \,\mu C/m^2\).

Using boundary condition for normal component:

\[ E_{above} - E_{below} = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} \Rightarrow E_{above} = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} \approx \frac{5 \times 10^{-6}}{8.85 \times 10^{-12}} \approx 5.65 \times 10^5 \,\mathrm{N/C} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Explain why the tangential component of \(\vec{E}\) is continuous across boundaries.
  2. Compute the magnetic field just outside a surface carrying current density \(K = 2\,\mathrm{A/m}\).
  3. Discuss the effect of a dielectric slab on normal and tangential components of \(\vec{E}\).
