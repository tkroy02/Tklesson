Section 10.8: Boundary Conditions for Fields

This section covers the boundary conditions that electric and magnetic fields must satisfy at interfaces between different media.

Electric Field Boundary Conditions:
  • Normal component: \( E_{2\perp} - E_{1\perp} = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} \) (surface charge density)
  • Tangential component: \( E_{2\parallel} = E_{1\parallel} \) (continuous across boundary)
Magnetic Field Boundary Conditions:
  • Normal component: \( B_{2\perp} = B_{1\perp} \) (continuous)
  • Tangential component: \( B_{2\parallel} - B_{1\parallel} = \mu_0 K \) (surface current density K)
Implications:
  • Determine reflection and refraction of EM waves at interfaces.
  • Used in waveguides, capacitors, and magnetic shielding calculations.

Example 1

An EM wave strikes a dielectric boundary. If the surface has no free charges, what are the boundary conditions for E and B?

Since \(\sigma = 0\) and \( K = 0 \):

  • Electric field tangential component continuous: \( E_{2\parallel} = E_{1\parallel} \)
  • Magnetic field tangential component continuous: \( B_{2\parallel} = B_{1\parallel} \)
  • Normal components of E and B may change depending on permittivity/permeability: \( E_{2\perp} \neq E_{1\perp}, B_{2\perp} = B_{1\perp} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Determine the tangential and normal components of E at the surface of a conductor.
  2. Find the boundary conditions for B at the interface of two magnetic media with surface current.
  3. Explain why E tangential component is continuous across any boundary.
  4. Apply boundary conditions to derive Fresnel equations for reflection and transmission.
  5. Calculate B field above a sheet carrying uniform surface current K.
