Section 11.5: Applications & Review

This section combines trigonometric concepts to solve real-world problems and provides review exercises covering previous trig topics.

Example 1: Angle of Elevation

A person observes the top of a building at an angle of elevation of 30° from a point 50 m away. Find the height of the building (ignoring the person’s height).

Step 1: Use tangent: \( \tan 30^\circ = h / 50 \)

Step 2: Solve for h: \( h = 50 \cdot \tan 30^\circ \approx 50 \cdot 0.577 = 28.85 \) m

Example 2: Harmonic Motion

A mass on a spring oscillates according to \( x(t) = 5\sin(2t) \) cm. Find the amplitude and period of the motion.

Step 1: Amplitude is the coefficient of sine: 5 cm

Step 2: Period formula: \( T = \frac{2\pi}{\text{frequency}} = \frac{2\pi}{2} = \pi \) seconds

Practice Problems

  1. A ladder leans against a wall forming an angle of 60° with the ground. If the foot is 4 m from the wall, find the height reached by the ladder.
  2. Find the amplitude and period of \( y = 3\cos(4t) \)
  3. A ship sails northeast. The bearing is 45° with the east direction. Find the north and east components of a 100 km path.
  4. Verify the identity \( \tan x + \cot x = \frac{1}{\sin x \cos x} \)
  5. Solve \( 2\cos^2 x - 1 = 0 \) for \( 0 \le x < 2\pi \)
