Section 9.7: Impedance in RLC Circuits

An RLC circuit contains a resistor (R), inductor (L), and capacitor (C) in series or parallel. The opposition to AC current is called impedance.

Series RLC Circuit Impedance:

\[ Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \]

Where \( X_L = \omega L \) is inductive reactance, \( X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C} \) is capacitive reactance, and \( \omega = 2 \pi f \).

Phase Angle:

\[ \phi = \tan^{-1}\frac{X_L - X_C}{R} \]

Phase angle indicates the phase difference between voltage and current.

Current in Series RLC:

\[ I = \frac{V_\text{rms}}{Z} \]

Current depends on total impedance of the circuit.

Example 1

A series RLC circuit has R = 10 Ω, L = 0.2 H, C = 50 μF, and frequency f = 50 Hz. Find the impedance and phase angle.

Angular frequency: \( \omega = 2 \pi f = 314.16 \, \text{rad/s} \)

Inductive reactance: \( X_L = \omega L = 314.16 \cdot 0.2 \approx 62.83 \, \Omega \)

Capacitive reactance: \( X_C = 1/(\omega C) = 1 / (314.16 \cdot 50\times10^{-6}) \approx 63.66 \, \Omega \)

Impedance: \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} = \sqrt{10^2 + (62.83 - 63.66)^2} \approx \sqrt{10^2 + (-0.83)^2} \approx 10.03 \, \Omega \)

Phase angle: \( \phi = \tan^{-1}\frac{X_L - X_C}{R} = \tan^{-1}(-0.83/10) \approx -4.8^\circ \)

Practice Problems

  1. A series RLC circuit has R = 5 Ω, L = 0.1 H, C = 100 μF, f = 60 Hz. Calculate impedance and phase angle.
  2. Determine the current in a series RLC circuit with V_rms = 12 V and impedance Z = 15 Ω.
  3. Explain how the current leads or lags the voltage depending on X_L and X_C.
  4. For a series RLC, X_L = X_C. What is the impedance and current phase angle?
  5. Sketch phasor diagram for a series RLC circuit with R, L, and C values of your choice.
