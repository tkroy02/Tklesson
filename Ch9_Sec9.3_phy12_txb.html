Section 9.3: Time Dilation

Time dilation is a phenomenon in special relativity where a moving clock runs slower relative to a stationary observer. It is calculated using the Lorentz factor.

Time Dilation Formula:

\( t' = \frac{t}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} \)

  • \(t'\) = time interval measured by moving observer
  • \(t\) = time interval measured by stationary observer
  • \(v\) = relative velocity
  • \(c\) = speed of light

Example 1

A spaceship travels at 0.9c. If 10 years pass on Earth, how much time passes on the spaceship?

\( t' = 10 \sqrt{1 - 0.9^2} = 10 \sqrt{0.19} \approx 4.36 \text{ years} \)
