Section 9.9: Mixed Practice

Practice problems combining time dilation, length contraction, velocity addition, and relativistic energy-mass equivalence. Apply all strategies learned from previous sections.

Practice Problems

  1. A spaceship moves at 0.6c. Determine the time elapsed on a clock on Earth after 3 hours onboard.
  2. A rod of length 50 m moves at 0.8c. Find its contracted length as observed from a stationary frame.
  3. Two spaceships move toward each other at 0.5c and 0.7c relative to Earth. Find their relative speed.
  4. A particle of mass 2 kg moves at 0.9c. Compute its relativistic energy.
  5. Light signal emitted from a moving source at 0.6c. Draw a spacetime diagram showing emission and reception events.
  6. A muon travels at 0.98c. Its lifetime at rest is 2.2 Î¼s. How long does it last in Earth's frame?
  7. A spacecraft moves at 0.7c and sends signals at 0.5c relative to it. Find speed relative to Earth.
  8. Determine kinetic energy of a 1 kg particle moving at 0.95c.
  9. A length of 100 m moving at 0.85c passes a stationary observer. Find contracted length.
  10. Using velocity addition, find speed of light signal sent from a moving spaceship at 0.8c.
