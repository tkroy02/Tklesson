Section 8.4: Wave Speed

Waves move energy from one point to another, and the rate at which this happens is called wave speed. Wave speed depends on the medium and the type of wave.

Wave Speed Formulas

The speed \(v\) of a wave can be calculated in three equivalent ways:

  • Distance / Time: \(v = \frac{\text{distance travelled by the wave}}{\text{time taken}}\)
  • Using frequency: \(v = f \lambda\)
  • Using period: \(v = \frac{\lambda}{T}\), where \(T\) is the period of the wave

Where:

  • \(v\) = wave speed (m/s)
  • \(\lambda\) = wavelength (m)
  • \(f\) = frequency (Hz)
  • \(T\) = period (s)

Examples

1. A water wave has a wavelength of 2 m and a frequency of 2 Hz. Find the speed:

\(v = f \lambda = 2 \times 2 = 4 \, \mathrm{m/s}\)

2. A sound wave has a wavelength of 0.5 m and a period of 0.001 s. Find the speed:

\(v = \frac{\lambda}{T} = \frac{0.5}{0.001} = 500 \, \mathrm{m/s}\)

3. A wave travels 10 m in 2 s. Find its speed using distance/time:

\(v = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{time}} = \frac{10}{2} = 5 \, \mathrm{m/s}\)

Key Points

  • Wave speed is determined by the medium and type of wave.
  • Higher frequency waves with the same wavelength travel at the same speed.
  • For mechanical waves, speed depends on properties like tension and density (for strings) or elasticity and density (for solids and fluids).
  • For electromagnetic waves in vacuum, all waves travel at the constant speed \(c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \, \mathrm{m/s}\).
