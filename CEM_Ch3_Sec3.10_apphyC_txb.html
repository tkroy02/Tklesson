Section 3.10: Electric Potential and Potential Energy in a Uniform Field

In a uniform electric field, the potential difference between two points is related to the field strength and the displacement along the field lines.

Key Relation:

\[ \Delta V = -E \Delta x \]

where \(E\) is the magnitude of the uniform field and \(\Delta x\) is the displacement parallel to the field.

The potential energy of a charge is given by: \[ U = qV \]

Example 1

A proton moves through a uniform electric field of \( E = 2000 \, \text{N/C} \). If the proton moves \( 0.02 \, \text{m} \) parallel to the field, what is the change in electric potential energy?

Potential difference: \[ \Delta V = -E \Delta x = -(2000)(0.02) = -40 \, \text{V} \]

Change in potential energy: \[ \Delta U = q \Delta V = (1.6 \times 10^{-19})(-40) = -6.4 \times 10^{-18} \, J \]

The proton’s potential energy decreases, meaning its kinetic energy increases by the same amount.

Practice Problems

  1. Derive the relationship between electric field and potential difference in a uniform field.
  2. An electron moves opposite to a uniform electric field over \( 0.01 \, \text{m} \) when \( E = 1000 \, \text{N/C} \). Find the change in potential energy.
  3. What happens to a charged particle’s kinetic energy when it moves in the direction of the electric field?
  4. A proton experiences a potential drop of 25 V in a uniform field. Calculate the change in potential energy.
  5. Explain how energy conservation applies in this situation.
