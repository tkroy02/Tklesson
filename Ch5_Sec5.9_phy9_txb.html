Section 5.9: Review Questions

Answer the following questions to reinforce your understanding of Work, Energy, and Power:

  1. Define work in physics and give the formula.
  2. Explain the difference between positive, negative, and zero work, with examples.
  3. A 5 kg object is pushed horizontally 4 m by a 10 N force. Calculate the work done.
  4. A person lifts a 12 kg box 3 m vertically. Calculate the work done against gravity.
  5. State the law of conservation of energy with an example.
  6. Define kinetic energy and give the formula.
  7. Define potential energy and give the formula.
  8. A 2 kg ball is dropped from 5 m height. Calculate its potential energy at the top and kinetic energy just before hitting the ground.
  9. Describe how energy is transformed in a rollercoaster from top of the hill to bottom.
  10. Explain why friction does negative work on moving objects.
  11. A 10 N force is applied perpendicular to the displacement. Determine the work done.
  12. A 50 W light bulb is turned on for 3 hours. How much energy does it consume in joules?
  13. Describe two ways machines make work easier.
  14. Explain the concept of mechanical advantage with an example.
  15. A car of mass 1200 kg accelerates from 0 to 20 m/s. Calculate the change in kinetic energy.
  16. Compare elastic potential energy in a stretched spring versus gravitational potential energy.
  17. A rollercoaster car of mass 500 kg is at 15 m height. Find its potential energy relative to the ground.
  18. Explain why power is important in evaluating how quickly work is done.
  19. Give an example of zero work in real life.
  20. Summarize the relationship between work, energy, and power in your own words.
Key Tip: Remember to consider direction of force relative to displacement to determine positive, negative, or zero work. Keep units consistent: Joules for energy/work, Watts for power.
