Section 8.2: Pressure in Fluids at Rest

When a fluid is at rest, the pressure at any point is the same in all directions (Pascal’s principle). Pressure increases with depth according to:

\[ P = P_0 + \rho g h \] where \(P_0\) is surface pressure, \(\rho\) is fluid density, \(g\) is acceleration due to gravity, and \(h\) is depth.

Units: Pressure in Pascals (Pa), density in kg/m³, depth in meters.

Applications

  • Hydraulic systems.
  • Calculating pressure at the bottom of lakes or containers.
  • Understanding forces on submerged surfaces.

Example 1

Find the pressure 10 m below the surface of water (density 1000 kg/m³, \(g=9.8\) m/s²) assuming atmospheric pressure \(P_0=1.01\times10^5\) Pa.

\( P = P_0 + \rho g h = 1.01\times10^5 + 1000*9.8*10 \)

\( P = 1.01\times10^5 + 9.8\times10^4 = 1.99\times10^5 \, \text{Pa} \)

Example 2

A fluid in a tank has density 850 kg/m³. Find the pressure at 5 m depth. Atmospheric pressure is 1.0×10⁵ Pa.

\( P = 1.0\times10^5 + 850*9.8*5 = 1.0\times10^5 + 41650 = 1.04165\times10^5 \, \text{Pa} \)
