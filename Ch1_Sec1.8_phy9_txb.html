Section 1.8: Problem Solving in Physics

Physics problem solving involves a systematic approach to find unknown quantities using known formulas, principles, and logical reasoning.

Steps for Solving Problems

  1. Understand the Problem: Read carefully and identify what is given and what is to be found.
  2. Identify Variables: Determine independent, dependent, and constant variables.
  3. Select Relevant Formulas: Choose formulas or principles that relate the variables.
  4. Substitute Known Values: Insert numbers, ensuring correct units.
  5. Calculate and Solve: Perform the math step-by-step.
  6. Check Your Answer: Verify units, reasonableness, and consistency with physical laws.

Worked Example 1: Speed

Problem: A car travels 150 km in 3 hours. Find its average speed.

Step 1: Identify the known variables:

  • Distance, d = 150 km
  • Time, t = 3 h

Step 2: Identify what to find: average speed (v).

Step 3: Use the formula: $$v = \frac{d}{t}$$

Step 4: Substitute values: $$v = \frac{150 \text{ km}}{3 \text{ h}}$$

Step 5: Solve: $$v = 50 \text{ km/h}$$

Worked Example 2: Force

Problem: A box has a mass of 10 kg and accelerates at 2 m/s². Find the force acting on it.

Step 1: Identify variables:

  • Mass, m = 10 kg
  • Acceleration, a = 2 m/s²

Step 2: Formula: $$F = ma$$

Step 3: Substitute values: $$F = 10 \times 2$$

Step 4: Solve: $$F = 20 \text{ N}$$

Tips for Effective Problem Solving

  • Always write down what is known and unknown before starting calculations.
  • Draw diagrams to visualize the problem.
  • Check your units at each step to avoid errors.
  • If stuck, think about the physical principles involved rather than just formulas.
  • Break complex problems into smaller, manageable steps.

Key Takeaways

  • Physics problem solving is methodical and relies on identifying variables, selecting formulas, and logical calculation.
  • Always check answers for reasonableness and unit consistency.
  • Diagrams and clear steps reduce errors and make problem solving easier.
  • Practice helps internalize the process, making complex problems manageable.
