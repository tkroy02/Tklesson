Section 7.7: Schrödinger Equation Intro

The Schrödinger equation is a fundamental equation in quantum mechanics that describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time. It allows the calculation of probabilities of finding particles in particular states.

Time-Independent Schrödinger Equation:

\( -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{d^2 \psi(x)}{dx^2} + V(x)\psi(x) = E\psi(x) \)

  • \( \psi(x) \): wavefunction of the particle
  • \( V(x) \): potential energy function
  • \( E \): energy eigenvalue
  • \( \hbar \): reduced Planck’s constant

Example 1

A particle in a 1D infinite potential well of width 2 nm. Write the general form of the wavefunction inside the well.

The wavefunction inside an infinite square well: \( \psi_n(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \sin\left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right) \), with \( n = 1,2,3,... \) and \( L = 2 \text{ nm} \).

Practice Problems

  1. State the physical meaning of the wavefunction \( \psi(x) \).
  2. For a particle in a box of length 1 nm, find the energy of the ground state.
  3. Sketch the first three wavefunctions for a 1D infinite well.
  4. Explain why the wavefunction must be zero at infinite potential boundaries.
  5. Given a potential step, describe qualitatively the behavior of a particle encountering it.
  6. Show how normalization of \( \psi(x) \) is done.
  7. Discuss the significance of energy quantization in bound systems.
  8. Calculate the energy difference between the first and second levels for a 2 nm well.
  9. Explain how Schrödinger equation reduces to classical equations for large quantum numbers.
  10. Discuss the relevance of the Schrödinger equation to atomic spectra.
