Section 10.9: Review Questions

Conceptual Questions (5)

  1. Define a series circuit and explain its main characteristics.
  2. Define a parallel circuit and explain its main characteristics.
  3. Explain why the total resistance in a parallel circuit is always less than the smallest branch resistance.
  4. Compare the effects of a component failure in series and parallel circuits.
  5. What is a series-parallel circuit? Give a practical example.

Calculation Questions (15)

  1. Two resistors, 8 Ω and 12 Ω, are connected in series across a 20 V battery. Find the total resistance, current, and voltage drop across each resistor.
  2. Three resistors of 5 Ω, 10 Ω, and 15 Ω are connected in series with a 30 V supply. Determine total resistance, current, and voltage drops.
  3. Two resistors, 6 Ω and 12 Ω, are connected in parallel across 12 V. Find total resistance, branch currents, and total current.
  4. Three resistors in parallel: 4 Ω, 8 Ω, 12 Ω with 24 V supply. Find total resistance and current in each branch.
  5. A 24 V battery supplies a parallel circuit of R1 = 10 Ω, R2 = 15 Ω. Find total current and current through each resistor.
  6. Three lamps of resistances 6 Ω, 12 Ω, and 18 Ω are in series across 36 V. Find current and voltage drops.
  7. Two resistors in series (R1 = 8 Ω, R2 = 12 Ω) are connected across 24 V. Find total resistance, current, and voltage drops.
  8. Two resistors in parallel, R1 = 10 Ω and unknown R2, total voltage 20 V, total current 3 A. Find R2 and branch currents.
  9. Three resistors, R1 = 5 Ω, R2 = 10 Ω, R3 = 20 Ω, connected in series-parallel: R1 and R2 in series, then in parallel with R3 across 30 V. Find total resistance and total current.
  10. Series-parallel: R1 = 6 Ω in series with unknown R2, in parallel with R3 = 12 Ω, across 24 V. Total current = 4 A. Find R2 and branch currents.
  11. Four resistors in series: 5 Ω, 10 Ω, 15 Ω, 20 Ω across 40 V. Find current and voltage drops.
  12. Three resistors in parallel: 8 Ω, 12 Ω, 24 Ω, 48 V supply. Find total resistance, current in each branch, and total current.
  13. Two lamps R1 = 6 Ω, R2 = 9 Ω in parallel across 18 V. Find branch currents and total current.
  14. Series-parallel: R1 = 10 Ω in series with R2 = 20 Ω, this series in parallel with R3 = 15 Ω, across 30 V. Find total resistance, total current, and voltage across each resistor.
  15. Series-parallel: R1 = 5 Ω in series with unknown R2, in parallel with R3 = 10 Ω, across 20 V. Total current = 3 A. Find R2 and voltage drops.
