Section 7.7: Vector Calculus Applications

Vector calculus allows us to express magnetic field laws concisely: divergence, curl, and line integrals are key in describing field behavior.

Key Relations:

\[ \nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0, \quad \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 \vec{J}, \quad \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{\ell} = \mu_0 I_{enc} \] These are useful for symmetry analysis and Ampère’s law applications.

Example 1

Use Ampère’s law to find the magnetic field inside a long solenoid.

For a solenoid of n turns/m carrying current I:

\[ B = \mu_0 n I \]

The field outside is approximately zero due to cancellation.

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate \(\nabla \cdot \vec{B}\) for a uniform magnetic field.
  2. Determine \(\nabla \times \vec{B}\) for an infinite straight wire.
  3. Apply line integral of \(\vec{B}\) around a circular loop enclosing a current.
  4. Verify Ampère’s law for a toroidal coil.
  5. Discuss physical meaning of \(\nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0\).
