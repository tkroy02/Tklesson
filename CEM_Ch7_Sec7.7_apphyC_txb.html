Section 7.7: Vector Calculus Applications

Vector calculus is essential for analyzing magnetic fields, calculating forces, and applying Maxwell's equations. This section introduces line integrals, surface integrals, and curl in the context of magnetic fields.

Magnetic Force on Moving Charge:

\[ \vec{F} = q (\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \]

Useful for computing force vectors along arbitrary paths.

Line Integral of Magnetic Field:

\[ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} \]

Used in Ampère's law to relate current enclosed by a path to the magnetic field along that path.

Curl of Magnetic Field:

\[ \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 \vec{J} \]

Indicates how currents produce rotation in the magnetic field. Basis for Maxwell's equations.

Example 1

A long straight wire carries current \(I = 5\,\text{A}\). Using Ampère's law, find the magnetic field at a distance \(r = 0.1\,\text{m}\) from the wire.

Ampère's law: \[ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_\text{enc} \]

For a circular path of radius \(r\): \[ B (2 \pi r) = \mu_0 I \implies B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r} \]

Substitute values: \[ B = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5)}{2 \pi (0.1)} = 1 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{T} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Compute the line integral of \(\vec{B}\) around a square loop enclosing a current-carrying wire.
  2. Calculate the curl of a magnetic field \(\vec{B} = B_0 \hat{k}\) in Cartesian coordinates.
  3. Verify Ampère's law for a coaxial cable carrying uniform current density.
  4. Show that the magnetic field outside a long straight wire is divergence-free (\(\nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0\)).
  5. Explain how vector calculus is used to compute forces on a current loop in a non-uniform magnetic field.
