Section 9.4: Length Contraction

Length contraction is the phenomenon in special relativity where the length of an object moving relative to an observer is measured to be shorter along the direction of motion.

Length Contraction Formula:

\( L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2} \)

  • \(L\) = contracted length observed
  • \(L_0\) = proper length (length in the rest frame)
  • \(v\) = relative velocity
  • \(c\) = speed of light

Example 1

A spaceship has a proper length of 100 m and moves at 0.8c. Find its length observed from Earth.

\( L = 100 \sqrt{1 - 0.8^2} = 100 \sqrt{0.36} = 100 * 0.6 = 60 \text{ m} \)

Practice Problems

  1. A rod of proper length 2 m moves at 0.9c. Determine its length as seen by a stationary observer.
  2. A spacecraft 120 m long moves at 0.95c. Find its contracted length.
  3. A train moves at 0.7c and has proper length 300 m. Calculate its observed length on the ground.
  4. Explain why length contraction only occurs along the direction of motion.
  5. A particle accelerator contains a beam tube 1 km long at rest. What is its observed length for particles moving at 0.99c?
