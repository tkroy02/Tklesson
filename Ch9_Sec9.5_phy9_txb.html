Section 9.5: Plane Mirrors

Plane mirrors are flat reflective surfaces that produce images of objects placed in front of them. The image formed is virtual, upright, and of the same size as the object.

Properties of Images in Plane Mirrors

  • The image is virtual (cannot be projected on a screen).
  • The image is upright (same orientation as the object).
  • The image is same size as the object.
  • The image is laterally inverted (left-right reversed).
  • Distance from the mirror to the image = distance from mirror to object.

Example 1: Image Location

Problem: An object is placed 15 cm in front of a plane mirror. Find the distance of the image from the mirror.

Given:

  • Distance of object from mirror, \(u = 15 \, \mathrm{cm}\)

Solution:

For plane mirrors, the distance of the image from the mirror equals the distance of the object from the mirror:

\[ v = u = 15 \, \mathrm{cm} \]

Answer: The image is 15 cm behind the mirror.

Example 2: Lateral Inversion

Problem: A person looks into a plane mirror and raises their right hand. Which hand appears to move in the image?

Solution:

  • The image in a plane mirror is laterally inverted.
  • The person's right hand appears as the left hand in the image.

Answer: The image shows the left hand moving.

Key Points

  • Plane mirrors form virtual, upright, and same-size images.
  • Images are laterally inverted.
  • Distance from mirror to image = distance from mirror to object.
  • Used in daily life: household mirrors, dressing mirrors, vehicle rear-view mirrors.
