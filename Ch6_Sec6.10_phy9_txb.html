Section 6.10: Chapter 6 Test - Calculation-Based

Solve the following 20 calculation-based problems. Use \(g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\) where needed, and show all steps.

  1. A 2 kg mass moves in a horizontal circle of radius 1.5 m at speed 3 m/s. Find the centripetal force.
  2. A car of mass 800 kg goes over a hill of radius 20 m at 12 m/s. Find the normal force at the top.
  3. A rollercoaster of mass 500 kg moves through a dip of radius 8 m at 10 m/s. Calculate the normal force at the bottom.
  4. A pendulum of mass 1 kg swings in a vertical circle of radius 2 m with speed 4 m/s at the bottom. Find the tension.
  5. Using the same pendulum, find the tension at the top of the swing.
  6. A cyclist of mass 80 kg moves through a shallow dip of radius 12 m at 6 m/s. Find the normal force at the bottom.
  7. Find the minimum speed at the top of a vertical loop of radius 10 m for a 500 kg rollercoaster.
  8. A 1.2 kg ball rotates in a horizontal circle of radius 0.8 m at 5 m/s. Determine the period and frequency.
  9. An object completes 10 revolutions in 20 s. Find angular velocity and angular acceleration if it starts from rest and accelerates uniformly.
  10. A car turns a flat curve of radius 25 m at 15 m/s. Determine the minimum coefficient of friction required.
  11. A satellite orbits Earth at 7000 km radius and 7.5 km/s speed. Compute the centripetal acceleration and gravitational force.
  12. A 0.5 kg mass rotates in a vertical circle of radius 1 m at 4 m/s. Find tension at top and bottom.
  13. A 60 kg person rides a carousel of radius 3 m at 2 m/s. Compute the centrifugal force they feel.
  14. A mass 2 kg is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1 m at 6 m/s. Find tension.
  15. A car of mass 1000 kg goes over a crest of radius 18 m at 13 m/s. Determine if the car remains in contact and find the normal force.
  16. Derive the speed at the top of a vertical loop if normal force is zero. Radius 5 m, g = 9.8.
  17. A 0.8 kg ball swings in a vertical circle of radius 1 m at 3 m/s. Find tension at top and bottom.
  18. A 1.5 kg ball swings horizontally at 2 m radius, speed 5 m/s. Compute period, frequency, centripetal force.
  19. A car of mass 1200 kg goes around a curve of radius 30 m at 12 m/s. Find friction force providing centripetal force.
  20. Summarize: A rollercoaster of mass 600 kg moves in a vertical loop of radius 6 m at 9 m/s. Calculate centripetal force, normal force at top and bottom, and apparent weight at bottom.
