Section 7.8: Gyroscopes and Precession

Gyroscopes demonstrate conservation of angular momentum and experience precession under torque.

Gyroscope Basics:
  • Rapidly spinning wheel mounted on gimbals
  • Maintains orientation due to angular momentum conservation
Precession:
  • Change in orientation of rotational axis due to applied torque
  • Precession angular velocity: \(\Omega = \frac{\tau}{L}\)
  • \(\tau\) = torque applied, \(L\) = angular momentum

Example 1

A gyroscope with angular momentum \(L = 50 \text{ kg·m²/s}\) experiences a torque of \(10 \text{ N·m}\). Find precession rate.

\(\Omega = \frac{\tau}{L} = \frac{10}{50} = 0.2\ \text{rad/s}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A spinning wheel has I = 2 kg·m², ω = 10 rad/s, and experiences a torque of 5 N·m. Find precession rate.
  2. A gyroscope is used in navigation. Explain why it resists changes in orientation.
  3. Calculate the torque needed to cause a precession rate of 0.5 rad/s for a gyroscope with L = 25 kg·m²/s.
