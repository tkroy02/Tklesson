Section 7.3: Torque and Rotational Equilibrium

Torque is the rotational equivalent of force and determines how a force causes an object to rotate around an axis.

Torque (\(\tau\)):
  • \(\tau = r F \sin \theta\)
  • r = distance from pivot to point of force application
  • F = magnitude of force
  • \(\theta\) = angle between force and lever arm
Rotational Equilibrium:
  • Object is in equilibrium if \(\sum \tau = 0\) (net torque = 0)
  • Also requires \(\sum F = 0\) (translational equilibrium)

Example 1

A 2 m long beam is pivoted at one end. A force of 50 N is applied perpendicular at the other end. Find the torque about the pivot.

\(\tau = r F \sin \theta = 2 \times 50 \times \sin 90^\circ = 100 \text{ N·m}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A 1.5 m wrench has a force of 80 N applied at 30° to the handle. Find the torque.
  2. Two forces act on a seesaw: 100 N at 2 m from pivot, 120 N at 1.5 m opposite side. Determine if seesaw is balanced.
  3. A uniform rod of length 3 m is pivoted at center. Two weights of 20 N and 30 N hang at ends. Find net torque and check equilibrium.
