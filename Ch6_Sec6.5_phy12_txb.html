Section 6.5: Resonance in AC Circuits

Resonance in AC circuits occurs when the inductive reactance (\(X_L\)) equals the capacitive reactance (\(X_C\)). At this point, the impedance is minimum, and the current is maximum.

\( X_L = X_C \quad \implies \quad \omega L = \dfrac{1}{\omega C} \)

Resonant frequency: \(\; f_r = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} \)

  • At resonance, current and voltage are in phase (\(\phi = 0\)).
  • Impedance is minimum and equal to resistance \(R\).
  • Power factor is unity (\(\cos\phi = 1\)).

Example

A series RLC circuit has \(L = 0.1 \,\text{H}\) and \(C = 40 \,\mu\text{F}\). Find the resonance frequency.

Solution:

\[ f_r = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{0.1 \times 40 \times 10^{-6}}} \]

\(\sqrt{0.1 \times 40 \times 10^{-6}} = \sqrt{4.0 \times 10^{-6}} = 0.002\).

\( f_r = \dfrac{1}{2\pi \times 0.002} \approx 79.6 \,\text{Hz} \).

Thus, the circuit resonates at about 80 Hz.
