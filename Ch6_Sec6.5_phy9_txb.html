Section 6.5: Centripetal Force and Tension

In circular motion where a string or rod provides the centripetal force, tension plays a critical role. The tension force adjusts depending on the speed, mass, and radius of the circular path.

Key Concept: The tension in the string is the net centripetal force that keeps the object moving in a circle.

Calculating Tension

For an object of mass m moving in a circle of radius r with speed v, the tension T in the string is:

\( T = m \frac{v^2}{r} \)

Note: If other forces act (gravity, vertical motion), tension will be adjusted accordingly.

Example 1: Horizontal Circular Motion

A 2 kg ball is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1.5 m at speed 3 m/s. Find the tension in the string.

Step 1: Identify the formula: \( T = m \frac{v^2}{r} \)

Step 2: Substitute values: \( T = 2 \times \frac{3^2}{1.5} \)

Step 3: Calculate numerator: \( 2 \times 9 = 18 \)

Step 4: Divide by radius: \( T = 18 / 1.5 = 12 \, \text{N} \)

Example 2: Vertical Circular Motion at the Bottom

A 0.5 kg mass attached to a string of 1 m is swung in a vertical circle at the bottom with speed 4 m/s. Find the tension at the bottom.

Step 1: Formula at bottom: \( T = mg + \frac{m v^2}{r} \)

Step 2: Compute weight: \( mg = 0.5 \times 9.8 = 4.9 \, \text{N} \)

Step 3: Compute centripetal term: \( \frac{0.5 \times 16}{1} = 8 \, \text{N} \)

Step 4: Total tension: \( T = 4.9 + 8 = 12.9 \, \text{N} \)

Example 3: Vertical Circular Motion at the Top

Using the same mass and radius as Example 2, speed at the top is 4 m/s. Find tension at the top.

Step 1: Formula at top: \( T = \frac{m v^2}{r} - mg \)

Step 2: Compute centripetal term: \( 0.5 \times 16 / 1 = 8 \, \text{N} \)

Step 3: Subtract weight: \( T = 8 - 4.9 = 3.1 \, \text{N} \)

Example 4: Horizontal Swing with Different Radius

A 1.5 kg ball swings in a horizontal circle of radius 2 m at speed 5 m/s. Find the tension.

Step 1: Formula: \( T = m v^2 / r \)

Step 2: Compute numerator: \( 1.5 \times 25 = 37.5 \)

Step 3: Divide by radius: \( T = 37.5 / 2 = 18.75 \, \text{N} \)
