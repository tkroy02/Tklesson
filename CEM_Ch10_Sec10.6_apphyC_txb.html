Section 10.6: Electromagnetic Wave Derivation

This section derives the wave equations for electric and magnetic fields in free space from Maxwell’s equations.

Wave Equation for Electric Field:

From Maxwell’s equations in free space (\( \rho=0, \mathbf{J}=0 \)):

\( \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \), \( \nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t} \)

Taking the curl of Faraday’s Law:

\( \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf{E}) = -\frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla \times \mathbf{B}) = -\mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2} \)

Using vector identity: \( \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf{E}) = \nabla(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E}) - \nabla^2 \mathbf{E} = -\nabla^2 \mathbf{E} \)

Wave equation: \( \nabla^2 \mathbf{E} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2} \)

Wave Equation for Magnetic Field:

Similarly, for the magnetic field:

\( \nabla^2 \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{B}}{\partial t^2} \)

Both fields propagate as waves at speed \( c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}} \)

Relationship Between E and B:
  • \( \mathbf{E} \perp \mathbf{B} \)
  • Both perpendicular to the direction of propagation
  • Magnitude relation: \( B = \frac{E}{c} \)

Example 1

Given a plane electromagnetic wave with electric field \( E = E_0 \hat{x} \cos(kz - \omega t) \), find the corresponding magnetic field.

Use Faraday’s Law: \( \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \)

Calculation yields: \( \mathbf{B} = \frac{E_0}{c} \hat{y} \cos(kz - \omega t) \)

Direction perpendicular to \( \mathbf{E} \) and propagation (\( \hat{z} \)) axis.

Practice Problems

  1. Derive the wave equation for E in a source-free region starting from Maxwell’s equations.
  2. Show that B satisfies the same wave equation in free space.
  3. Determine the speed of an electromagnetic wave in vacuum.
  4. Given an E field, find the corresponding B field and direction of propagation.
  5. Show that the energy density of the wave is equally shared between electric and magnetic fields.
