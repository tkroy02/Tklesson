Section 10.6: Electromagnetic Wave Derivation

This section derives the electromagnetic wave equations from Maxwell's equations in free space.

From Maxwell's equations in free space:
\[ \nabla \cdot \vec{E} = 0, \quad \nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0, \quad \nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}, \quad \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \vec{E}}{\partial t} \]

Derivation Example

Derive the wave equation for \(\vec{E}\) in free space.

Take the curl of Faraday's law:

\[ \nabla \times (\nabla \times \vec{E}) = - \frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla \times \vec{B}) = - \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \vec{E}}{\partial t^2} \]

Apply the vector identity \(\nabla \times (\nabla \times \vec{E}) = \nabla(\nabla \cdot \vec{E}) - \nabla^2 \vec{E}\) and \(\nabla \cdot \vec{E} = 0\):

\[ \nabla^2 \vec{E} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \vec{E}}{\partial t^2} \]

This is the electromagnetic wave equation with speed \( c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}} \).

Practice Problems

  1. Show that the magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) satisfies a similar wave equation.
  2. Explain why \(\vec{E}\) and \(\vec{B}\) are perpendicular in an electromagnetic wave.
  3. Calculate the wavelength of a wave in free space if its frequency is \(6 \times 10^{14}\) Hz.
