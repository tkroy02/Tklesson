Section 8.6: Applications of Nuclear Physics

Nuclear physics has a wide range of practical applications, from energy production to medical imaging and treatment. Understanding these applications helps connect theoretical concepts to real-world uses.

Key Applications:
  • Nuclear Power: Energy generation via fission in reactors.
  • Medical Imaging: PET scans and radiography using radioisotopes.
  • Cancer Therapy: Radiation therapy targeting tumors.
  • Industrial Applications: Radiography, material testing, and tracers.
  • Astrophysics: Stellar nucleosynthesis and energy production in stars.

Example 1

Explain how Uranium-235 fission can produce electricity in a nuclear power plant.

Uranium-235 absorbs a neutron → nucleus becomes unstable → splits into smaller nuclei + 2-3 neutrons + energy (~200 MeV per fission). The released energy heats water to produce steam → drives turbines → generates electricity.
