Section 9.2: Special Relativity Basics

Special Relativity, introduced by Einstein, redefines the concepts of space and time for objects moving at significant fractions of the speed of light. Classical mechanics no longer applies at these high velocities.

Key Concepts:
  • Speed of light \(c = 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}\) is constant in all inertial frames.
  • Time and space are relative: moving clocks run slower, moving rulers contract.
  • Relativity applies to inertial frames moving at constant velocity.

Example 1

A spaceship moves at 0.8c relative to Earth. How much time passes on the ship if 5 years pass on Earth?

Time dilation: \( t' = t \sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2} = 5 \sqrt{1 - 0.64} = 5 \sqrt{0.36} = 5 * 0.6 = 3 \text{ years} \)

Practice Problems

  1. A clock on a satellite moving at 0.6c runs for 2 years. Find time elapsed on Earth.
  2. Length contraction: A spaceship of 100 m moves at 0.9c. Find its length as measured from Earth.
  3. Two spacecrafts move toward each other at 0.5c each relative to Earth. Find their relative speed.
  4. A particle moves at 0.99c. Calculate its relativistic factor \(\gamma\).
  5. Explain why Newtonian addition of velocities fails at relativistic speeds.
