Section 1.1: Position, Velocity & Acceleration (1D)

In one-dimensional motion, objects move along a straight line. We describe motion using position, velocity, and acceleration.

Key Concepts:
  • Position (x): Location along a line (coordinate).
  • Displacement (Δx): Change in position, Δx = x₂ − x₁.
  • Velocity (v): Rate of change of position: \( v = \dfrac{dx}{dt} \).
  • Acceleration (a): Rate of change of velocity: \( a = \dfrac{dv}{dt} \).

Equations of Motion (Constant Acceleration)

When acceleration is constant, the following equations apply:

  • \( v = v_0 + a t \)
  • \( x = x_0 + v_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2} a t^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2 a (x - x_0) \)

Example 1

A car starts from rest and accelerates at \(2\ \mathrm{m/s^2}\) for \(5\ \mathrm{s}\). Find its final velocity and displacement.

Final velocity: \( v = v_0 + a t = 0 + 2(5) = 10\ \mathrm{m/s} \).

Displacement: \( x = x_0 + v_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2} a t^2 = 0 + 0 + \tfrac{1}{2}(2)(5^2) = 25\ \mathrm{m} \).

Example 2

An object moves with velocity \(v(t)=4t\) (m/s). Find acceleration and displacement from \(t=0\) to \(t=3\) s.

Acceleration: \( a(t)=\dfrac{dv}{dt}=4\ \mathrm{m/s^2} \) (constant).

Displacement: \( x(3)-x(0) = \int_0^3 v(t)\,dt = \int_0^3 4t\,dt = 2t^2\Big|_0^3 = 18\ \mathrm{m}. \)

Practice Problems

  1. A bike accelerates from rest at \(1.5\ \mathrm{m/s^2}\) for \(8\) s. Find final velocity and displacement.
  2. An object with \(v_0=20\ \mathrm{m/s}\) decelerates at \(2\ \mathrm{m/s^2}\). How long to stop and how far does it travel?
  3. A runner covers \(100\) m in \(10\) s starting from rest. Find the constant acceleration (assume constant).
  4. Given \(x(t)=5+3t+t^2\), find \(v(t)\) and \(a(t)\) at \(t=2\) s.
