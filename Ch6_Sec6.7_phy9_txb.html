Section 6.7: Orbital Motion

Orbital motion refers to the motion of objects around a central body due to the gravitational attraction between them. Examples include planets orbiting the Sun, moons orbiting planets, and satellites orbiting Earth.

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation: Sir Isaac Newton postulated that every particle in the universe attracts every other particle with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them: \[ F_\text{gravity} = G \frac{M m}{r^2} \]
  • \(F_\text{gravity}\) = gravitational force between the two objects
  • \(M\) = mass of the central body (e.g., Earth, Sun)
  • \(m\) = mass of the orbiting object (e.g., satellite, planet)
  • \(r\) = distance between the centers of the two masses
  • \(G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m²/kg²}\) is the universal gravitational constant
In orbital motion, this gravitational force provides the necessary centripetal force to keep the object moving in a circular path: \[ F_\text{gravity} = \frac{m v^2}{r} \]

Orbital Speed

The speed required for a circular orbit is obtained by equating gravitational force to centripetal force:

  • Equation: \( \frac{G M m}{r^2} = \frac{m v^2}{r} \)
  • Simplify: \( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} \)
The orbital speed depends only on the mass of the central body and the orbital radius, not on the mass of the orbiting object.

Orbital Period

The time for one complete orbit (period) is:

  • Definition: \( T = \frac{\text{circumference}}{\text{speed}} = \frac{2 \pi r}{v} \)
  • Substitute orbital speed: \( T = 2 \pi r \sqrt{\frac{r}{G M}} = 2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{r^3}{G M}} \)
This is a simplified form of Kepler's Third Law for circular orbits.

Example: Satellite Orbit

A satellite orbits Earth at a radius of 7000 km. Find its orbital speed.

Step 1: Identify formula: \( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} \)

Step 2: List known values:

  • Gravitational constant: \( G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m²/kg²} \)
  • Mass of Earth: \( M = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \)
  • Orbital radius: \( r = 7000 \, \text{km} = 7.0 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)

Step 3: Compute orbital speed: \[ v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 5.97 \times 10^{24}}{7.0 \times 10^6}} \approx 7.55 \, \text{km/s} \]
