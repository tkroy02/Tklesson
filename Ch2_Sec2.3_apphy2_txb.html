Section 2.3: Calorimetry

Calorimetry is the study of measuring heat transfer during physical or chemical processes. A calorimeter is an insulated device designed to minimize energy exchange with the surroundings, so that the heat lost by one body equals the heat gained by another.

Key Concepts

  • Heat transfer equation: \( Q = mc\Delta T \)
  • Conservation of energy in calorimetry: \[ Q_\text{lost} + Q_\text{gained} = 0 \]
  • In a typical calorimetry problem, hot and cold objects exchange heat until reaching thermal equilibrium.

Worked Example

A \(200 \, \text{g}\) piece of copper at \(150^\circ\text{C}\) is placed into \(100 \, \text{g}\) of water at \(20^\circ\text{C}\). What is the final equilibrium temperature, assuming no heat loss to the environment? (\(c_\text{Cu} = 390 \, \text{J/kg·°C}\), \(c_\text{water} = 4186 \, \text{J/kg·°C}\))

Heat lost by copper = Heat gained by water.

\[ (0.200)(390)(T_f - 150) + (0.100)(4186)(T_f - 20) = 0 \]

Expanding: \(-78(T_f - 150) + 419(T_f - 20) = 0\)

Simplify: \(-78T_f + 11700 + 419T_f - 8380 = 0\)

\(341T_f + 3320 = 0 \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; T_f \approx 26.7^\circ\text{C}\)

Final Temperature: about \(27^\circ\text{C}\).

Practice Problem

A \(500 \, \text{g}\) block of aluminum at \(100^\circ\text{C}\) is placed into \(300 \, \text{g}\) of water at \(25^\circ\text{C}\). Find the equilibrium temperature. (\(c_\text{Al} = 900 \, \text{J/kg·°C}\))
