4.9 Review Questions

Test your understanding of the concepts from this chapter. These questions include definitions, conceptual explanations, and calculations related to forces, Newton's laws, friction, and air resistance.

Conceptual Questions

  1. Define force. Give two everyday examples of forces acting on objects.
  2. State Newton's three laws of motion and provide one real-life example for each.
  3. Explain the difference between mass and weight. Which one changes depending on location, and why?
  4. What is inertia? How does it relate to Newton's first law of motion?
  5. Why do passengers jerk forward when a moving bus suddenly stops?
  6. Describe the action-reaction force pair when you jump off a small boat into the water.
  7. Distinguish between static and kinetic friction with an example.
  8. Explain why parachutes are effective at slowing a skydiver's descent.

Calculation Questions

  1. A 10 kg object is acted on by a net force of 50 N. Calculate its acceleration.
    \( F = m \cdot a \)
  2. Calculate the weight of a 70 kg person on Earth (\(g = 9.8 \, m/s^2\)).
  3. A 5 kg block is pushed with a force of 30 N across a horizontal surface. If the frictional force opposing motion is 10 N, find:
    • The net force
    • The resulting acceleration
  4. A car moving at 15 m/s comes to a stop in 5 seconds. Calculate its deceleration.
  5. A 25 kg box is resting on a flat surface. If the coefficient of friction (\(\mu\)) is 0.3, calculate the frictional force acting on the box.
    \( F_{friction} = \mu \cdot F_{normal} \)
  6. A person pushes a 50 kg cart with a force of 200 N. If there is 50 N of resistance due to friction, determine the acceleration of the cart.
  7. A 2 kg object is dropped from rest. Neglecting air resistance, calculate its velocity after 4 seconds.

Challenge Questions

  1. A rocket expels gas downward with a force of 5000 N. What is the upward acceleration of the rocket if its total mass is 1000 kg?
  2. Explain why astronauts appear weightless while orbiting the Earth, even though gravity is still acting on them.
  3. A block is on an inclined plane. Explain how you would resolve the forces acting on the block to determine whether it will slide down.
  4. A motorcyclist riding at high speed spots a sinkhole 5 seconds before reaching it but still rides into it. Could this accident have been avoided the moment the motorcyclist first spotted the hole? Explain using the concepts of reaction time, braking force, and Newton's laws.
  5. Describe the combined effects of friction and air resistance on a cyclist moving at high speed.
