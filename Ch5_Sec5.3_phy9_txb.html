5.3 Potential Energy

While kinetic energy is the energy of motion, potential energy is stored energy that an object possesses due to its position or condition. When this stored energy is released, it can be converted into kinetic energy or other forms of energy.

Key Idea: Potential energy is energy waiting to be used â€” it depends on position or configuration.

Types of Potential Energy

  • Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE): Energy an object has because of its height above the ground.
  • Elastic Potential Energy: Energy stored in stretched or compressed elastic materials like springs or rubber bands.
  • Chemical Potential Energy: Energy stored in chemical bonds, like in batteries or food.

In this section, we will focus on gravitational potential energy.

Gravitational Potential Energy

The formula for gravitational potential energy is:

\[ PE = m g h \]

  • PE = gravitational potential energy (Joules, J)
  • m = mass (kilograms, kg)
  • g = acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s2)
  • h = height above a reference point (meters, m)

The higher an object is lifted, the more gravitational potential energy it gains.

Worked Example

A 5.0 kg backpack is lifted to a shelf 2.0 m above the ground. Calculate its gravitational potential energy relative to the ground.

Solution:
\[ PE = m g h = (5.0)(9.8)(2.0) = 98.0\,\mathrm{J} \]
Answer: 98.0 J

Relationship Between Kinetic and Potential Energy

As an object moves, its energy can transform between kinetic and potential forms.

Example: When a ball is thrown upward, its speed decreases as it rises, converting kinetic energy into potential energy. When it falls back down, potential energy is converted back into kinetic energy.

In the absence of friction, the total mechanical energy (KE + PE) remains constant.

Practice Problems

Calculate the gravitational potential energy in the following situations:

  1. A 2.0 kg rock is held 3.0 m above the ground.
  2. A diver with a mass of 60 kg climbs to a platform 10 m high.
  3. A 0.50 kg book sits 1.5 m above the floor.

Answers

  1. \( PE = (2.0)(9.8)(3.0) = 58.8\,\mathrm{J} \)
  2. \( PE = (60)(9.8)(10) = 5880\,\mathrm{J} \)
  3. \( PE = (0.50)(9.8)(1.5) = 7.35\,\mathrm{J} \)

Summary

  • Potential energy is stored energy due to position or condition.
  • Gravitational potential energy depends on mass, gravity, and height: \( PE = mgh \).
  • Energy can transform between kinetic and potential, but total mechanical energy remains constant (if no friction).
  • The height of an object above a reference point is the only factor that determines its gravitational potential energy, provided that its mass and the acceleration due to gravity remain constant.
