Section 6.6: Problem-Solving Strategies

This section provides a systematic approach to solving oscillation and resonance problems in physics.

General Steps:
  1. Identify the type of oscillation (SHM, damped, driven).
  2. Write down the governing equation of motion.
  3. Determine relevant parameters: mass, spring constant, damping, driving force.
  4. Apply formulas for amplitude, frequency, energy as needed.
  5. Check units and reasonableness of solution.

Example 1

A 1 kg mass on a spring with k = 50 N/m undergoes SHM. Determine maximum velocity and displacement given amplitude A = 0.2 m.

\( \omega = \sqrt{k/m} = \sqrt{50/1} = 7.07 \text{ rad/s} \)

Maximum velocity: \( v_\text{max} = \omega A = 7.07*0.2 \approx 1.41 \text{ m/s} \)

Maximum displacement: \( x_\text{max} = A = 0.2 \text{ m} \)

Practice Problems

  1. A 0.5 kg spring-mass system with k = 20 N/m, amplitude 0.1 m. Find max velocity.
  2. Determine the period of a 2 kg mass on a spring k = 200 N/m.
  3. For a damped oscillator with b = 0.5 kg/s, m = 1 kg, k = 50 N/m, find the damped angular frequency.
  4. A driven oscillator has F0 = 5 N, m = 1 kg, k = 40 N/m, b = 0.2 kg/s, Ï‰ = 6 rad/s. Calculate amplitude.
  5. For a pendulum of length 1 m, find the period for small oscillations.
