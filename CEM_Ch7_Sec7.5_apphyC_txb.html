Section 7.5: Circular Motion of Charges

When a charged particle moves perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field, it undergoes uniform circular motion due to the Lorentz force acting as a centripetal force.

Key Relations:

\[ F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = qvB \quad \Rightarrow \quad r = \frac{mv}{qB}, \quad \omega = \frac{v}{r} = \frac{qB}{m} \] where \( r \) is radius, \( \omega \) is angular frequency.

Example 1

A proton moves at \( 10^6 \, \text{m/s} \) perpendicular to a 0.1 T field. Find its circular path radius.

\( r = \frac{mv}{qB} = \frac{1.67\times10^{-27} \cdot 10^6}{1.6\times10^{-19} \cdot 0.1} \approx 0.104 \, \text{m} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the period of circular motion for an electron in a 0.05 T field.
  2. Compare the circular motion radius of electron vs proton at same speed in same B.
  3. Explain why the motion is circular, not straight.
  4. Determine velocity if radius and B are given.
  5. Derive the centripetal acceleration from Lorentz force.
