Section 7.5: Circular Motion of Charges

When a charged particle moves perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field, it experiences a centripetal force and moves in a circular path. This is a direct application of the Lorentz force.

Magnetic Force as Centripetal Force:

\[ qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r} \]

where \(q\) is charge, \(v\) is velocity, \(B\) is magnetic field strength, \(m\) is mass, and \(r\) is radius of circular motion.

Radius of Circular Path:

\[ r = \frac{mv}{qB} \]

Period of Revolution:

\[ T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB} \]

Independent of speed \(v\).

Example 1

A proton (\(m = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\, \text{kg}\), \(q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\, \text{C}\)) enters a uniform magnetic field of strength \(B = 0.5\,\text{T}\) with velocity \(v = 2 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\) perpendicular to the field. Find the radius of its circular path.

\[ r = \frac{mv}{qB} = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(2 \times 10^6)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.5)} \approx 0.042\,\text{m} \]

The proton moves in a circle of radius approximately 4.2 cm.

Practice Problems

  1. An electron enters a uniform magnetic field of 0.2 T with speed \(1 \times 10^7\,\text{m/s}\) perpendicular to the field. Find its circular radius.
  2. A particle of mass \(2 \times 10^{-26}\, \text{kg}\) and charge \(3 \times 10^{-19}\, \text{C}\) moves at \(5 \times 10^5\,\text{m/s}\) in a 0.1 T field. Calculate the period of revolution.
  3. Explain why the period of revolution is independent of the speed of the particle.
  4. A proton moves at an angle of \(60^\circ\) to a uniform magnetic field. Describe its trajectory.
  5. Derive the general expression for radius \(r\) and period \(T\) for a particle moving at arbitrary velocity in a perpendicular magnetic field.
