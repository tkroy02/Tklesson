Section 5.9: Real-Life Applications

Systems of linear equations are useful in solving problems in everyday life, such as finance, mixtures, and motion problems.

Example 1: Ticket Sales

A theater sold 200 tickets. Adult tickets cost $12, student tickets $8. Total sales were $1,760. Find how many adult and student tickets were sold.

Step 1: Let x = adult tickets, y = student tickets.

Equation 1 (total tickets): \( x + y = 200 \)

Equation 2 (total sales): \( 12x + 8y = 1760 \)

Step 2: Solve equation 1 for y → \( y = 200 - x \)

Step 3: Substitute into equation 2: \( 12x + 8(200 - x) = 1760 \) → \( 12x + 1600 - 8x = 1760 \) → \( 4x = 160 \) → \( x = 40 \)

Step 4: \( y = 200 - 40 = 160 \)

Solution: 40 adult tickets, 160 student tickets.

Example 2: Mixture Problem

A grocer wants to mix nuts costing $5 per kg with nuts costing $8 per kg to make 20 kg of a mixture costing $6.80 per kg. How much of each type should be used?

Step 1: Let x = kg of $5 nuts, y = kg of $8 nuts.

Equation 1 (total weight): \( x + y = 20 \)

Equation 2 (total cost): \( 5x + 8y = 20 * 6.8 = 136 \)

Step 2: Solve equation 1 for y → \( y = 20 - x \)

Step 3: Substitute into equation 2: \( 5x + 8(20 - x) = 136 \) → \( 5x + 160 - 8x = 136 \) → \( -3x = -24 \) → \( x = 8 \)

Step 4: \( y = 20 - 8 = 12 \)

Solution: 8 kg of $5 nuts, 12 kg of $8 nuts.

Practice Problems

  1. A school sold 300 tickets: adult $10, child $6. Total sales $2,200. Find number of adult and child tickets.
  2. A mixture of 10 liters costs $12 per liter and $15 per liter. How much of each for 30 liters costing $13.50 per liter?
  3. A car rental company rents cars at $40/day and trucks at $60/day. Total rentals 50 vehicles, total revenue $2,400. Find numbers rented.
  4. Two numbers sum to 50. One is three times the other. Find the numbers.
  5. Tickets sold: concert has VIP $100, regular $50. 150 tickets sold, total $10,000. How many of each type?
