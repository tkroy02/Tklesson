Section 6.4: Discharging Capacitor

When a charged capacitor is disconnected from the voltage source and connected across a resistor, it discharges exponentially with time constant \( \tau = RC \).

Voltage Across Capacitor:

\[ V_C(t) = V_0 e^{-t/RC} \]

where \( V_0 \) is the initial capacitor voltage, \( R \) is resistance, and \( C \) is capacitance.

Current in Circuit:

\[ I(t) = -\frac{V_0}{R} e^{-t/RC} \]

Note: The negative sign indicates current direction opposite to charging.

Example 1

A 10 µF capacitor initially charged to 12 V is connected across a 2 kΩ resistor. Find the voltage after 5 ms.

Time constant: \( \tau = RC = 2000 \cdot 10 \times 10^{-6} = 0.02 \, s \)

Voltage: \( V_C(t) = 12 e^{-0.005/0.02} \approx 10.6 \, V \)

Practice Problems

  1. Derive the expression for voltage and current during capacitor discharge.
  2. Calculate the current through a 1 kΩ resistor when a 5 µF capacitor discharges from 10 V after 3 ms.
  3. Explain the effect of increasing resistance on the discharge rate.
  4. Sketch the voltage and current vs time graphs for the discharging process.
  5. Determine the time for the voltage to drop to 10% of initial value.
