Section 7.2: Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational potential energy (GPE) is the energy an object possesses due to its position in a gravitational field. It represents the work done by gravity in moving the object to a reference point.

Gravitational Potential Energy Formula:

\( U = - G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r} \)

  • \( U \): Gravitational potential energy
  • \( G \): Universal gravitational constant
  • \( m_1, m_2 \): Masses of the two objects
  • \( r \): Distance between the centers of the masses

Example 1

Find the gravitational potential energy of a 1000 kg satellite 7000 km from the center of Earth (mass = \(5.97 \times 10^{24}\) kg).

\( U = - G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r} = - 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \frac{(5.97 \times 10^{24})(1000)}{7 \times 10^6} \approx -5.70 \times 10^7 \, \text{J} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Compute GPE of a 50 kg object 2 m from a 50 kg mass.
  2. A 500 kg satellite orbits at 8000 km from Earth’s center. Find its GPE.
  3. Find the GPE of two planets with masses \(3 \times 10^{24}\) kg and \(5 \times 10^{24}\) kg separated by \(1 \times 10^7\) m.
  4. How does GPE change if the distance between two objects is halved?
  5. Calculate the GPE of a 70 kg person at Earth’s surface.
