Chemistry Grade 12: Unit 5 Quiz 1

5.1: Alcohols Classification — Types, Naming & Structural Recognition

🧪 Alcohols Classification Challenge

🔴 Primary (1°) –OH on carbon bonded to 1 other carbon
Example: ethanol
🟠 Secondary (2°) –OH on carbon bonded to 2 other carbons
Example: propan-2-ol
🔵 Tertiary (3°) –OH on carbon bonded to 3 other carbons
Example: 2-methylpropan-2-ol
General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₁OH
Suffix: -ol
–OH has priority in numbering
Diol = two –OH groups
Triol = three –OH groups
Glycerol = propane-1,2,3-triol
Allylic: –OH next to C=C
Benzylic: –OH on C next to benzene
Common: isopropyl = propan-2-ol
Glycol = ethane-1,2-diol
Phenol: –OH on benzene (not alcohol)

This 10-question MCQ quiz tests your ability to classify alcohols as primary, secondary, or tertiary, apply IUPAC naming rules, and recognize special alcohol types.

🔬 Skills Tested:

  • ✓ Classifying alcohols as 1°, 2°, or 3° based on structure
  • ✓ Writing the general molecular formula for monohydric alcohols
  • ✓ Applying IUPAC naming rules (suffix -ol, numbering priority)
  • ✓ Identifying diols, triols, and allylic alcohols
  • ✓ Recognizing common names: isopropyl alcohol, glycerol, glycol
  • ✓ Distinguishing the carbinol carbon and its substituents
