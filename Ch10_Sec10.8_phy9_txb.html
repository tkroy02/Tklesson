Section 10.8: Parallel Circuits

A parallel circuit is a circuit in which the components are connected across the same two points, so each component has its own path to the power supply. If one component fails, the others can continue to operate.

Key Features of Parallel Circuits

  • The voltage across each branch is the same as the source voltage.
  • The current divides among the branches according to their resistance.
  • The total resistance is always less than the smallest branch resistance: \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \frac{1}{R_3} + \dots \]
  • If one branch is removed or fails, current continues through other branches.

Example 1: Two Resistors in Parallel

Problem: Two resistors, \(R_1 = 6\,\Omega\) and \(R_2 = 12\,\Omega\), are connected in parallel across a 12 V battery. Find the total resistance and the total current drawn from the battery.

Solution:

  1. Total resistance: \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{12} = \frac{2+1}{12} = \frac{3}{12} \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_{\text{total}} = 4\,\Omega \]
  2. Total current: \[ I_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{12}{4} = 3\,\text{A} \]
  3. Current through each resistor: \[ I_1 = \frac{12}{6} = 2\,\text{A}, \quad I_2 = \frac{12}{12} = 1\,\text{A} \]

Example 2: Three Branches in Parallel

Problem: Three resistors, \(R_1 = 8\,\Omega\), \(R_2 = 12\,\Omega\), \(R_3 = 24\,\Omega\), are connected in parallel across 24 V. Find total resistance and total current.

Solution:

  1. \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{8} + \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{24} = \frac{3+2+1}{24} = \frac{6}{24} \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_{\text{total}} = 4\,\Omega \]
  2. Total current: \[ I_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{24}{4} = 6\,\text{A} \]

Example 3: Parallel Lamps

Problem: Two lamps of resistances 10 Ω and 15 Ω are connected in parallel across a 12 V supply. Find the current in each lamp and the total current drawn.

Solution:

  1. Current in each lamp: \[ I_1 = \frac{12}{10} = 1.2\,\text{A}, \quad I_2 = \frac{12}{15} = 0.8\,\text{A} \]
  2. Total current: \[ I_{\text{total}} = I_1 + I_2 = 1.2 + 0.8 = 2.0\,\text{A} \]
  3. Total resistance: \[ R_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{I_{\text{total}}} = \frac{12}{2} = 6\,\Omega \]

Example 4: Unknown Resistor in Parallel

Problem: Two resistors \(R_1 = 8\,\Omega\) and \(R_2 = ?\) are connected in parallel across 16 V. The total current is measured as 4 A. Find \(R_2\) and the current in each resistor.

Solution:

  1. Total resistance: \[ R_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{I_{\text{total}}} = \frac{16}{4} = 4\,\Omega \]
  2. Use parallel formula: \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} \quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{8} + \frac{1}{R_2} \] \[ \frac{1}{R_2} = \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{1}{8} \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_2 = 8\,\Omega \]
  3. Current through each resistor: \[ I_1 = \frac{16}{8} = 2\,\text{A}, \quad I_2 = \frac{16}{8} = 2\,\text{A} \]

Series-Parallel Circuits

Some circuits combine series and parallel connections. Resistors or lamps may be in series in one branch and in parallel with other branches. Total resistance can be calculated by combining series and parallel formulas step by step.

Example 5: Mixed Circuit

Problem: Two resistors \(R_1 = 6\,\Omega\) and \(R_2 = 12\,\Omega\) are connected in series. This series combination is connected in parallel with \(R_3 = 8\,\Omega\) across 24 V. Find the total resistance and total current.

Solution:

  1. Series combination: \[ R_{\text{series}} = R_1 + R_2 = 6 + 12 = 18\,\Omega \]
  2. Parallel with \(R_3\): \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{R_{\text{series}}} + \frac{1}{R_3} = \frac{1}{18} + \frac{1}{8} = \frac{4+9}{72} = \frac{13}{72} \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_{\text{total}} \approx 5.54\,\Omega \]
  3. Total current: \[ I_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{24}{5.54} \approx 4.33\,\text{A} \]

Example 6: Series-Parallel with Unknown Resistor

Problem: A resistor \(R_1 = 10\,\Omega\) is in series with an unknown resistor \(R_2\). This combination is in parallel with \(R_3 = 20\,\Omega\) across 30 V. The total current is measured as 3 A. Find \(R_2\).

Solution:

  1. Let \(R_{\text{series}} = R_1 + R_2\). Parallel total resistance: \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{R_{\text{series}}} + \frac{1}{R_3} \]
  2. Total resistance from Ohm’s law: \[ R_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{I} = \frac{30}{3} = 10\,\Omega \]
  3. Now solve for \(R_{\text{series}}\): \[ \frac{1}{10} = \frac{1}{R_{\text{series}}} + \frac{1}{20} \quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{1}{R_{\text{series}}} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{20} = \frac{1}{20} \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_{\text{series}} = 20\,\Omega \]
  4. Then \(R_2 = R_{\text{series}} - R_1 = 20 - 10 = 10\,\Omega\)

Summary / Key Points

  • In parallel circuits, voltage across each branch is the same.
  • Current divides inversely with resistance: more resistance → less current.
  • Total resistance is always less than the smallest branch resistance.
  • Series-parallel circuits can be analyzed step by step: simplify series or parallel parts first.
  • Failures in one branch of a parallel circuit do not stop current in other branches.

  6. A 24 V battery supplies a parallel circuit with R1 = 12 Ω and R2 = 8 Ω. Find total current and current in each branch.
  7. R1 = 10 Ω, R2 = ? in parallel with total current = 5 A, voltage = 20 V. Find R2 and branch currents.
  8. Two series resistors, 6 Ω and 12 Ω, in parallel with 8 Ω across 24 V. Find total resistance and current.
  9. Three resistors 5 Ω, 10 Ω, 20 Ω in parallel with 30 V. Find total resistance and currents.
  10. A mixed series-parallel circuit: R1 = 6 Ω in series with R2 = 12 Ω, parallel with R3 = 8 Ω across 24 V. Find total resistance and current.
