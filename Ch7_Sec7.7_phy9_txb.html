Section 7.7: The Kinetic Theory of Matter

The kinetic theory of matter explains the physical properties of solids, liquids, and gases in terms of the motion of their particles. It provides a framework to understand temperature, pressure, and phase changes by describing how particles move and interact.

Postulates of the Kinetic Theory

  • Matter is made up of a large number of very small particles (atoms or molecules).
  • Particles are in constant random motion.
  • Collisions between particles and with container walls are elastic (no net loss of energy).
  • The average kinetic energy of particles is proportional to the temperature of the substance (in Kelvin):
    \( \langle KE \rangle = \frac{3}{2} k_B T \)
    where \( k_B \) is Boltzmannâ€™s constant.
  • Intermolecular forces are negligible except during collisions or when in condensed phases.

Particle Motion in Different States

  • Solids: Particles vibrate about fixed positions, giving solids a definite shape and volume.
  • Liquids: Particles move more freely but remain close together, allowing liquids to take the shape of their container.
  • Gases: Particles move rapidly and randomly, occupying the entire volume of the container.

Relation to Temperature and Pressure

According to the kinetic theory, temperature is directly proportional to the average kinetic energy of particles. Pressure in gases arises from particles colliding with the walls of their container. The greater the particle speed, the higher the pressure at constant volume.

\( P V = \frac{1}{3} N m \overline{v^2} \)

  • \( P \) = pressure of the gas
  • \( V \) = volume
  • \( N \) = number of molecules
  • \( m \) = mass of a single molecule
  • \( \overline{v^2} \) = mean square speed of molecules

Applications of Kinetic Theory

  • Explains the expansion of gases with temperature.
  • Helps understand diffusion and effusion of gases.
  • Provides a basis for understanding pressure-volume-temperature relationships in gases (ideal gas law).
  • Predicts the behavior of real gases under different temperatures and pressures.
