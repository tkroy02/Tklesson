Section 6.7: Transformers

A transformer is a device that changes the voltage and current levels of alternating current using electromagnetic induction. It consists of two coils (primary and secondary) wound on a common core.

  • Step-up transformer: Increases voltage, decreases current.
  • Step-down transformer: Decreases voltage, increases current.

The voltage ratio is given by:

\(\dfrac{V_s}{V_p} = \dfrac{N_s}{N_p}\)

where \(V_s\) and \(V_p\) are the secondary and primary voltages, and \(N_s\), \(N_p\) are the number of turns in secondary and primary coils.

For an ideal transformer, power input equals power output:

\( V_p I_p = V_s I_s \)

Example

A transformer has 100 turns in the primary and 500 turns in the secondary. If the primary voltage is 120 V, find the secondary voltage (assuming an ideal transformer).

Solution:

\[ \dfrac{V_s}{V_p} = \dfrac{N_s}{N_p} \quad \Rightarrow \quad V_s = V_p \cdot \dfrac{N_s}{N_p} \]

\( V_s = 120 \cdot \dfrac{500}{100} = 120 \cdot 5 = 600 \,\text{V} \).

The transformer steps the voltage up to 600 V.
