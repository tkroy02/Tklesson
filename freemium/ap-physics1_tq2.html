Centripetal Force
🔵 Newton's Laws First: inertia. Second: F equals ma. Third: equal and opposite forces on different objects. Action-reaction pairs never cancel.
🟠 Friction Forces Static: matches applied force up to max (mu s times N). Kinetic: constant during sliding (mu k times N). Friction opposes relative motion.
🟢 Tension & Connected Objects Atwood: a equals (m2 minus m1)g divided by (m1 plus m2). Connected blocks: find system acceleration first, then isolate individual objects.
🔴 Circular Motion Centripetal force points inward. Provided by static friction on turntables. Elevator: normal force greater than mg when accelerating upward.
