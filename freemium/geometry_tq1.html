🔵 Congruence Postulates SSS: three sides. SAS: two sides and included angle. ASA: two angles and included side. AAS: two angles and non-included side.
🟠 Key Properties Reflexive: a segment equals itself. Symmetric: if A equals B then B equals A. Transitive: if A equals B and B equals C then A equals C.
🟢 Angle Theorems Vertical angles are congruent. Alternate interior angles congruent when parallel lines cut by transversal. CPCTC after congruence.
🔴 Proof Pitfalls SSA is ambiguous (not a valid postulate). AAA proves similarity, not congruence. HL only works for right triangles.

This 10-question MCQ quiz tests your understanding of triangle congruence postulates, geometric proof structure, angle theorems, and the logical reasoning behind deductive geometry.
