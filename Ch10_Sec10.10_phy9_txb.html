Section 10.10: Chapter 10 Test

Instructions:

Answer all 20 questions. Show your workings for calculation questions.

Questions (20)

  1. Two point charges, \(q_1 = 2 \times 10^{-6} \, C\) and \(q_2 = -3 \times 10^{-6} \, C\), are separated by 0.05 m in vacuum. Calculate the electrostatic force between them.
  2. Two charges of 5 μC each are 0.2 m apart. What is the force of repulsion?
  3. Two small spheres carrying charges 4 μC and 6 μC are placed 0.3 m apart. If the force between them is measured as 2 N, what is the relative permittivity of the medium?
  4. A 1 μC charge experiences a force of 9 N when placed 0.1 m from another charge. Find the magnitude of the other charge.
  5. Explain Coulomb’s law and its inverse-square dependence on distance.
  6. Define electrostatic induction and give an everyday example.
  7. A neutral metallic sphere is placed near a positively charged rod. Explain the charge redistribution on the sphere.
  8. Explain why a conductor can be charged by induction without direct contact.
  9. Two identical small spheres of mass 0.01 kg are suspended from strings and each carries a charge of 5 μC. The strings are 0.2 m long and the spheres are in equilibrium 0.1 m apart. Find the tension in the strings.
  10. Two charges, 3 μC and 6 μC, are fixed 0.4 m apart. A third charge of 2 μC is placed midway. Calculate the net force on the third charge.
  11. A parallel plate setup creates a uniform electric field. Calculate the force on a 2 μC charge placed in a 5000 N/C field.
  12. Three point charges, \(q_1 = 2 \, \mu C\), \(q_2 = -3 \, \mu C\), and \(q_3 = 4 \, \mu C\) are placed along a straight line. The distance between \(q_1\) and \(q_2\) is 0.3 m, and between \(q_2\) and \(q_3\) is 0.5 m. Calculate the net electrostatic force on \(q_2\).
  13. A 12 V battery is connected to a 6 Ω resistor. Find the current through the resistor.
  14. A resistor of 10 Ω carries 2 A. Find the voltage across it.
  15. If two resistors, 8 Ω and 12 Ω, are connected in series to a 24 V battery, find the current and voltage drop across each resistor.
  16. Two resistors, 6 Ω and 12 Ω, are connected in parallel across 12 V. Find total resistance, total current, and branch currents.
  17. A 24 V battery supplies a series-parallel circuit: R1 = 6 Ω in series with R2 = 12 Ω, and this series combination is in parallel with R3 = 8 Ω. Find total resistance and total current.
  18. R1 = 10 Ω and unknown R2 are in series, this series in parallel with R3 = 20 Ω across 30 V. If total current is 3 A, find R2 and branch currents.
  19. Three resistors, R1 = 5 Ω, R2 = 10 Ω, R3 = 15 Ω, are connected in series-parallel: R1 and R2 in series, then in parallel with R3 across 24 V. Find total resistance and current.
  20. Compare the advantages and disadvantages of series, parallel, and series-parallel circuits in household wiring.
