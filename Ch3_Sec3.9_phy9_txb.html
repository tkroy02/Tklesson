3.9 Review

This section summarizes the main concepts from Chapter 3 on motion. Use this as a guide to check your understanding before moving on to the next chapter.

Key Concepts Recap

  • Motion: Change in position of an object with respect to time and a reference point.
  • Types of Motion: Translational, Rotational, Oscillatory, and Random.
  • Distance: Total length of the path traveled (scalar).
  • Displacement: Straight-line change in position with direction (vector).
  • Speed: Rate of change of distance (scalar). \( \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{time}} \)
  • Velocity: Rate of change of displacement (vector). \( \text{Velocity} = \frac{\text{displacement}}{\text{time}} \)
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity (vector). \( a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} \)
  • Deceleration: Negative acceleration indicating slowing down.
  • Uniform Motion: Motion at a constant speed or velocity.
  • Position-Time Graphs: Show how position changes with time. The slope represents velocity.
  • Velocity-Time Graphs: Show how velocity changes with time. The slope represents acceleration, and area under the graph represents displacement.
  • Equations of Motion: Apply to objects moving with constant acceleration.

Key Equations of Motion

Equation Description
\( v = u + at \) Final velocity after time \( t \)
\( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \) Displacement after time \( t \)
\( v^2 = u^2 + 2as \) Relates velocity, acceleration, and displacement

Review Questions

Answer the following questions to test your understanding of this chapter:

  1. Define motion and give two examples of motion around you.
  2. Differentiate between distance and displacement with examples.
  3. A car travels 120 km north and then 50 km south. What is its total distance and displacement?
  4. A ball starts from rest and rolls with an acceleration of \( 3 \, m/s^2 \) for 4 seconds. Find its final velocity.
  5. A cyclist travels at 10 m/s for 30 seconds. How far does he travel?
  6. Explain the difference between speed and velocity.
  7. The velocity of a car changes from 15 m/s to 5 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate its acceleration and state whether it is positive or negative.
  8. Sketch a position-time graph for an object at rest, moving with uniform motion, and moving with non-uniform motion.
  9. Sketch a velocity-time graph for an object with uniform acceleration.
  10. State two everyday situations where more than one type of motion occurs simultaneously.
