2.4 Unit Conversions

Measurements appear in many different units. To compare or combine them correctly, we often need to convert from one unit to another. You’ll convert both within the metric system and between the metric system and the imperial / U.S. customary system.

Why Unit Conversion Matters

  • Ensures accuracy when combining data from different sources.
  • Prevents serious mistakes in medicine dosing, engineering, or science labs.
  • Enables clear communication across countries and disciplines.
Real-World Example: In 1999, NASA lost a Mars orbiter because one team used imperial units (pound-force) while another used metric units (newton) without proper conversion!

Converting Within the Metric System

The metric system is based on powers of ten, so you mostly move the decimal point.

Example: Convert \(5.2\,\mathrm{km}\) to meters.

  1. \(1\,\mathrm{km} = 1000\,\mathrm{m}\)
  2. \(5.2\,\mathrm{km}\times \dfrac{1000\,\mathrm{m}}{1\,\mathrm{km}} = 5200\,\mathrm{m}\)

The Factor-Label (Dimensional Analysis) Method

  1. Write the given quantity.
  2. Multiply by a conversion factor so unwanted units cancel.
  3. Repeat until the target unit remains.

Example: Inches to Centimeters

Convert \(12\,\mathrm{in}\) to centimeters: \[ 12\,\mathrm{in}\times \frac{2.54\,\mathrm{cm}}{1\,\mathrm{in}} = 30.48\,\mathrm{cm}. \]

Example: Miles per Hour to Meters per Second

Convert \(60\,\mathrm{mi/h}\) to \(\mathrm{m/s}\): \[ 60\,\frac{\mathrm{mi}}{\mathrm{h}} \times \frac{1609.344\,\mathrm{m}}{1\,\mathrm{mi}} \times \frac{1\,\mathrm{h}}{3600\,\mathrm{s}} \approx 26.8\,\mathrm{m/s}. \]

Tip: Place conversion factors so units cancel diagonally, just like fractions.

Common Conversion Examples

1. Kilometers per hour to meters per second

Convert \(90\,\mathrm{km/h}\) to \(\mathrm{m/s}\):

\[ 90\,\frac{\mathrm{km}}{\mathrm{h}} \times \frac{1000\,\mathrm{m}}{1\,\mathrm{km}} \times \frac{1\,\mathrm{h}}{3600\,\mathrm{s}} = 25\,\mathrm{m/s}. \]

2. Square meters to square millimeters

Convert \(3\,\mathrm{m^2}\) to \(\mathrm{mm^2}\):

\[ 3\,\mathrm{m^2} \times \left(\frac{1000\,\mathrm{mm}}{1\,\mathrm{m}}\right)^2 = 3 \times 10^6\,\mathrm{mm^2}. \]

3. Square centimeters to square millimeters

Convert \(45\,\mathrm{cm^2}\) to \(\mathrm{mm^2}\):

\[ 45\,\mathrm{cm^2} \times \left(\frac{10\,\mathrm{mm}}{1\,\mathrm{cm}}\right)^2 = 4500\,\mathrm{mm^2}. \]

4. Hours to seconds

Convert \(2.5\,\mathrm{hr}\) to seconds:

\[ 2.5\,\mathrm{hr} \times \frac{3600\,\mathrm{s}}{1\,\mathrm{hr}} = 9000\,\mathrm{s}. \]

5. Days to seconds

Convert \(3\,\mathrm{days}\) to seconds:

\[ 3\,\mathrm{days} \times \frac{24\,\mathrm{hr}}{1\,\mathrm{day}} \times \frac{3600\,\mathrm{s}}{1\,\mathrm{hr}} = 259200\,\mathrm{s}. \]

6. Weeks to seconds

Convert \(2\,\mathrm{weeks}\) to seconds:

\[ 2\,\mathrm{weeks} \times \frac{7\,\mathrm{days}}{1\,\mathrm{week}} \times \frac{24\,\mathrm{hr}}{1\,\mathrm{day}} \times \frac{3600\,\mathrm{s}}{1\,\mathrm{hr}} = 1209600\,\mathrm{s}. \]
