Section 6.9: Mixed Practice

Solve the following AC circuits problems to strengthen your understanding of the concepts:

  1. Find the RMS current in a series RLC circuit with R = 20 Ω, L = 0.1 H, C = 50 μF, and a 120 V, 60 Hz source.
  2. A transformer steps down 240 V to 24 V. Calculate the primary current if the secondary current is 10 A.
  3. Determine the resonance frequency of a series RLC circuit with L = 0.2 H and C = 80 μF.
  4. Compute the impedance of a series RC circuit with R = 30 Ω and C = 100 μF at 50 Hz.
  5. A voltage source of 220 V, 50 Hz is applied to a series RL circuit with R = 40 Ω and L = 0.2 H. Find the current and power factor.
  6. Calculate the average power consumed by a load of 10 Ω in an AC circuit with RMS voltage of 120 V and current of 5 A.
  7. Find the phase angle in a series RLC circuit where R = 50 Ω, X_L = 40 Ω, X_C = 30 Ω.
  8. Determine the total reactance in a parallel LC circuit with L = 0.1 H and C = 50 μF at 60 Hz.
  9. A series RLC circuit has a peak voltage of 170 V and a peak current of 2 A. Find the RMS values and the impedance.
  10. Calculate the secondary voltage in a transformer if the turns ratio is 10:1 and the primary voltage is 220 V.
