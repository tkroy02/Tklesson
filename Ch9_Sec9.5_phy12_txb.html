Section 9.5: Relativistic Mass

Relativistic mass increases with the velocity of an object according to special relativity. This effect becomes significant at speeds close to the speed of light.

Relativistic Mass Formula:

\( m = \frac{m_0}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} \)

  • \(m\) = relativistic mass
  • \(m_0\) = rest mass
  • \(v\) = velocity of the object
  • \(c\) = speed of light

Example 1

An electron has rest mass \(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\) kg and moves at 0.8c. Find its relativistic mass.

\( m = \frac{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}{\sqrt{1-0.8^2}} = \frac{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}{\sqrt{0.36}} = \frac{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}{0.6} \approx 1.518 \times 10^{-30} \text{ kg} \)
