Section 10.2: Gauss’s Law for Magnetism

Gauss’s Law for Magnetism states that the net magnetic flux through any closed surface is zero, reflecting the absence of magnetic monopoles.

Equation:

\[ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0 \]

Example

Consider a spherical surface in a uniform magnetic field. Compute the net flux.

Because magnetic field lines are continuous with no beginning or end, the net flux through any closed surface is zero:

\[ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0 \]

Practice Problems

  1. Explain why Gauss’s Law for Magnetism implies no isolated magnetic charges.
  2. Verify the law for a cube placed in a uniform magnetic field.
  3. Contrast this with Gauss’s Law for Electricity in terms of enclosed charges.
