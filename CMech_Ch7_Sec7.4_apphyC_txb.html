Section 7.4: Satellite Motion

This section covers the motion of satellites, including circular and elliptical orbits, velocity requirements, and altitude considerations for stable orbits.

Circular Orbit Velocity:

\( v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} \)

Orbital Period:

\( T = 2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{r^3}{GM}} \)

Example 1

Find the velocity required for a satellite 500 km above Earth’s surface (Earth radius 6371 km, mass \(5.97 \times 10^{24}\) kg).

Radius from Earth's center: \( r = 6371 + 500 = 6871 \, \text{km} = 6.871 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)

Velocity: \( v = \sqrt{\frac{6.674\times10^{-11} \cdot 5.97\times10^{24}}{6.871\times10^6}} \approx 7610 \, \text{m/s} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate velocity for a satellite orbiting 200 km above Earth.
  2. Compute orbital period for a satellite 10,000 km from Earth's center.
  3. Determine speed for a geostationary satellite.
  4. Compare orbital velocities of two satellites at 300 km and 1000 km altitudes.
  5. Find the period of a satellite orbiting a planet of mass \(6 \times 10^{24}\) kg at 1 × 10^7 m.
