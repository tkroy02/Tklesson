Section 8.3: Nuclear Reactions

Nuclear reactions involve changes in an atom's nucleus, leading to energy release, particle emission, or transformation of elements. These reactions are central to both nuclear energy and astrophysics.

Key Nuclear Reactions:
  • Fission: Splitting of a heavy nucleus into smaller nuclei with energy release.
  • Fusion: Combining light nuclei to form a heavier nucleus, releasing energy.
  • Alpha, Beta, Gamma emissions: Forms of radioactive decay during nuclear reactions.
  • Reaction notation example: \( _Z^A X + _1^0 n \rightarrow _Z^{A+1}X + \gamma \)

Example 1

A Uranium-235 nucleus absorbs a neutron and splits into Barium-141, Krypton-92, and 3 neutrons. Write the balanced nuclear equation.

\( ^{235}_{92}U + ^1_0 n \rightarrow ^{141}_{56}Ba + ^{92}_{36}Kr + 3 ^1_0 n \)

Practice Problems

  1. Explain the energy release mechanism in nuclear fission.
  2. Write the nuclear equation for the fusion of Deuterium and Tritium.
  3. A nucleus undergoes beta-minus decay. How does the atomic and mass numbers change?
  4. Calculate the energy released when 1 gram of Uranium-235 undergoes fission. (Use \( E = mc^2 \))
  5. Distinguish between controlled and uncontrolled nuclear reactions.
  6. Sketch a diagram showing neutron-induced fission chain reaction.
  7. Describe one practical application of nuclear reactions in medicine.
  8. Explain why fusion reactions require extremely high temperatures.
  9. Write the nuclear reaction for alpha decay of Polonium-210.
  10. Discuss safety concerns associated with nuclear reactors.
