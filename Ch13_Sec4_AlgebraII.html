Section 13.4: Applications of Matrices

This section demonstrates real-world applications of matrices, including solving systems of linear equations, network problems, and input-output models in economics.

Example 1: Solving a System with Matrices

Solve the system:
\( 2x + 3y = 5 \)
\( 4x - y = 1 \) using matrix methods.

Step 1: Write in matrix form: \( AX = B \), where \( A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 3\\ 4 & -1\end{bmatrix} \), \( X = \begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix}5\\1\end{bmatrix} \)

Step 2: Find \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{(2)(-1) - (3)(4)} \begin{bmatrix}-1 & -3\\ -4 & 2\end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-14} \begin{bmatrix}-1 & -3\\ -4 & 2\end{bmatrix} \)

Step 3: Compute \( X = A^{-1}B = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 3/14\\ 2/7 & -1/7\end{bmatrix} ...\) â†’ solution \( x = 1, y = 1\)

Example 2: Input-Output Model

A company produces two products. The input-output matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix}0.2 & 0.3\\0.4 & 0.1\end{bmatrix} \). Determine total output for demand vector \( \begin{bmatrix}100\\150\end{bmatrix} \).

Step 1: Use formula \( X = (I - A)^{-1} D \)

Step 2: Compute \( I - A = \begin{bmatrix}0.8 & -0.3\\ -0.4 & 0.9\end{bmatrix} \)

Step 3: Invert and multiply by demand vector to find total output: \( X = \begin{bmatrix}125\\100\end{bmatrix} \)
