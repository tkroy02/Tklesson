Section 9.3: Inductors & Inductance

Inductors are components that oppose changes in current by producing an induced EMF. The proportionality constant is called inductance, denoted \( L \).

Definition:

\[ \text{EMF}_{\text{induced}} = -L \frac{dI}{dt} \] where \( L \) is the inductance in henrys (H), and \( dI/dt \) is the rate of change of current.

Energy Stored in Inductor:

\[ U = \frac{1}{2} L I^2 \]

Example 1

A 2 H inductor carries a current increasing at 3 A/s. Calculate the induced EMF.

\[ \text{EMF} = -L \frac{dI}{dt} = -2 \cdot 3 = -6 \text{ V} \]

The negative sign indicates the EMF opposes the change.
