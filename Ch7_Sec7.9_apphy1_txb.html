Section 7.9: Review Examples

This section reviews key concepts from rotational motion including angular velocity, torque, moment of inertia, and gyroscopic precession.

Example 1: Torque and Angular Acceleration

A disc of radius 0.5 m and mass 2 kg is subjected to a torque of 4 N·m. Find its angular acceleration.

Moment of inertia of disc: \(I = \frac{1}{2} m r^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 \cdot 0.5^2 = 0.25\ \text{kg·m²}\)

Angular acceleration: \(\alpha = \frac{\tau}{I} = \frac{4}{0.25} = 16\ \text{rad/s²}\)

Example 2: Gyroscope Precession

A gyroscope has angular momentum 60 kg·m²/s. A torque of 12 N·m acts on it. Find precession rate.

\(\Omega = \frac{\tau}{L} = \frac{12}{60} = 0.2\ \text{rad/s}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A solid sphere of mass 3 kg and radius 0.4 m is subjected to torque 6 N·m. Find angular acceleration.
  2. Wheel with I = 5 kg·m² spins with ω = 10 rad/s. Calculate kinetic energy.
  3. A gyroscope with L = 100 kg·m²/s is under torque 20 N·m. Determine precession angular velocity.
