Section 12.3: Optimization Problems

Optimization involves finding the maximum or minimum value of a quantity under given constraints. These problems often use systems of inequalities to define feasible regions, and then test vertices of the region to find optimal solutions.

Example 1: Maximizing Profit

A company makes two products, A and B. Each unit of A yields \$40 profit, each unit of B yields \$30 profit. Production is limited by: - \( 2A + B \leq 100 \) (labor constraint) - \( A + 2B \leq 80 \) (machine constraint) - \( A, B \geq 0 \). Maximize profit.

Profit function: \( P = 40A + 30B \).

Feasible region determined by inequalities.

Vertices: (0,0), (0,40), (50,0), (20,30).

Evaluate P: (0,0)=0, (0,40)=1200, (50,0)=2000, (20,30)=1700.

Maximum profit = \$2000 at (50,0).

Example 2: Minimizing Cost

A farmer wants to build a rectangular pen with fencing on 3 sides (one side along a barn). If 200 m of fencing is available, what dimensions minimize the area?

Let width = x, length = y. Constraint: \( 2x + y = 200 \).

Area: \( A = xy = x(200 - 2x) = 200x - 2x^2 \).

This is quadratic, opens downward, max area at vertex.

x = 50 → y = 100 → Max area = 5000 m².

Practice Problems

  1. Maximize \( P = 5x + 3y \) subject to: \( x + y \leq 10 \), \( x \geq 0 \), \( y \geq 0 \).
  2. A company produces tables and chairs. Tables need 4 hours of labor, chairs need 2 hours. Max labor is 80 hours. Tables yield \$50 profit, chairs \$30. Write and solve the optimization problem.
  3. Find dimensions of a rectangle with perimeter 60 m that maximizes area.
  4. Maximize \( P = 7x + 9y \) subject to: \( 2x + y \leq 18 \), \( x + y \leq 12 \), \( x, y \geq 0 \).
  5. A farmer has 240 m of fencing to make a rectangular pen divided into 3 sections by fences parallel to one side. Find dimensions maximizing area.
