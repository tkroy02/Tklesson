Section 8.6: Symmetry Applications

Using symmetry considerations can greatly simplify the application of Ampère’s Law and other magnetic field calculations.

Concept:

Symmetry in the current distribution allows choosing Ampèrian loops where the magnetic field is constant along the loop and either parallel or perpendicular to the path.

Example 1

Determine the magnetic field around a long straight wire using cylindrical symmetry.

By cylindrical symmetry, the magnetic field magnitude is the same at all points equidistant from the wire. Using an Ampèrian circular loop of radius \( r \):

\[ B(2 \pi r) = \mu_0 I \quad \Rightarrow \quad B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Use symmetry to find the magnetic field inside a long solenoid.
  2. Explain why the magnetic field outside a toroid is zero using symmetry.
  3. Apply Ampère’s Law to an infinite sheet of current and find the field above and below.
  4. Justify choosing circular loops for straight wires and solenoids.
  5. Discuss a case where symmetry cannot simplify the problem and why.
