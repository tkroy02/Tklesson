Section 7.1: Lorentz Force on Moving Charge

The Lorentz Force describes the force experienced by a charged particle moving in electric and magnetic fields.

Formula:

\[ \vec{F} = q(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \] where \(q\) is the charge, \(\vec{v}\) is the velocity, \(\vec{E}\) is the electric field, and \(\vec{B}\) is the magnetic field.

Example 1

A particle with charge \(q = 1\,C\) moves with velocity \( \vec{v} = 2\hat{i}\, m/s\) in a magnetic field \( \vec{B} = 3\hat{k}\, T\). Find the magnetic force on the particle.

Using the cross product: \(\vec{F} = q \vec{v} \times \vec{B}\)

\(\vec{F} = 1 (2\hat{i} \times 3\hat{k}) = 6 \hat{j}\, N\)

Practice Problems

  1. Compute the Lorentz force for a charge moving perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field.
  2. Describe the motion of a charged particle moving parallel to a magnetic field.
  3. Find the trajectory radius of a particle in a uniform magnetic field.
  4. Explain the effect of the electric field on a moving charge in combination with a magnetic field.
  5. Conceptual: What direction is the force relative to velocity and magnetic field?
