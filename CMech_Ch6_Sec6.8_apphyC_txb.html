Section 6.8: Advanced Problems

This section contains challenging problems on oscillations to strengthen problem-solving skills in SHM, damped, and driven oscillations.

Advanced Problems

  1. A 2 kg mass attached to a spring k = 200 N/m undergoes SHM with amplitude 0.1 m. Calculate the maximum kinetic energy.
  2. A damped oscillator has m = 0.5 kg, b = 1 kg/s, k = 50 N/m. Determine damping ratio and classify motion.
  3. A driven oscillator has F0 = 5 N, m = 1 kg, k = 25 N/m, b = 0.2 kg/s, ω = 4 rad/s. Compute amplitude.
  4. A pendulum of length 0.8 m and small angle oscillation has initial displacement θ0 = 0.1 rad. Compute maximum speed.
  5. Two coupled oscillators: m1 = m2 = 1 kg, k = 100 N/m each, initially displaced 0.05 m. Determine normal mode frequencies.
  6. A 0.3 kg mass-spring system has damping coefficient b = 0.1 kg/s and k = 8 N/m. Determine time for amplitude to reduce to half.
  7. A mass of 1 kg oscillates with SHM at frequency 2 Hz and amplitude 0.2 m. Compute acceleration at displacement x = 0.1 m.
  8. A forced oscillator has natural frequency 5 rad/s. Find driving frequency that produces resonance.
  9. Damped pendulum: m = 0.4 kg, length = 1 m, b = 0.05 kg·m²/s. Calculate angular frequency of damped oscillation.
  10. SHM: mass 0.5 kg, k = 10 N/m. If total mechanical energy is 0.25 J, determine amplitude.
