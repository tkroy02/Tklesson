Section 8.4: Fission & Fusion

Fission and fusion are two nuclear processes that release massive amounts of energy. Fission splits heavy nuclei, while fusion combines light nuclei. Both are fundamental in energy generation and astrophysics.

Key Concepts:
  • Nuclear Fission: Heavy nucleus splits into smaller nuclei + neutrons + energy. Example: \( ^{235}U + n \rightarrow ^{141}Ba + ^{92}Kr + 3n \)
  • Nuclear Fusion: Light nuclei combine to form heavier nucleus + energy. Example: \( ^2H + ^3H \rightarrow ^4He + n + \text{energy} \)
  • Energy comes from mass defect using Einstein’s equation: \( E = \Delta m c^2 \)
  • Applications: Nuclear reactors, nuclear weapons, stars’ energy source.

Example 1

Calculate the energy released when 1 gram of Uranium-235 undergoes fission. (Use \( E = mc^2 \))

Mass converted to energy: \( m = 1\text{ g} = 0.001 \text{ kg} \)

\( E = mc^2 = 0.001 * (3*10^8)^2 = 9*10^{13} \text{ J} \)
