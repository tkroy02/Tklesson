Section 6.2: Energy in SHM

In simple harmonic motion, energy oscillates between kinetic and potential forms while total mechanical energy remains constant.

Potential Energy:

\( U = \frac{1}{2} k x^2 \)

Kinetic Energy:

\( K = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \)

Total Energy:

\( E_\text{total} = K + U = \frac{1}{2} k A^2 \)

Maximum kinetic energy occurs at equilibrium; maximum potential energy occurs at maximum displacement.

Example 1

A 0.5 kg mass oscillates on a spring with k = 200 N/m and amplitude A = 0.1 m. Find total mechanical energy.

\( E_\text{total} = \frac{1}{2} k A^2 = 0.5 * 200 * 0.1^2 = 1 \text{ J} \)

Example 2

Find the kinetic energy of the mass when it is at x = 0.05 m from equilibrium.

\( U = 0.5 * 200 * 0.05^2 = 0.25 \text{ J} \)

\( K = E_\text{total} - U = 1 - 0.25 = 0.75 \text{ J} \)
