Section 9.9: Power in AC Circuits

In AC circuits, the instantaneous power is:

\[ p(t) = v(t) \cdot i(t) \]

For sinusoidal voltage and current:

\[ v(t) = V_m \sin(\omega t), \quad i(t) = I_m \sin(\omega t - \phi) \]

Average power delivered to a load is:

\[ P_{avg} = V_{rms} I_{rms} \cos\phi \]

\( \phi \) is the phase angle between voltage and current. \( \cos\phi \) is called the power factor.

Key Formulas:
  • RMS values: \( V_{rms} = V_m/\sqrt{2}, I_{rms} = I_m/\sqrt{2} \)
  • Apparent power: \( S = V_{rms} I_{rms} \)
  • Reactive power: \( Q = V_{rms} I_{rms} \sin\phi \)

Example 1

An AC circuit has \( V_{rms} = 120 \text{ V}, I_{rms} = 5 \text{ A}, \phi = 30^\circ \). Find the average, apparent, and reactive power.

Average power: \( P_{avg} = 120 \cdot 5 \cdot \cos30^\circ \approx 519.6 \text{ W} \)

Apparent power: \( S = 120 \cdot 5 = 600 \text{ VA} \)

Reactive power: \( Q = 120 \cdot 5 \cdot \sin30^\circ = 300 \text{ VAR} \)
