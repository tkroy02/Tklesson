3.10 Chapter Three Test

This test contains 20 questions covering concepts from Chapter 3 on Motion. Answer all questions carefully. Show your working where required. Calculators may be used unless instructed otherwise.

  1. Multiple Choice: Which of the following quantities is a vector?
    A) Speed
    B) Distance
    C) Velocity
    D) Time
  2. Define displacement and explain how it differs from distance.
  3. A boy walks 100 m east and then 60 m west.
    • a) Calculate the total distance traveled.
    • b) Calculate his displacement.
  4. A car accelerates uniformly from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 8 seconds. Calculate its acceleration.
  5. The velocity of a bicycle decreases from 20 m/s to 10 m/s in 5 seconds.
    • a) Calculate its acceleration.
    • b) State whether this is positive or negative acceleration.
  6. Match the type of motion with its correct example:

    Type of Motion Examples
    Translational i) Swinging pendulum
    Rotational ii) Moving car
    Oscillatory iii) Ceiling fan
    Random iv) Flying mosquito

    Write your answers using the format: Type - Letter.

  7. A car moves at a speed of 72 km/h. Convert this speed to m/s.
  8. Explain the difference between uniform motion and non-uniform motion with examples.
  9. A bus covers 150 km in 3 hours and then 90 km in 2 hours.
    • a) Calculate the average speed of the bus for the whole journey.
    • b) Calculate the total distance traveled.
  10. A ball rolls from rest with an acceleration of 2.5 m/s² for 6 seconds.
    • a) Find the final velocity.
    • b) Calculate the distance traveled in that time.
  11. Draw a position-time graph for:
    • a) An object at rest.
    • b) An object moving with uniform speed.
    • c) An object accelerating.
  12. A velocity-time graph of a car shows a straight line with a slope of 3 m/s². Explain what this indicates about the car's motion.
  13. A stone is dropped from rest and falls freely under gravity.
    • a) State the acceleration of the stone near Earth's surface.
    • b) Calculate its velocity after 5 seconds (g = 9.8 m/s²).
  14. A train moving at 25 m/s comes to a stop in 50 seconds.
    • a) Calculate the retardation (negative acceleration).
    • b) Find the distance covered before stopping.
  15. Describe two real-life examples where two types of motion occur simultaneously.
  16. A cyclist moves east 300 m, then north 400 m.
    • a) Calculate the total distance traveled.
    • b) Find the displacement using the Pythagorean theorem.
  17. A car starts from rest and accelerates at 4 m/s² for 10 seconds. Use equations of motion to find:
    • a) Final velocity
    • b) Total distance covered
  18. A graph shows velocity vs. time for a moving object.
    • a) State what the slope of the graph represents.
    • b) State what the area under the graph represents.
  19. State three differences between speed and velocity.
  20. A runner completes a circular track of 400 m in length. After one full lap:
    • a) What is the total distance traveled?
    • b) What is the displacement?
