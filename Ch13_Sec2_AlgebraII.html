Section 13.2: Matrix Operations

This section covers addition, subtraction, and scalar multiplication of matrices, along with basic properties and rules for valid operations.

Example 1: Adding Matrices

Add the matrices:

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 2\\ 3 & 4\end{bmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{bmatrix}5 & 6\\ 7 & 8\end{bmatrix} \]

Step 1: Add corresponding elements:

\[ A + B = \begin{bmatrix}1+5 & 2+6\\ 3+7 & 4+8\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 8\\ 10 & 12\end{bmatrix} \]

Example 2: Scalar Multiplication

Multiply the matrix \( C = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -1\\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix} \) by scalar 4.

Step 1: Multiply each element by 4:

\[ 4C = \begin{bmatrix}8 & -4\\ 0 & 12\end{bmatrix} \]
