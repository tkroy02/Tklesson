Section 6.2: RC Circuit Differential Equations

RC circuits involve a resistor and a capacitor in series or parallel. The voltage and current follow first-order differential equations derived from Kirchhoff’s laws.

Series RC Circuit Equation:

For a series RC circuit connected to a voltage source \( V_0 \):

\[ V_0 = IR + \frac{q}{C} \]

Since \( I = \frac{dq}{dt} \), we get:

\[ R \frac{dq}{dt} + \frac{q}{C} = V_0 \]

Solution Form:

The differential equation has solution for charging:

\[ q(t) = C V_0 \left( 1 - e^{-t/RC} \right) \]

and for discharging (with no source):

\[ q(t) = Q_0 e^{-t/RC} \]

Example 1

A 10 µF capacitor in series with a 2 kΩ resistor is charged with a 12 V battery. Find the time constant and charge after 5 ms.

Time constant: \( \tau = RC = 2000 \cdot 10 \times 10^{-6} = 0.02 \, s \)

Charge after 5 ms: \( q(t) = CV_0(1 - e^{-t/\tau}) = 10\times10^{-6}\cdot12(1 - e^{-0.005/0.02}) \approx 2.91 \times 10^{-5} \, C \)

Practice Problems

  1. Derive the differential equation for a series RC circuit with a step input voltage.
  2. Calculate the time constant of a 100 Ω resistor and 50 µF capacitor.
  3. Find the current as a function of time for the charging circuit in Example 1.
  4. Analyze a parallel RC circuit and write its governing differential equation.
  5. Explain how the time constant affects the charging and discharging curves.
