3.4 Acceleration

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity of an object with respect to time. It tells us how quickly an object speeds up, slows down, or changes direction.

Key Concept: Acceleration can be positive (speeding up) or negative (slowing down, also called deceleration). It is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction.

Formula

The average acceleration \(a\) is given by:

\( a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t_f - t_i} \)

Where:

  • \(v_f\) = final velocity
  • \(v_i\) = initial velocity
  • \(\Delta t = t_f - t_i\) = time interval

Examples

  • A car accelerates from 0 m/s to 20 m/s in 5 s. Solution: \(a = \frac{20 - 0}{5} = 4 \text{ m/s²}\)
  • A train slows from 30 m/s to 10 m/s in 20 s. Solution: \(a = \frac{10 - 30}{20} = -1 \text{ m/s²}\) (negative indicates deceleration)
  • A cyclist increases velocity from 5 m/s to 15 m/s in 10 s. Solution: \(a = \frac{15 - 5}{10} = 1 \text{ m/s²}\)

Units

The SI unit of acceleration is meters per second squared (m/s²). Other units may include km/h² or cm/s² depending on context.

Deceleration (Negative Acceleration)

Negative acceleration is also known as deceleration or retardation. It occurs when an object slows down. Like positive acceleration, it is measured in meters per second squared (m/s²). Since acceleration is a vector quantity, it has both magnitude and direction.

  • Example: A car slows down from 30 m/s to 10 m/s in 5 s.
  • Calculation: \( a = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t} = \frac{10 - 30}{5} = -4 \text{ m/s²} \)
  • The negative sign indicates deceleration.

Practice Problems

  1. A car accelerates from 0 m/s to 25 m/s in 10 s. Find the acceleration.
  2. A train slows from 40 m/s to 10 m/s in 15 s. Determine its deceleration.
  3. An object reaches a velocity of 30 m/s from rest in 6 s. Calculate the acceleration.
  4. A cyclist accelerates from 5 m/s to 15 m/s in 10 s. Determine the acceleration.
  5. A vehicle decelerates from 20 m/s to 0 m/s in 4 s. Find the magnitude of deceleration.
  6. A car moving at 18 m/s comes to rest in 9 s. Calculate the deceleration and the final velocity.
