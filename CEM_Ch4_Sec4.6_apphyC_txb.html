Section 4.6: Capacitors in Series & Parallel

Capacitors can be combined in circuits, and their equivalent capacitance depends on whether they are connected in series or in parallel.

Capacitors in Parallel:

\[ C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2 + C_3 + \cdots \]

Each capacitor has the same potential difference \(V\), and the total charge is the sum of charges stored.

Capacitors in Series:

\[ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \frac{1}{C_3} + \cdots \]

Each capacitor has the same charge \(Q\), and the potential difference divides among them.

Example 1

Two capacitors, \(C_1 = 6 \,\mu\text{F}\) and \(C_2 = 12 \,\mu\text{F}\), are connected in (a) parallel and (b) series. Find the equivalent capacitance in each case.

(a) Parallel:

\[ C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2 = 6 + 12 = 18 \,\mu\text{F} \]

(b) Series:

\[ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{12} = \frac{1}{4} \quad \Rightarrow \quad C_{eq} = 4 \,\mu\text{F} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Three capacitors \(C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}\), \(C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}\), and \(C_3 = 12\,\mu\text{F}\) are connected in parallel. Find \(C_{eq}\).
  2. Calculate the equivalent capacitance of \(C_1 = 10\,\mu\text{F}\) and \(C_2 = 20\,\mu\text{F}\) in series.
  3. A \(5\,\mu\text{F}\) and \(15\,\mu\text{F}\) capacitor are connected in parallel across a \(12\,\text{V}\) battery. Find the total energy stored.
  4. Why do capacitors in series reduce equivalent capacitance?
  5. Explain a practical application of connecting capacitors in series or parallel in electronic devices.
