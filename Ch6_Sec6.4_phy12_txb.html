Section 6.4: RLC Circuits

An RLC circuit is an electrical circuit consisting of a resistor (R), an inductor (L), and a capacitor (C) connected in series or parallel with an AC source.

  • Series RLC circuit: Current is the same in all components, but voltages differ.
  • Impedance:

    \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \)

  • Phase Angle:

    \( \tan\phi = \dfrac{X_L - X_C}{R} \)

  • If \( X_L > X_C \): circuit is inductive, current lags voltage.
  • If \( X_C > X_L \): circuit is capacitive, current leads voltage.
  • If \( X_L = X_C \): circuit is at resonance, current and voltage are in phase.

Example

A series RLC circuit has \( R = 20 \,\Omega \), \( L = 0.2 \,\text{H} \), and \( C = 50 \,\mu\text{F} \). The supply frequency is 60 Hz. Find the impedance and phase angle.

Solution:

\( \omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi(60) \approx 377 \,\text{rad/s} \).

\( X_L = \omega L = 377 \times 0.2 \approx 75.4 \,\Omega \).

\( X_C = \dfrac{1}{\omega C} = \dfrac{1}{377 \times 50 \times 10^{-6}} \approx 53.0 \,\Omega \).

\( Z = \sqrt{20^2 + (75.4 - 53.0)^2} = \sqrt{400 + 22.4^2} \approx 30.0 \,\Omega \).

\( \tan\phi = \dfrac{22.4}{20} = 1.12 \implies \phi \approx 48.1^\circ \).

Thus, the circuit is inductive, and current lags voltage by about 48Â°.
