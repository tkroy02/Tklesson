Section 7.2: Force on Current-Carrying Wire

A current-carrying wire placed in a magnetic field experiences a force given by the Lorentz force law applied to a distribution of moving charges.

Formula:

\[ \vec{F} = I \vec{L} \times \vec{B} \] where \(I\) is the current, \(\vec{L}\) is the vector along the wire segment, and \(\vec{B}\) is the magnetic field.

Example 1

A wire of length 0.5 m carries a current of 2 A and is placed perpendicular to a magnetic field of 0.3 T. Calculate the force on the wire.

Force magnitude: \( F = I L B = 2 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.3 = 0.3\, N \)

Direction is given by the right-hand rule perpendicular to both the wire and the field.

Practice Problems

  1. Find the force on a wire loop in a uniform magnetic field.
  2. Explain how the orientation of the wire affects the magnitude and direction of the force.
  3. Calculate the torque on a rectangular current loop in a magnetic field.
  4. Conceptual: Why does a current-carrying wire experience a force?
  5. Determine the net force on a wire bent into a semicircle in a uniform field.
