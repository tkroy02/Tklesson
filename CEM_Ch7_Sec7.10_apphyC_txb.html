Section 7.10: Practice Problems

This section consolidates the concepts of magnetic fields and forces. Attempt these problems to test your understanding of Chapter 7.

Problems

  1. A proton moves at \(2 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\) perpendicular to a 0.3 T field. Calculate the radius and period of its circular path.
  2. Find the torque on a square loop of side 0.1 m carrying 5 A in a 0.2 T uniform field at 30° to the plane of the loop.
  3. Using Ampère's law, find the magnetic field inside a solenoid of 500 turns/m carrying 2 A current.
  4. Compute the magnetic dipole moment of a circular coil of radius 0.05 m carrying 3 A with 10 turns.
  5. A charged particle moves at an angle of 60° to a uniform magnetic field of 0.1 T. Find the pitch of its helical path if its speed is \(1 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}\).
  6. Evaluate the curl of the magnetic field \(\vec{B} = B_0 (x\hat{i} - y\hat{j})\) T/m.
  7. A long straight wire carries 10 A. Find the magnetic field 0.2 m away from the wire.
  8. Explain why the divergence of any magnetic field is always zero and relate it to physical observations.
  9. Compute the line integral of a variable magnetic field \(\vec{B} = B_0 y \hat{k}\) along a path from (0,0,0) to (0,2,0).
  10. Derive the potential energy of a magnetic dipole in a uniform field oriented at an arbitrary angle \(\theta\).
