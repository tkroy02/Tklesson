Chapter 1 Test: Grade 9 Physics

Answer all questions to the best of your ability. Show all calculations and reasoning where necessary.

Multiple Choice / Short Answer

  1. Define physics in your own words.
  2. List three real-world applications of physics.
  3. What is the difference between classical and modern physics?
  4. Give the meaning of a theory and a law in science. Provide examples of each.
  5. Explain the relationship between a theory and a law. Can theories become laws?
  6. Identify the independent, dependent, and constant variables in the experiment: “Effect of sunlight on plant growth”.
  7. Convert 0.00056 m to standard form.
  8. Express 7,450,000 in scientific notation.
  9. Round 0.004567 to 3 significant figures.
  10. Perform the following addition using standard form: \(2.5 \times 10^3 + 3.4 \times 10^4 + 6.7 \times 10^2\).
  11. Perform the following multiplication using standard form: \((2 \times 10^3) \cdot (4 \times 10^2) \cdot (5 \times 10^1)\).
  12. In the equation \(y = 8x^2 + 5\): identify the coefficient, constant, dependent variable, and independent variable.
  13. By following the scientific method, work on the project “A football floating on water”. List each step you would take.

Problem Solving

  1. A car travels 120 km east and then 50 km west in a total time of 3 hours. Calculate its average speed.
  2. A scientist recorded the following plant heights after watering: 50 ml → 12 cm, 100 ml → 18 cm, 150 ml → 23 cm. Plot a graph showing plant height vs water amount and determine the trend.
  3. Using the equation \(F = ma\), calculate the force on a box with mass 10 kg accelerating at 2 m/s².
