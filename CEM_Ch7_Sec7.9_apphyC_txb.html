Section 7.9: Variable Field Calculus

Many practical magnetic field problems involve fields that vary in space. Vector calculus techniques such as gradient, divergence, and curl allow us to calculate magnetic field properties for non-uniform or variable fields.

Divergence of Magnetic Field:

\[ \nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0 \]

Magnetic fields are always solenoidal; there are no magnetic monopoles.

Curl of Magnetic Field:

\[ \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 \vec{J} + \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \vec{E}}{\partial t} \]

Relates currents and changing electric fields to spatial variation in \(\vec{B}\).

Line and Surface Integrals for Variable Fields:

\[ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l}, \quad \iint (\nabla \times \vec{B}) \cdot d\vec{A} \]

Used to evaluate magnetic effects over curves and surfaces when the field varies with position.

Example 1

A magnetic field varies with position as \(\vec{B} = B_0 \frac{y}{L} \hat{k}\). Find the curl of \(\vec{B}\).

\[ \nabla \times \vec{B} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ \partial_x & \partial_y & \partial_z \\ 0 & 0 & B_0 \frac{y}{L} \end{vmatrix} = \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x} B_z - \frac{\partial}{\partial z} B_x \right) \hat{j} - \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial y} B_z - \frac{\partial}{\partial z} B_y \right) \hat{i} = -\frac{B_0}{L} \hat{i} \]

The curl points in the negative x-direction, indicating the rotational tendency of the field.
