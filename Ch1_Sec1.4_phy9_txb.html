Section 1.4: Scientific Notation

Scientific notation is a method of expressing very large or very small numbers in a compact form. It is widely used in physics to simplify calculations and improve readability.

Key Concept: Scientific notation expresses numbers as a product of a number between 1 and 10 and a power of ten.

Standard Form

A number in scientific notation is written as:

$$N = a \times 10^n$$

where:

  • a is a number such that 1 ≤ a < 10
  • n is an integer representing the power of 10

Examples

  • 4,500 → \(4.5 \times 10^3\)
  • 0.00072 → \(7.2 \times 10^{-4}\)
  • 9,000,000 → \(9 \times 10^6\)
  • 0.0000051 → \(5.1 \times 10^{-6}\)
Tip: Count the number of places you move the decimal point to determine the exponent.

Operations with Scientific Notation

  • Multiplication: Multiply the coefficients and add the exponents:

    $$ (2 \times 10^3) \cdot (3 \times 10^4) = 6 \times 10^{7} $$

  • Division: Divide the coefficients and subtract the exponents:

    $$ \frac{6 \times 10^5}{2 \times 10^2} = 3 \times 10^3 $$

  • Addition/Subtraction: Convert numbers to the same exponent before adding/subtracting:

    $$ (3 \times 10^4) + (2 \times 10^3) = (3 \times 10^4) + (0.2 \times 10^4) = 3.2 \times 10^4 $$

Applications in Physics

  • Expressing the speed of light: \(c = 3 \times 10^8\) m/s
  • Mass of an electron: \(m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\) kg
  • Distance to the sun: \(1.496 \times 10^{11}\) m
  • Charge of an electron: \(1.602 \times 10^{-19}\) C

Key Takeaways

  • Scientific notation simplifies very large or very small numbers.
  • Numbers are written as a coefficient between 1 and 10 multiplied by a power of ten.
  • Operations with scientific notation follow specific rules for multiplication, division, addition, and subtraction.
  • Scientific notation is essential for expressing physical quantities in a concise and readable way.
