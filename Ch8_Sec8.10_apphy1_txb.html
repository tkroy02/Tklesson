Section 8.10: Unit Test

This unit test covers all topics from Fluid Mechanics. Attempt these problems to evaluate your understanding of density, pressure, buoyancy, Bernoulli’s principle, viscosity, and fluid flow.

Unit Test Questions

  1. A 0.05 m diameter pipe carries water at 2 m/s. Calculate the Reynolds number and determine flow type.
  2. Water flows from a 0.1 m diameter pipe into a 0.05 m diameter pipe. If velocity in the larger pipe is 1.5 m/s, find velocity in the smaller pipe.
  3. Calculate the pressure difference between two points in a horizontal pipe if water rises 0.3 m in a vertical tube.
  4. A cube of density 800 kg/m³ is floating in water. Find the fraction submerged.
  5. Derive Bernoulli’s equation along a streamline and explain each term.
  6. Explain the difference between laminar and turbulent flow with examples.
  7. Air flows in a duct 0.25 m in diameter at 3 m/s. Determine whether flow is laminar or turbulent. Air density = 1.2 kg/m³, viscosity = 1.8×10⁻⁵ Pa·s.
  8. Compute velocity in a pipe narrowing from 0.2 m² to 0.05 m² cross-section, given initial velocity 4 m/s.
  9. Describe how viscosity affects flow in a pipe.
  10. Explain why turbulent flow increases mixing and energy dissipation.
