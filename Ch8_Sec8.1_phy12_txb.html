Section 8.1: Atomic Structure

Atomic structure is the study of the components of an atom, including the nucleus (protons and neutrons) and electrons. Understanding energy levels, electron configurations, and isotopes is fundamental for nuclear physics.

Key Concepts:
  • Protons: Positively charged particles in the nucleus.
  • Neutrons: Neutral particles in the nucleus.
  • Electrons: Negatively charged particles orbiting the nucleus.
  • Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons.
  • Mass Number (A): Total number of protons and neutrons.

Example 1

Determine the number of neutrons in an atom of Carbon-14.

Number of neutrons = Mass number - Atomic number = 14 - 6 = 8

  6. Describe the arrangement of electrons in the first 3 energy levels of oxygen.

  8. Determine the electron configuration of \( ^{12}C \).
  9. Explain why the atomic number determines the chemical properties of an element.
  10. Sketch the Bohr model of a hydrogen atom.
