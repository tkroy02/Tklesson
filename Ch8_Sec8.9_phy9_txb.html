Section 8.9: Review Questions (Chapter 8)

These review questions summarize the key learning points from Chapter 8: Waves and Sound. They include definitions, calculations, and applications to help reinforce your understanding.

  1. Define a wave. How is a wave different from a flow of matter?
  2. Differentiate between mechanical and electromagnetic waves. Give one example of each.
  3. What is the main difference between transverse and longitudinal waves?
  4. Define the following terms: wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and period.
  5. A wave travels at 300 m/s with a frequency of 15 Hz. Calculate its wavelength.
  6. If the period of a wave is 0.05 s, what is its frequency?
  7. How is wave speed related to distance and time? Express this relationship in equation form.
  8. State and explain the Doppler Effect in your own words.
  9. A car horn emits 500 Hz. If the car moves toward an observer at 20 m/s (speed of sound = 340 m/s), calculate the observed frequency.
  10. A train whistle of 600 Hz moves away from a stationary observer at 25 m/s. Speed of sound = 340 m/s. Find the observed frequency.
  11. Why does the pitch of an ambulance siren change as it passes by an observer?
  12. Explain how the amplitude of a sound wave relates to loudness.
  13. Explain how the frequency of a sound wave relates to pitch.
  14. What is the frequency range of human hearing?
  15. Trace the path of sound through the ear, from the outer ear to the brain.
  16. What is the role of the ossicles (malleus, incus, stapes) in the ear?
  17. How do the semicircular canals help maintain balance?
  18. Why does permanent hearing loss occur when hair cells in the cochlea are damaged?
  19. An echo is heard 3 seconds after a sound is produced near a cliff. If the speed of sound is 340 m/s, how far away is the cliff?
  20. Ultrasound waves are used in medical imaging. Explain why higher frequency waves are preferred in such applications.
