Section 7.4: Moment of Inertia and Rotational Mass

The moment of inertia is the rotational equivalent of mass. It measures how difficult it is to change an object's rotational motion.

Definition:
  • \(I = \sum m_i r_i^2\) for discrete masses
  • \(I = \int r^2 dm\) for continuous bodies
  • r = distance from rotation axis
Rotational Analogies:
  • Torque ↔ Force
  • Angular acceleration ↔ Linear acceleration
  • Moment of inertia ↔ Mass

Example 1

A uniform rod of length 2 m and mass 5 kg rotates about one end. Find its moment of inertia.

Moment of inertia: \(I = \frac{1}{3} M L^2 = \frac{1}{3} (5)(2^2) = \frac{20}{3} \approx 6.67 \text{ kg·m²}\)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the moment of inertia of a solid cylinder of mass 4 kg and radius 0.5 m about its central axis.
  2. A thin ring of radius 1 m and mass 3 kg rotates about an axis through its center perpendicular to the plane. Find its I.
  3. Compare the rotational resistance of a solid disk vs a hoop of same mass and radius.
