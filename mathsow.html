Mathematics Schemes of Work (Grades 8–11)

Algebra I (Grade 8–9) – Full Academic Year

Prerequisites: Pre-Algebra

Term 1: Foundations of Algebra (Weeks 1–13)

  • Weeks 1–2: Algebraic Thinking & Expressions – variables, constants, coefficients; simplifying expressions; substitution
  • Weeks 3–5: Linear Equations in One Variable – one-step, two-step, multi-step, word problems
  • Weeks 6–8: Inequalities – solve & graph, compound inequalities, word problems
  • Weeks 9–11: Functions & Relations – definition, notation, domain/range, tables, graphs, mapping diagrams
  • Weeks 12–13: Review & Assessment

Term 2: Linear & Nonlinear Relationships (Weeks 14–26)

  • Weeks 14–16: Graphing Linear Equations – slope, slope-intercept, tables
  • Weeks 17–19: Writing Linear Equations – point-slope, real-world data
  • Weeks 20–22: Systems of Linear Equations – graphing, substitution, elimination, applications
  • Weeks 23–24: Exponents & Exponential Functions – laws, scientific notation, growth/decay
  • Weeks 25–26: Review & Mid-Year Assessment

Term 3: Polynomials, Factoring & Quadratics (Weeks 27–40)

  • Weeks 27–29: Polynomials – operations, degree, special products
  • Weeks 30–32: Factoring – common factors, trinomials, grouping, solving
  • Weeks 33–35: Quadratics – graphing, vertex, solving, applications
  • Weeks 36–37: Radicals & Irrational Numbers – simplify, operations, rational vs irrational
  • Weeks 38–40: End-of-Year Review & Exams

Algebra II (Grade 10–11) – Full Academic Year

Prerequisites: Algebra I

Term 1: Advanced Functions & Equations (Weeks 1–13)

  • Review of Algebra I essentials
  • Quadratic functions: graphing, factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula
  • Polynomial functions: terminology, end behavior, division, Factor/Remainder Theorems
  • Complex numbers & rational expressions: operations, solving, applications

Term 2: Exponentials, Logarithms & Sequences (Weeks 14–26)

  • Radical functions & rational exponents
  • Exponential functions – growth, decay, applications
  • Logarithms – properties, equations, conversions
  • Sequences & series – arithmetic/geometric, formulas, summation, infinite series
  • Mid-Year Review & Assessment

Term 3: Advanced Topics & Applications (Weeks 27–40)

  • Probability & combinatorics – counting, permutations, combinations, binomial theorem
  • Statistics – mean, median, mode, variance, SD, histograms, normal distribution
  • Trigonometry – right triangles, radians/degrees, sine/cosine graphs
  • Advanced systems & inequalities – 3 variables, nonlinear, 2-variable inequalities
  • Matrices & linear programming – operations, determinants, solving systems, optimization
  • Final Review & Assessment

Geometry (Grade 9–10) – Full Academic Year

Prerequisites: Algebra I

Term 1: Basics, Proofs & Lines (Weeks 1–13)

  • Foundations: points, lines, planes, angles
  • Reasoning & proof: inductive/deductive, conditional statements, congruence, geometric proofs
  • Parallel/perpendicular lines, slope, equations
  • Triangles: classification, sum theorem, congruence postulates, proofs
  • Special segments: medians, altitudes, bisectors, centroid, orthocenter, incenter, circumcenter

Term 2: Polygons, Quadrilaterals & Similarity (Weeks 14–26)

  • Polygons: interior/exterior angles, diagonals, symmetry
  • Quadrilaterals: parallelograms, rhombus, rectangles, squares, trapezoids, midsegment theorem
  • Similarity: AA, SSS, SAS, proportions, scale factors, indirect measurement
  • Right triangles & trigonometry: Pythagorean theorem, special triangles, trig ratios, solving triangles
  • Mid-Year Review & Assessment

Term 3: Circles, Area, Surface Area & Volume (Weeks 27–40)

  • Circles: radius, diameter, chord, arc, sector, central/inscribed angles, circle theorems
  • Transformations: translations, rotations, reflections, dilations, compositions
  • Area of plane figures & surface area of solids
  • Volume of solids: prisms, cylinders, cones, pyramids, spheres, applications
  • End-of-Year Review & Final Assessment
