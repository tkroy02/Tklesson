Section 6.10: Chapter Test

This chapter test assesses your understanding of oscillations, SHM, energy, damping, driven oscillations, and resonance.

Chapter Test

  1. A 0.5 kg mass attached to a spring with k = 200 N/m is displaced 0.1 m from equilibrium. Find the maximum velocity.
  2. A pendulum of length 1.2 m is released from 10°. Determine its period (small-angle approximation).
  3. For a damped oscillator, describe how the amplitude changes over time for underdamping.
  4. A 2 kg mass oscillates with amplitude 0.3 m. Calculate the total energy if k = 150 N/m.
  5. Explain what happens to a driven oscillator when the driving frequency is much larger than the natural frequency.
  6. A spring-mass system oscillates with ω = 5 rad/s and amplitude 0.2 m. Find the maximum acceleration.
  7. A pendulum exhibits resonance with a driving force. Describe the effect on amplitude and energy.
  8. Compare the kinetic and potential energies at maximum displacement and equilibrium position for SHM.
  9. A damped oscillator has a damping coefficient b = 0.1 kg/s and mass m = 1 kg. Determine if it is underdamped, overdamped, or critically damped.
  10. Explain qualitatively how increasing the stiffness of the spring affects the period of oscillation.
