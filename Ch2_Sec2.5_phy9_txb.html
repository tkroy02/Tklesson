2.5 Dimensional Analysis

Dimensional analysis is a technique used in physics to check the consistency of equations and to convert between units. Every physical quantity can be expressed in terms of fundamental dimensions such as length [L], mass [M], time [T], electric current [I], temperature [Θ], etc.

Key Concept: The dimensions on both sides of a physically valid equation must be the same.

Common Dimensions of Physical Quantities

QuantitySymbolDimension
Lengthl[L]
Massm[M]
Timet[T]
Velocityv[L][T]-1
Accelerationa[L][T]-2
ForceF[M][L][T]-2
EnergyE[M][L]2[T]-2

Steps for Dimensional Analysis

  1. Identify the physical quantities in the equation.
  2. Express each quantity in terms of its fundamental dimensions.
  3. Check that the dimensions are consistent on both sides of the equation.
  4. If required, use dimensional analysis to derive a relation between quantities.
Example: Verify the dimensional correctness of the equation \( F = ma \):

Left-hand side: \( [F] = [M][L][T]^{-2} \)
Right-hand side: \( [m][a] = [M][L][T]^{-2} \)
Both sides match → Equation is dimensionally correct.

Applications of Dimensional Analysis

  • Checking equations in physics for dimensional correctness.
  • Deriving formulas when experimental relationships are known.
  • Unit conversions across different measurement systems.

Practice Problems

  1. Find the dimensions of speed \(v = d/t\).
  2. Find the dimensions of weight \(W = mg\).
  3. Find the dimensions of pressure \(P = F/A\).
  4. Use dimensional analysis to find the period of oscillation for a spring-mass system.
