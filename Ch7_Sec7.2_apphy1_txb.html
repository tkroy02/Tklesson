Section 7.2: Rotational Kinematics Equations

Rotational motion with constant angular acceleration can be described using equations analogous to linear motion:

Equations of Rotational Kinematics:
  • \(\omega = \omega_0 + \alpha t\)
  • \(\theta = \theta_0 + \omega_0 t + \frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2\)
  • \(\omega^2 = \omega_0^2 + 2\alpha (\theta - \theta_0)\)
Variables:
  • \(\theta\): angular displacement
  • \(\omega\): angular velocity
  • \(\omega_0\): initial angular velocity
  • \(\alpha\): angular acceleration
  • t: time

Example 1

A wheel starts from rest and accelerates at 3 rad/s² for 4 s. Find the final angular velocity and angular displacement.

\(\omega = \omega_0 + \alpha t = 0 + 3(4) = 12 \text{ rad/s}\)

\(\theta = \theta_0 + \omega_0 t + \frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2 = 0 + 0 + \frac{1}{2} (3)(16) = 24 \text{ rad}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A disc rotates at 5 rad/s and accelerates at 2 rad/s² for 6 s. Find final angular velocity and displacement.
  2. Wheel has initial angular velocity 10 rad/s, angular acceleration -2 rad/s². Find time to stop.
  3. A fan rotates through 15 rad in 3 s from rest. Find angular acceleration assuming constant acceleration.
