2.8 Time

Time is a fundamental quantity that measures the interval between events. In physics, the SI unit of time is the second (s).

Key Concept: Accurate measurement of time is crucial in experiments, motion studies, and periodic phenomena.

Measuring Time

  • Stopwatch: For events lasting from milliseconds to hours, precision ±0.01 s to ±0.1 s.
  • Clock: For daily activities, precision ±1 s.
  • Atomic clock: Extremely precise, used in scientific research, precision ~10⁻⁹ s.

Time Conversions

Some common conversions:

  • 1 minute = 60 seconds
  • 1 hour = 60 minutes = 3600 seconds
  • 1 day = 24 hours = 86,400 seconds
  • 1 week = 7 days = 604,800 seconds

Temperature

Temperature measures the degree of hotness or coldness of a body. The SI unit is kelvin (K), although Celsius (°C) is commonly used in everyday life.

Key Concept: Temperature affects the physical properties of substances, such as volume, density, and pressure.

Temperature Scales

  • Celsius (°C): Water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C at 1 atm.
  • Kelvin (K): Absolute temperature scale. 0 K = absolute zero. Relation: \( T(K) = T(°C) + 273.15 \).
  • Fahrenheit (°F): Common in the US. Relation: \( T(°F) = \frac{9}{5} T(°C) + 32 \).

Absolute Zero: The Lowest Temperature

Absolute zero is the theoretical lowest possible temperature. At this temperature, particles have minimal motion, meaning they possess almost no kinetic energy. It is defined as 0 K (Kelvin), which equals −273.15 °C.

Key Concept: Nothing can get colder than absolute zero, because particles cannot have less than zero motion. Scientists can get extremely close, but never reach it exactly.

Measuring Temperature

  • Mercury or alcohol thermometer: ±0.5°C accuracy.
  • Digital thermometer: ±0.1°C to ±0.5°C accuracy.
  • Thermocouples and resistance thermometers: Used in laboratories and industrial processes, high precision.

Practice: Time Conversions

  1. Convert 4 days to hours.
  2. Convert 50,000 seconds to hours.
  3. Convert 1 year to hours (assume 1 year = 365 days).
