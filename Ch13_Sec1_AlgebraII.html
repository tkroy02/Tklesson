Section 13.1: Introduction to Matrices

This section introduces matrices, their notation, types, and basic applications in algebra. We will learn how to represent systems of equations and organize data using matrices.

Example 1: Writing a Matrix

Write the following system as a matrix:

\[ \begin{cases} 2x + 3y = 5 \\ 4x - y = 1 \end{cases} \]

Step 1: Extract the coefficients into a matrix:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 4 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 2: Represent constants as a separate column matrix:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Example 2: Types of Matrices

Identify the type of each matrix:

  • \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)
  • \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 5 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\)

Step 1: First matrix is a 2x2 identity matrix.

Step 2: Second matrix is a 1x3 row matrix.

Practice Problems

  1. Write the system \(x + 2y = 7, 3x - y = 4\) as a matrix.
  2. Identify the type of matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 & 6 \\ 1 & 3 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\)
  3. Construct a 3x3 zero matrix.
  4. Determine whether \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\) is square, row, or column.
  5. Write a 2x2 diagonal matrix with entries 5 and -2.
