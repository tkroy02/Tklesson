Section 13.5: Linear Programming & Optimization

This section covers setting up linear programming problems, graphing feasible regions, identifying constraints, and solving optimization problems.

Example 1: Maximizing Profit

A company produces two products, A and B. Each unit of A gives $30 profit, each unit of B gives $20 profit. Constraints: 4A + 2B ≤ 40 (hours), A + 2B ≤ 20 (materials). Determine the number of units of A and B to maximize profit.

Step 1: Graph constraints and identify feasible region.

Step 2: Evaluate profit at each vertex of feasible region: (0,0), (0,10), (8,6), (10,0)

Step 3: Profit at vertices: P(0,0)=0, P(0,10)=200, P(8,6)=360, P(10,0)=300

Step 4: Maximum profit is $360 at (8,6). Produce 8 units of A and 6 units of B.

Example 2: Minimizing Cost

A company must purchase at least 100 units of material X and 80 units of material Y. Suppliers A and B have costs and limits: Supplier A: $2X + $3Y ≤ $300, Supplier B: $4X + $2Y ≤ $400. Find the combination of purchases minimizing cost while meeting requirements.

Step 1: Graph feasible region according to constraints.

Step 2: Check corner points: (X,Y) = (50,50), (0,100), (75,50), etc.

Step 3: Evaluate cost at each point and select minimum.

Step 4: Minimum cost occurs at optimal combination satisfying constraints.

Practice Problems

  1. A bakery makes cakes and cookies. Each cake yields $5 profit, each cookie $2. Constraints: 2C + 1K ≤ 16 (hours), C + 2K ≤ 12 (ingredients). Maximize profit.
  2. A factory produces chairs and tables. Chair costs $15, table $40. Constraints: 3Ch + 5T ≤ 120 (labor), 2Ch + 4T ≤ 100 (materials). Find minimum production cost.
  3. Farm uses fertilizer types A and B. Constraints: 3A + 2B ≤ 60, A + 4B ≤ 40. Maximize crop yield.
  4. Company shipping: boxes X and Y. Constraint: 2X + 3Y ≤ 24, X + Y ≤ 10. Maximize revenue.
  5. Investment problem: two portfolios P1 and P2, constraints on total investment and risk. Optimize return.
