Section 6.4: Variables of Circular Motion

Circular motion can be described using several key variables including period, frequency, angular velocity, angular acceleration, and time. These variables allow us to calculate speed, rotations, and the effects of forces on the object.

Key Concept: Understanding these variables is crucial for analyzing any system in circular motion.

Period (T)

Time for one complete revolution. Formula: \( T = \frac{\text{time}}{\text{number of revolutions}} \)

Example: If a wheel makes 10 revolutions in 5 s, \( T = 5/10 = 0.5 \) s per revolution.

Frequency (f)

Number of revolutions per second. Formula: \( f = 1/T \) Hz

Example: Using T = 0.5 s, \( f = 1/0.5 = 2 \) Hz

Angular Velocity (ω)

Rate of rotation in radians per second: \( \omega = 2\pi f = \frac{2\pi}{T} \) rad/s

Conversion to revolutions per minute (rev/min): \( \text{rev/min} = f \times 60 \)

Example: For f = 2 Hz, ω = 2π × 2 = 12.57 rad/s, rev/min = 2 × 60 = 120 rev/min

Angular Velocity: Converting rev/min to rad/s

To convert from revolutions per minute (rev/min) to radians per second (rad/s):

\( \omega \, [\text{rad/s}] = \text{rev/min} \times \frac{2\pi}{60} \)

Example 1: 120 rev/min → ω = 120 × 2π/60 = 4π ≈ 12.57 rad/s
Example 2: 300 rev/min → ω = 300 × 2π/60 = 10π ≈ 31.42 rad/s
Example 3: 75 rev/min → ω = 75 × 2π/60 = 2.5π ≈ 7.85 rad/s
Example 4: 500 rev/min → ω = 500 × 2π/60 ≈ 52.36 rad/s

Angular Acceleration (α)

Rate of change of angular velocity: \( \alpha = \frac{\Delta \omega}{\Delta t} \)

Example: If ω changes from 5 rad/s to 15 rad/s in 2 s, α = (15-5)/2 = 5 rad/s²

Time Calculations

Time can be calculated from period and number of revolutions: \( t = N \cdot T \)

Example: 20 revolutions with T = 0.5 s gives t = 20 × 0.5 = 10 s
