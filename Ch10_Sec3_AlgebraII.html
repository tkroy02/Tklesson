Section 10.3: Standard Deviation & Normal Distribution

This section introduces standard deviation as a measure of data spread and the characteristics of the normal distribution.

Example 1: Standard Deviation

Find the standard deviation of the data: 2, 4, 4, 4, 5, 5, 7, 9.

Step 1: Compute mean: (2+4+4+4+5+5+7+9)/8 = 5

Step 2: Compute squared differences: (2-5)²=9, (4-5)²=1, (4-5)²=1, ..., (9-5)²=16

Step 3: Sum squared differences: 9+1+1+1+0+0+4+16=32

Step 4: Divide by n (population) or n-1 (sample). Using sample: 32/7 ≈ 4.571

Step 5: Take square root: √4.571 ≈ 2.14

Standard deviation ≈ 2.14

Example 2: Normal Distribution

For a dataset with mean μ=50 and standard deviation σ=5, determine the range covering approximately 68% of data.

Step 1: Recall 68-95-99.7 rule

Step 2: 68% of data lies within ±1σ of mean: 50-5 to 50+5

Range: 45 to 55
