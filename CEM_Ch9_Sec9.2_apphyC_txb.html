Section 9.2: Lenz’s Law

Lenz’s Law states that the direction of the induced current in a conductor due to a changing magnetic flux is such that it opposes the change that produced it. This law is a direct consequence of the conservation of energy.

Lenz’s Law Formula:

\[ \mathcal{E} = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \]

The negative sign indicates that the induced EMF generates a current whose magnetic field opposes the change in original flux.

Physical Meaning:
  • If magnetic flux increases, induced current produces a field opposing the increase.
  • If magnetic flux decreases, induced current produces a field trying to maintain it.
  • Ensures **energy conservation**; you cannot get energy for free.

Example 1

A bar magnet is pushed into a coil connected to a resistor. Explain the direction of induced current using Lenz’s Law.

As the magnet enters, the magnetic flux through the coil increases. Lenz’s Law states the induced current will create a magnetic field opposing this increase. Hence, the current flows in a direction that produces a north pole facing the approaching magnet, opposing its motion.

Practice Problems

  1. A magnet is pulled out of a coil. Determine the direction of induced current and explain using Lenz’s Law.
  2. Describe how Lenz’s Law ensures energy conservation in an electromagnetic induction experiment.
  3. A conducting loop has a decreasing magnetic flux. Draw the induced current and field directions.
  4. A coil of 50 turns experiences a flux increase of 0.2 Wb in 0.1 s. Calculate the induced EMF and state its polarity.
  5. Explain why it is harder to push a magnet into a coil connected to a circuit than into a disconnected coil.
