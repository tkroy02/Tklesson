Section 7.10: Unit Test

This unit test covers all topics from rotational motion including angular kinematics, torque, moment of inertia, work & energy, angular momentum, and gyroscopic precession.

Unit Test Questions

  1. A solid disc of radius 0.5 m and mass 2 kg is subjected to a torque of 4 N·m. Find its angular acceleration.
  2. Wheel with moment of inertia 5 kg·m² spins with angular velocity 10 rad/s. Calculate rotational kinetic energy.
  3. A gyroscope has angular momentum 60 kg·m²/s and is subjected to a torque of 12 N·m. Find its precession rate.
  4. A solid sphere of mass 3 kg and radius 0.4 m rolls down an incline of 2 m height. Calculate final angular velocity.
  5. A flywheel initially at rest experiences torque 10 N·m for 5 s. Determine angular velocity after 5 s if I = 2 kg·m².
  6. Compute angular momentum of a 1.5 kg wheel of radius 0.3 m spinning at 20 rad/s.
  7. A torque of 15 N·m acts on a rod pivoted at one end. Moment of inertia is 2.5 kg·m². Find angular acceleration.
  8. A flywheel’s kinetic energy is 200 J with I = 4 kg·m². Find its angular speed.
  9. Wheel with I = 3 kg·m² and ω = 12 rad/s is slowed by torque 6 N·m. Find time to stop.
  10. A gyroscope precesses at 0.3 rad/s under torque 9 N·m. Find its angular momentum.
