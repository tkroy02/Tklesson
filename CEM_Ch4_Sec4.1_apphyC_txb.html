Section 4.1: Conductors in Electrostatics

A conductor is a material in which charges (usually electrons) are free to move. In electrostatic equilibrium, conductors have properties that simplify many problems in electricity and magnetism.

Key Properties of Conductors in Electrostatics:
  • Electric field inside a conductor is zero.
  • Excess charge resides on the surface of the conductor.
  • Electric field at the surface is perpendicular to the surface.
  • The surface of a conductor is an equipotential surface.

Example 1

A solid conducting sphere of radius \(R\) carries a net charge \(Q\). Determine the electric field inside and outside the conductor.

Inside (\(r < R\)): In electrostatic equilibrium, \(E = 0\).

Outside (\(r \ge R\)): Treat the charge as if concentrated at the center:

\[ E = \frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_0} \frac{Q}{r^2} \]
