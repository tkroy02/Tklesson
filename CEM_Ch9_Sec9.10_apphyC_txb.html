Section 9.10: Practice Problems

This section provides a set of practice problems covering all topics of Chapter 9: Electromagnetic Induction & AC Circuits. Attempt these problems to strengthen understanding of Faraday’s Law, Lenz’s Law, Inductance, AC circuits, resonance, impedance, and power in AC circuits.

Problems

  1. A circular loop of radius 0.1 m is placed in a magnetic field perpendicular to its plane. The field decreases from 0.5 T to 0.1 T in 0.02 s. Find the induced EMF.
  2. A coil of 200 turns and area 0.05 m² is placed in a uniform magnetic field of 0.8 T. If the coil is rotated 90° in 0.01 s, calculate the average induced EMF.
  3. A series RL circuit has R = 10 Ω and L = 0.2 H. Determine the time constant and the current after 0.05 s if a 12 V DC source is applied.
  4. A series RLC circuit has R = 5 Ω, L = 0.1 H, and C = 50 μF. Find the resonant frequency and maximum current if V = 10 V.
  5. An AC circuit has V_rms = 120 V, I_rms = 5 A, and phase angle φ = 45°. Calculate average, apparent, and reactive power.
  6. Explain the effect of frequency on the impedance of a series RLC circuit and sketch the current vs. frequency curve.
  7. For a parallel RLC circuit, determine the condition for resonance and explain how impedance behaves at resonance.
  8. A coil has an inductance of 0.3 H and a resistor of 20 Ω in series. If a 6 V AC source with f = 60 Hz is applied, calculate the impedance, current, and phase angle.
  9. Describe Lenz’s Law and provide an example of its application in an induced current problem.
  10. Compute the power factor of an AC circuit with V_rms = 240 V, I_rms = 8 A, and average power P_avg = 1200 W. Explain its significance.
