Section 7.10: Chapter 7 Test

Choose the correct answer for each question below:

  1. What is the temperature in Kelvin for 25°C?
    • A) \(248.15 \, \mathrm{K}\)
    • B) \(298.15 \, \mathrm{K}\)
    • C) \(273.15 \, \mathrm{K}\)
    • D) \(300.15 \, \mathrm{K}\)
  2. A 0.2 kg block of ice melts at 0°C. If \(L_f = 334{,}000 \, \mathrm{J/kg}\), how much heat is absorbed?
    • A) \(66{,}800 \, \mathrm{J}\)
    • B) \(16{,}700 \, \mathrm{J}\)
    • C) \(33{,}400 \, \mathrm{J}\)
    • D) \(334{,}000 \, \mathrm{J}\)
  3. Which mode of heat transfer does not require a medium?
    • A) Conduction
    • B) Convection
    • C) Radiation
    • D) All of the above
  4. For a solid, the coefficient of volume expansion (\(\gamma\)) is related to the linear expansion coefficient (\(\alpha\)) as:
    • A) \(\gamma = \alpha\)
    • B) \(\gamma = 2 \alpha\)
    • C) \(\gamma = 3 \alpha\)
    • D) \(\gamma = 4 \alpha\)
  5. A 2 m aluminum rod (\(\alpha = 2.3 \times 10^{-5} \, \mathrm{K^{-1}}\)) is heated by 50 K. Find the change in length (\(\Delta L\)).
    • A) 1.15 mm
    • B) 2.3 mm
    • C) 0.23 mm
    • D) 0.115 mm
  6. Which of the following is an example of convection?
    • A) Heat spreading along a metal rod
    • B) Warm air rising from a heater
    • C) Feeling heat from the Sun
    • D) Melting ice
  7. The first law of thermodynamics is expressed as:
    • A) \(Q = mc\Delta T\)
    • B) \(\Delta U = Q - W\)
    • C) \(P V = n R T\)
    • D) \(\eta = \frac{W}{Q_H}\)
  8. A heat engine absorbs \(Q_H = 1000 \, \mathrm{J}\) and expels \(Q_C = 400 \, \mathrm{J}\). Its efficiency (\(\eta\)) is:
    • A) 60%
    • B) 40%
    • C) 100%
    • D) 25%
  9. Which of the following statements is true according to kinetic theory of gases?
    • A) Gas particles have negligible volume but finite mass
    • B) Gas pressure is due to particle collisions with walls
    • C) Increasing temperature increases average kinetic energy of particles
    • D) All of the above
  10. Specific heat capacity (\(c\)) is defined as:
    • A) Heat required to change temperature of 1 kg of substance by 1°C
    • B) Heat required to melt 1 kg of substance
    • C) Heat required to boil 1 kg of substance
    • D) Total heat of a body
