Section 8.9: Review Examples

This section consolidates concepts from Fluid Mechanics, including laminar and turbulent flow, Bernoulli’s principle, viscosity, and pressure in fluids. Work through these examples to strengthen understanding.

Example 1

Water flows through a horizontal pipe narrowing from 0.1 m to 0.05 m diameter. If velocity in the wider section is 2 m/s, find the velocity in the narrower section assuming incompressible flow.

Continuity: \(A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2\)

\(\pi (0.1/2)^2 * 2 = \pi (0.05/2)^2 * v_2\)

\(v_2 = 8 \text{ m/s}\)

Example 2

Air flows in a 0.2 m diameter duct at 3 m/s. Determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. Air density 1.2 kg/m³, viscosity 1.8×10⁻⁵ Pa·s.

\(Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\eta} = \frac{1.2*3*0.2}{1.8e-5} \approx 40,000\)

Flow is turbulent

Example 3

A fluid in a pipe rises 0.5 m at the narrow section. Using Bernoulli’s equation, determine pressure difference if fluid density is 1000 kg/m³.

\(\Delta P = \rho g h = 1000*9.8*0.5 = 4900 \text{ Pa}\)

Practice Problems

  1. Water flows in a horizontal pipe with diameters 0.1 m and 0.05 m. Velocity at 0.1 m section is 1.5 m/s. Find velocity at narrow section.
  2. Air in a duct of 0.25 m diameter has velocity 2 m/s. Determine Reynolds number and flow type. Density = 1.2 kg/m³, viscosity = 1.8×10⁻⁵ Pa·s.
  3. Fluid rises 0.3 m in a vertical tube. Find pressure difference using Bernoulli’s principle. Density = 1000 kg/m³.
  4. Explain the effect of viscosity on laminar flow.
  5. Calculate Reynolds number for honey flowing at 0.05 m/s in 0.01 m pipe. Density = 1400 kg/m³, viscosity = 10 Pa·s.
  6. Describe differences between laminar and turbulent flow.
  7. Determine flow type for water in a pipe 0.05 m diameter with velocity 0.5 m/s.
  8. Explain why turbulent flow enhances mixing.
  9. Apply Bernoulli’s equation to determine pressure in a narrowing pipe.
  10. Compute velocity in narrow section if area reduces by factor of 4 and initial velocity is 2 m/s.
