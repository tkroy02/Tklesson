Section 7.6: Coupled Oscillations

Coupled oscillations occur when two or more oscillating systems influence each other. Energy can be transferred between systems, leading to phenomena like beat frequencies and normal modes.

Key Concepts:
  • Normal Modes: Characteristic patterns where all parts oscillate with same frequency.
  • Beat Frequency: \( f_{beat} = |f_1 - f_2| \) observed when two close frequencies interfere.
  • Energy Transfer: Periodic exchange of energy between coupled systems.

Example 1

Two identical pendulums of length 1 m are coupled by a light spring. If one is displaced and released, describe the energy transfer between the pendulums.

The displaced pendulum begins oscillating and gradually transfers energy to the second pendulum through the spring. After a full period of energy exchange, the first pendulum comes to rest while the second oscillates with maximum amplitude. This cycle repeats, illustrating beats and normal modes.

Practice Problems

  1. Two identical coupled oscillators have frequencies 5 Hz and 5.2 Hz. Find the beat frequency.
  2. Describe the normal modes for two pendulums coupled by a spring.
  3. Calculate energy transfer period for two coupled oscillators with frequencies 10 Hz and 10.5 Hz.
  4. A pair of coupled pendulums has total energy 2 J. Determine energy in each pendulum at maximum amplitude of first normal mode.
  5. Explain why energy is conserved in coupled oscillations despite individual oscillator amplitudes changing.
  6. Two coupled LC circuits: explain how oscillations transfer between inductors.
  7. Sketch displacement vs time graph for coupled pendulums showing beats.
  8. How does coupling strength affect the beat frequency?
  9. Compute amplitude of second oscillator after half beat period given initial energy in first oscillator.
  10. Discuss applications of coupled oscillations in musical instruments or clocks.
