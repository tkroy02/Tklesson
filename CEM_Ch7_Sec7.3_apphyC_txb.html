Section 7.3: Torque on Current Loop

A current-carrying loop placed in a magnetic field experiences a torque which tends to rotate it. This is a fundamental principle behind electric motors.

Torque on a Rectangular Loop:

\[ \tau = n I A B \sin\theta \]

where \(n\) is the number of turns, \(I\) is current, \(A\) is loop area, \(B\) is magnetic field strength, and \(\theta\) is the angle between loop normal and field.

Magnetic Dipole Moment:

\[ \vec{\mu} = n I \vec{A} \]

Torque can also be expressed as \(\vec{\tau} = \vec{\mu} \times \vec{B}\).

Potential Energy:

\[ U = -\vec{\mu} \cdot \vec{B} = -\mu B \cos\theta \]

Minimum energy occurs when the dipole aligns with the magnetic field.

Example 1

A single rectangular loop of area \(0.02\,\text{m}^2\) carrying a current of \(5\,\text{A}\) is placed in a uniform magnetic field \(B = 0.3\,\text{T}\). If the plane of the loop makes an angle of \(30^\circ\) with the field, find the torque on the loop.

\[ \tau = n I A B \sin\theta = 1 \times 5 \times 0.02 \times 0.3 \times \sin(30^\circ) \]

\[ \tau = 0.015\,\text{N·m} \]

Practice Problems

  1. A circular loop of radius 0.1 m carries a current of 2 A in a 0.5 T uniform field. Calculate the maximum torque.
  2. Explain why the torque is zero when the plane of the loop is parallel to the field.
  3. For a 3-turn rectangular coil with current 4 A and area 0.01 m² in a 0.2 T field at 60°, calculate torque.
  4. Derive the expression for potential energy of a magnetic dipole in a uniform magnetic field.
  5. Discuss the physical meaning of magnetic dipole moment in terms of torque and energy.
