Section 8.4: Fluid Statics Applications

Fluid statics deals with fluids at rest. Key applications include pressure in fluids, forces on submerged surfaces, and design considerations for dams and containers.

Pressure at depth: \[ P = P_0 + \rho g h \] Where \(P_0\) is surface pressure, \(\rho\) fluid density, \(g\) acceleration due to gravity, and \(h\) depth.
Force on a submerged vertical surface: \[ F = \rho g A \bar{h} \] Where \(A\) is the area of the surface, \(\bar{h}\) is depth of center of pressure.

Example 1

A rectangular dam 5 m wide and 10 m deep. Calculate total hydrostatic force on the dam.

Force: \( F = \rho g A \bar{h} = 1000*9.8*50*(10/2) = 2,450,000 \, \text{N} \)

Example 2

Pressure at 15 m depth in a lake (density = 1000 kg/m³).

Pressure: \( P = P_0 + \rho g h = 101,325 + 1000*9.8*15 \approx 247,325 \, \text{Pa} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Find pressure 20 m below water surface.
  2. A 2 m² plate submerged 5 m deep. Find force on plate.
  3. Hydraulic lift with piston areas 0.1 m² and 0.02 m². Find output force for 500 N input.
  4. Water in U-tube, left side 10 m, right side 12 m. Find pressure at bottom.
  5. A dam 8 m wide and 15 m high. Total hydrostatic force?
  6. Force on vertical wall 3 m² at 6 m depth.
  7. Calculate gauge pressure at 50 m depth in sea water (\(\rho=1025\) kg/m³).
  8. Determine depth where pressure is double atmospheric.
  9. Design a container: max pressure at base 200 kPa, fluid density 850 kg/m³. Find height.
  10. Water exerts 5000 N on a plate 2 m². Find depth of center of pressure.
