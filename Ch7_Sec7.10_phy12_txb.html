Section 7.10: Chapter 7 Test

This test covers all key topics of Quantum Physics, including quantum theory, wave-particle duality, energy levels, and the Schrödinger equation. Attempt all questions and use previous sections as reference.

Test Questions

  1. Explain the concept of wave-particle duality with an example.
  2. Calculate the energy of a photon emitted when an electron transitions from n=3 to n=1 in a hydrogen-like atom.
  3. Describe the photoelectric effect and derive the equation for kinetic energy of emitted electrons.
  4. Compute the first three energy levels of a particle in a 1D infinite potential well of length 2 nm.
  5. Normalize the wavefunction \( \psi(x) = A \sin(\pi x/L) \) for a particle in a box of length L.
  6. Determine the uncertainty in position and momentum for a particle in the ground state of a 1D box.
  7. Use the Schrödinger equation to write the general form of stationary state solutions for a particle in a 1D box.
  8. Discuss the significance of quantum numbers in describing electron states.
  9. Sketch and explain the probability density for n=2 and n=3 in a box of length L.
  10. Analyze a particle encountering a finite potential barrier and calculate reflection probability conceptually.
