Section 5.7: Parallel Resistor Circuits

In a parallel circuit, resistors are connected across the same two nodes, so each resistor experiences the same voltage. The total current is the sum of currents through each resistor:

\[ \frac{1}{R_{total}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \frac{1}{R_3} + \dots \]

The current through each resistor is:

\[ I_n = \frac{V}{R_n} \]

Key Points:
  • Voltage is the same across all resistors.
  • Total current is the sum of individual currents.
  • Removing one resistor does not break the circuit.

Example 1

Three resistors, 2 Ω, 3 Ω, and 6 Ω, are connected in parallel to a 12 V battery. Find total resistance and current through each resistor.

Total resistance: \( 1/R_{total} = 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = 1 \, \Omega \)

Total current: \( I_{total} = V / R_{total} = 12 / 1 = 12 A \)

Individual currents: \( I_1 = 12/2 = 6 A, I_2 = 12/3 = 4 A, I_3 = 12/6 = 2 A \)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate total resistance and currents for a parallel circuit with resistors 4 Ω, 6 Ω, and 12 Ω across a 24 V battery.
  2. Explain what happens to total resistance if one resistor is removed.
  3. Find the power dissipated in each resistor from Example 1.
  4. Compare series and parallel circuits in terms of voltage, current, and failure behavior.
  5. Derive the formula for total resistance for N resistors in parallel.
