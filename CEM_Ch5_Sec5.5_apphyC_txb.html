Section 5.5: Work & Energy in Circuits

Electrical work is the energy transferred by the electric force in moving charges through a potential difference.

The work done on a charge \( q \) moving across a potential difference \( V \) is:

\[ W = qV \]

For a circuit carrying current \( I \) over time \( t \):

\[ W = P t = VI t \]

Key Points:
  • Energy can be stored in capacitors as \( U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 \).
  • Resistors dissipate energy as heat: \( W = I^2 R t \).
  • Work and energy calculations are essential for power management.

Example 1

A 12 V battery drives 2 A for 3 minutes through a resistor. Calculate the energy delivered.

\[ W = VI t = 12 \times 2 \times (3 \times 60) = 4320 \, \text{J} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the work done in a 5 Î© resistor with 3 A current over 10 s.
  2. A capacitor stores 0.5 J at 5 V. Find its capacitance.
  3. If a 60 W bulb operates for 2 hours, how much energy is consumed?
  4. Compare energy dissipation in series vs parallel resistor circuits.
  5. Derive \( W = P t = VI t \) starting from basic definitions.
