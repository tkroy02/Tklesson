Section 10.7: Energy & Poynting Vector

This section explains how energy is carried by electromagnetic fields and introduces the Poynting vector.

Electromagnetic Energy Density:

\[ u = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 E^2 + \frac{1}{2\mu_0} B^2 \]

Poynting Vector:

\[ \vec{S} = \frac{1}{\mu_0} \vec{E} \times \vec{B} \] Represents the energy flux per unit area of an EM wave.

Example Problem

Calculate the magnitude of the Poynting vector for an EM wave with electric field amplitude \(E_0 = 100\,\mathrm{V/m}\) in free space.

\[ S = \frac{1}{\mu_0} E B = \frac{1}{\mu_0} E \frac{E}{c} = \frac{E^2}{\mu_0 c} = \epsilon_0 c E^2 \]

Plug in numbers: \( \epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \), \( c = 3 \times 10^8 \), \( E = 100 \)

\[ S \approx 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 3 \times 10^8 \cdot 10^4 \approx 26.55\,\mathrm{W/m^2} \]
