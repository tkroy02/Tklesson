Section 10.7: Energy & Poynting Vector

This section introduces energy density in electromagnetic fields and the Poynting vector, which represents the energy flux of electromagnetic waves.

Energy Density of Fields:

Electric field: \( u_E = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 E^2 \)

Magnetic field: \( u_B = \frac{1}{2\mu_0} B^2 \)

Total energy density: \( u = u_E + u_B \)

Poynting Vector:

\( \mathbf{S} = \frac{1}{\mu_0} \mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B} \)

Represents energy flux (power per unit area) of electromagnetic waves. Direction is the direction of wave propagation.

Average Intensity of a Wave:

\( \langle S \rangle = \frac{c \epsilon_0 E_0^2}{2} = \frac{E_0 B_0}{2 \mu_0} \)

Useful for calculating power carried by plane waves.

Example 1

An EM wave has \( E_0 = 200 \, \text{V/m} \). Find its energy density and average intensity.

Energy density: \( u = \epsilon_0 E_0^2 = (8.85\times10^{-12})(200)^2 \approx 3.54\times10^{-7} \, \text{J/m}^3 \)

Average intensity: \( \langle S \rangle = c \epsilon_0 E_0^2/2 \approx 1.06 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{W/m}^2 \)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the energy density of an EM wave with \( E_0 = 100 \, \text{V/m} \).
  2. Compute the Poynting vector for a plane wave traveling in the z-direction.
  3. Show that the energy in electric and magnetic fields are equal in a plane wave.
  4. Given B field amplitude, find the corresponding E field and intensity.
  5. Derive the relation between average intensity and RMS electric field.
