Section 7.3: Orbits

This section examines the motion of objects in orbit, including circular and elliptical orbits, and the forces involved.

Orbital Velocity (Circular Orbit):

\( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} \)

  • \( M \): Mass of the central object (e.g., planet, star)
  • \( r \): Radius of the orbit from the center of the mass
  • \( G \): Universal gravitational constant
Orbital Period (Circular Orbit):

\( T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{r^3}{G M}} \)

Example 1

Find the orbital velocity of a satellite orbiting 7000 km from the center of Earth (mass = \(5.97 \times 10^{24}\) kg).

\( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 5.97 \times 10^{24}}{7 \times 10^6}} \approx 7.35 \times 10^3 \, \text{m/s} \)

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate orbital speed of a satellite 300 km above Earth’s surface.
  2. Find the orbital period of a satellite orbiting 8000 km from Earth’s center.
  3. A planet orbits a star at \(1 \times 10^{11}\) m. Compute orbital speed.
  4. Compare speeds of two satellites at different orbital radii: 5000 km and 10000 km.
  5. Find orbital speed of a moon around a planet of mass \(1 \times 10^{24}\) kg at 2 × 10^7 m distance.
