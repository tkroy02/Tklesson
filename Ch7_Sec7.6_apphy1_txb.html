Section 7.6: Rotational Work and Energy

Work done by a torque on a rotating object changes its rotational kinetic energy:

Rotational Work:
  • \(W = \tau \theta\)
  • \(\tau\) = torque applied
  • \(\theta\) = angular displacement (radians)
Rotational Kinetic Energy:
  • \(K_{rot} = \frac{1}{2} I \omega^2\)
  • Work-energy principle: \(W_{net} = \Delta K_{rot}\)

Example 1

A wheel with I = 2 kg·m² is rotated by a torque of 6 N·m through 3 radians. Find the work done and angular acceleration if it starts from rest.

Work done: \(W = \tau \theta = 6 \cdot 3 = 18 \text{ J}\)

Rotational kinetic energy after 3 rad: \(K_{rot} = W = 18 \text{ J}\)

Angular acceleration: \(\alpha = \tau/I = 6/2 = 3 \text{ rad/s²}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A disk of I = 1.5 kg·m² is acted on by a torque of 4 N·m for 5 rad. Find work done and final \(\omega\).
  2. A rotating rod of I = 0.8 kg·m² has 3 N·m torque applied for 2 rad. Compute final rotational kinetic energy.
  3. Compare work done if same torque rotates a heavier vs lighter wheel through same angle.
