Section 10.3: Faraday’s Law (Integral/Differential)

Faraday’s Law describes how a time-varying magnetic field induces an electromotive force (EMF) in a closed loop.

Integral Form:

\[ \mathcal{E} = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} = -\frac{d}{dt} \oint_S \mathbf{B} \cdot d\mathbf{A} \]

The induced EMF equals the negative rate of change of magnetic flux through surface S.

Differential Form (Maxwell-Faraday):

\[ \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = - \frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \]

Shows that a time-varying magnetic field produces a circulating electric field.

Lenz’s Law:

The negative sign indicates that the induced EMF generates a current whose magnetic field opposes the change in flux.

Example 1

A circular loop of radius 0.1 m is in a magnetic field that changes uniformly from 0.5 T to 0 T in 0.05 s. Find the induced EMF.

Magnetic flux: \( \Phi_B = B \cdot A = B \cdot \pi r^2 \)

Change in flux: \( \Delta \Phi_B = 0.5 \cdot \pi (0.1)^2 \approx 0.0157 \, \text{Wb} \)

Induced EMF: \( \mathcal{E} = - \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t} = - \frac{0.0157}{0.05} \approx -0.314 \, \text{V} \)

Magnitude: 0.314 V; direction opposes decreasing flux (Lenz’s Law).

Practice Problems

  1. A rectangular loop (0.2 m × 0.1 m) is in a magnetic field changing at 0.1 T/s. Calculate the induced EMF.
  2. A solenoid of 500 turns and area 0.01 m² has a time-varying magnetic field B(t) = 0.05t (T). Find EMF at t = 2 s.
  3. Explain the physical meaning of the negative sign in Faraday’s Law.
  4. Use the differential form to show the induced electric field in a circular loop due to a linearly increasing magnetic field.
  5. A metal ring of radius 0.05 m has a magnetic flux decreasing at 0.2 Wb/s. Determine the induced EMF and current if R = 2 Ω.
