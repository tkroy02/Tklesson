Section 10.3: Faraday’s Law (Integral/Differential)

Faraday’s Law of Electromagnetic Induction relates a changing magnetic flux through a surface to the induced electromotive force (EMF) in a loop enclosing that surface.

Integral Form:

\[ \mathcal{E} = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \quad , \quad \Phi_B = \int_S \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} \]

Differential Form:

\[ \nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t} \]

Example

A circular loop of radius \( r \) is placed in a uniform magnetic field increasing at a rate \( dB/dt \). Find the induced EMF.

Magnetic flux through loop:

\[ \Phi_B = B \cdot \pi r^2 \]

EMF induced:

\[ \mathcal{E} = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} = - \pi r^2 \frac{dB}{dt} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Explain Lenz’s Law in your own words.
  2. Calculate induced EMF for a rectangular loop in a changing magnetic field.
  3. Convert the integral form of Faraday’s Law to differential form for a region of space.
