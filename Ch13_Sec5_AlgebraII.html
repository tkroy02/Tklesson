Section 13.5: Linear Programming & Optimization

This section explores linear programming techniques for optimizing a linear objective function subject to constraints. Focus is on graphical solutions and applications in business and economics.

Example 1: Maximize Profit

A company produces two products, P1 and P2. Profit per unit: $5 for P1, $8 for P2. Constraints:
\( 2P1 + 3P2 \le 60 \) (raw material), \( P1 + P2 \le 20 \) (labor hours), \( P1, P2 \ge 0 \). Determine the production to maximize profit.

Step 1: Graph feasible region using constraints.

Step 2: Identify corner points: (0,0), (0,20), (12,8), (30,0)

Step 3: Compute profit at each corner:
(0,0) → 0, (0,20) → 160, (12,8) → 5*12 + 8*8 = 60 + 64 = 124, (30,0) → 150

Step 4: Maximum profit is $160 at (0,20)

Example 2: Minimization Problem

Minimize cost C = 3x + 4y subject to:
\( x + 2y \ge 8 \), \( 3x + y \ge 9 \), \( x, y \ge 0 \).

Step 1: Graph inequalities to find feasible region.

Step 2: Identify corner points: (0,4), (3,3), (9/3,0) → (3,3)

Step 3: Evaluate cost: C = 3*0 + 4*4 = 16, C = 3*3 + 4*3 = 21, C = 3*3 + 4*0 = 9

Step 4: Minimum cost is $9 at (3,0)
