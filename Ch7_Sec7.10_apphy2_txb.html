Section 7.10: Unit 7 Test

This test covers all topics from Unit 7: Quantum, Atomic & Nuclear Physics. Answer all questions carefully.

Unit 7 Test Questions

  1. Calculate the energy of a photon with wavelength 550 nm.
  2. An electron has momentum 3 × 10⁻²⁴ kg·m/s. Find its de Broglie wavelength.
  3. A radioactive isotope has half-life 5 hours. Determine the remaining fraction after 15 hours.
  4. Calculate energy released if 0.01 kg of matter is converted to energy.
  5. Sketch the first three energy levels of hydrogen and label possible transitions.
  6. Explain why the photoelectric effect cannot be explained by classical wave theory.
  7. Write the nuclear equation for alpha decay of Uranium-238.
  8. Calculate the decay constant for a nuclide if 20% decays in 2 hours.
  9. Determine the kinetic energy of an electron ejected by light of frequency 1 × 10¹⁵ Hz on a metal with work function 2 eV.
  10. Discuss the significance of the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle in atomic measurements.
