Section 6.6: Series-Parallel RC Combinations

In circuits containing both series and parallel resistors with capacitors, effective resistance and capacitance determine charging and discharging rates.

Effective Capacitance:

Series: \[ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \sum_i \frac{1}{C_i} \], Parallel: \[ C_{eq} = \sum_i C_i \]

Time Constant:

For series combinations: \[ \tau = R_{eq} C_{eq} \]

Example 1

Compute the time constant of a circuit with a 10 kΩ resistor in series with two 5 µF capacitors in parallel.

Equivalent capacitance: \( C_{eq} = 5 + 5 = 10 \, \mu F \)

Time constant: \( \tau = R C_{eq} = 10\,000 \cdot 10 \times 10^{-6} = 0.1 \, s \)

Practice Problems

  1. Find \( C_{eq} \) for a series of three capacitors 2 µF, 4 µF, and 6 µF.
  2. Determine \( \tau \) for a 2 kΩ resistor in series with 3 µF in parallel with 2 µF.
  3. Sketch voltage across each capacitor in a series-parallel RC circuit.
  4. Explain why time constants differ for series vs parallel combinations.
  5. Calculate charge distribution in series-parallel RC network at t = τ.
