Section 7.4: Resonance

Resonance occurs when a system is driven at its natural frequency, causing a significant increase in amplitude. This is a key phenomenon in oscillatory systems, from mechanical to electrical.

Key Points:
  • Resonance condition: \( f_{drive} = f_{natural} \)
  • Amplitude at resonance is maximum.
  • Energy transfer is most efficient at resonance.
  • Damped systems show finite maximum amplitude.

Example 1

A 0.5 kg mass on a spring with spring constant \( k = 200 \text{ N/m} \) is driven by a periodic force. Find its natural frequency and the condition for resonance.

Natural frequency: \( f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi} \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \sqrt{\frac{200}{0.5}} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \sqrt{400} \approx \frac{20}{2\pi} \approx 3.18 \text{ Hz} \)

Resonance occurs when driving frequency \( f_{drive} \approx 3.18 \text{ Hz} \).

Practice Problems

  1. A mass-spring system has \( m = 1 \text{ kg}, k = 100 \text{ N/m} \). Calculate natural frequency and resonance frequency.
  2. An RLC circuit has \( L = 0.5 H, C = 50 \mu F \). Find its resonance frequency.
  3. Explain why bridges can collapse due to resonance.
  4. A forced oscillator has amplitude 0.2 m at resonance. How does damping affect amplitude?
  5. Calculate maximum amplitude of a driven oscillator with damping coefficient \( b = 0.1 \text{ kg/s} \).
  6. Describe practical applications of resonance in musical instruments.
  7. How does resonance affect tuning of radio circuits?
  8. Compute angular frequency at resonance for a pendulum of length 1 m.
  9. Explain why large amplitude at resonance is dangerous for structures.
  10. Discuss energy transfer in resonance of coupled oscillators.
