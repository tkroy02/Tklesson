Section 8.5: Fluid Dynamics: Continuity Equation

The continuity equation describes the conservation of mass in fluid flow. For an incompressible fluid, the volume flow rate is constant along a streamline.

Continuity Equation: \[ A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2 \] Where \(A\) is the cross-sectional area, \(v\) the fluid speed at a point along a pipe.
Key Implication: If the pipe narrows, the fluid speed increases to maintain constant flow rate.

Example 1

A pipe narrows from 0.5 m² to 0.2 m². Fluid speed in the wide section is 3 m/s. Find speed in narrow section.

\(v_2 = \frac{A_1 v_1}{A_2} = \frac{0.5*3}{0.2} = 7.5 \text{ m/s}\)

Example 2

Water flows in a pipe of varying diameter. Wide section 0.4 m², narrow 0.1 m². If \(v_2 = 12\) m/s, find \(v_1\).

\(v_1 = \frac{A_2 v_2}{A_1} = \frac{0.1*12}{0.4} = 3 \text{ m/s}\)

Practice Problems

  1. Fluid flows from 0.6 m² to 0.3 m². Initial speed 2 m/s. Find final speed.
  2. Pipe diameter reduces from 1 m to 0.5 m. Speed in narrow section 8 m/s. Find speed in wide section.
  3. Water through a nozzle, diameter 0.2 m, speed 10 m/s. Find speed at 0.1 m diameter.
  4. Pipe with constant flow rate 0.4 m³/s. Cross-section 0.2 m². Find fluid speed.
  5. Pipe network: 3 branches with areas 0.2, 0.1, 0.05 m². If flow is divided proportionally, find speeds in each branch.
  6. A horizontal pipe narrows, find velocity change if area halves.
  7. Pipe narrows from 0.5 m² to 0.25 m². If initial speed 6 m/s, final speed?
  8. Water speed doubles. How does area change if flow is incompressible?
  9. Pipe widens, speed drops to 1/3. Find area ratio.
  10. Fluid enters a constriction. Area halves. Find ratio of velocities.
