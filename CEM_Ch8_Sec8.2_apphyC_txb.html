Section 8.2: Induced Currents & EMF

When a conductor experiences a changing magnetic flux, not only is an EMF induced, but a current also flows if the circuit is closed. The magnitude and direction of the current are determined by the induced EMF and the circuit’s resistance.

Ohm’s Law for Induced Current:

\[ I = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R} \]

Where \(I\) is the induced current, \(\mathcal{E}\) is the induced EMF, and \(R\) is the resistance of the circuit.

Faraday’s Law for a Coil:

\[ \mathcal{E} = -N \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \]

For a coil with \(N\) turns, the EMF is multiplied by the number of turns.

Lenz’s Law:

The induced current flows such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

Example 1

A 10-turn coil of resistance 5 Ω has area 0.02 m². The magnetic field through the coil changes from 0 T to 0.3 T in 0.1 s. Calculate the induced current.

Induced EMF: \(\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t} = -10 \times \frac{0.02 \times 0.3}{0.1} = -0.6 \text{ V}\)

Induced current: \( I = \frac{|\mathcal{E}|}{R} = \frac{0.6}{5} = 0.12 \text{ A} \)

Practice Problems

  1. A 20-turn coil of area 0.01 m² is exposed to a magnetic field increasing from 0 to 0.5 T in 0.2 s. If resistance is 10 Ω, calculate the induced current.
  2. Explain how Lenz’s Law determines the direction of induced current in a loop when the magnetic field increases.
  3. A single loop of 0.05 m² rotates in a uniform 0.3 T magnetic field at 60 rpm. Calculate the peak EMF.
  4. Determine the induced current in a 5-turn coil with resistance 2 Ω if the flux decreases by 0.04 Wb in 0.05 s.
  5. A conductor rod of length 0.5 m moves perpendicularly through a 0.4 T magnetic field at 2 m/s. Find the induced EMF and current if the rod’s resistance is 8 Ω.
