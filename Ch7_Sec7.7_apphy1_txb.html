Section 7.7: Angular Momentum and Conservation

Angular momentum measures the rotational motion of an object and is conserved when no external torque acts:

Angular Momentum:
  • \(\vec{L} = I \vec{\omega}\)
  • \(I\) = moment of inertia, \(\omega\) = angular velocity
  • Torque and angular momentum relation: \(\tau_{ext} = \frac{d\vec{L}}{dt}\)
Conservation of Angular Momentum:
  • If \(\tau_{ext} = 0\), then \(L_i = L_f\)
  • Example: Figure skater spinning faster by pulling arms in (\(I\) decreases, \(\omega\) increases)

Example 1

A disk of I = 2 kg·m² spins at 3 rad/s. It is compressed to I = 1 kg·m². Find new angular velocity.

Initial angular momentum: \(L_i = I_i \omega_i = 2 \cdot 3 = 6\ \text{kg·m²/s}\)

Final angular velocity: \(\omega_f = L_i/I_f = 6/1 = 6\ \text{rad/s}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A spinning wheel of I = 5 kg·m² rotates at 4 rad/s. What is its angular momentum?
  2. A rotating sphere (I = 0.6 kg·m²) slows down from 10 rad/s to 6 rad/s. Find change in angular momentum.
  3. Two disks collide rotationally and stick together. Calculate final \(\omega\) using conservation of angular momentum.
