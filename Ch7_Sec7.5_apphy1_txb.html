Section 7.5: Rotational Dynamics and Newton’s Second Law

Newton’s second law for rotation relates torque, moment of inertia, and angular acceleration:

Rotational Form:
  • \(\tau = I \alpha\)
  • \(\tau\) = net torque
  • I = moment of inertia
  • \(\alpha\) = angular acceleration
Key Concepts:
  • Torque depends on force and distance from axis: \(\tau = r F \sin\theta\)
  • Angular acceleration is proportional to net torque
  • Rotational motion analogs: mass ↔ moment of inertia, force ↔ torque, acceleration ↔ angular acceleration

Example 1

A wheel of radius 0.5 m and I = 2 kg·m² is subjected to a torque of 4 N·m. Find its angular acceleration.

\(\alpha = \frac{\tau}{I} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \text{ rad/s²}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A solid cylinder of I = 1.5 kg·m² experiences 6 N·m torque. Find angular acceleration.
  2. Torque applied to a uniform rod of length 1.5 m and mass 2 kg about center: calculate \(\alpha\).
  3. Compare angular acceleration for two disks of same radius but different masses under same torque.
