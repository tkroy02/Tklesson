Section 2.10: Chapter 2 Test

This test assesses your understanding of gravitation, satellite motion, escape velocity, Kepler's laws, and applications of satellites.

Test Questions

  1. Two masses, 2000 kg and 500 kg, are 10 m apart. Calculate the gravitational force between them.
  2. A satellite orbits Earth at 400 km altitude. Determine its orbital speed and period.
  3. Calculate escape velocity from Earth and the Moon.
  4. Find the gravitational potential energy of a 600 kg satellite at 300 km above Earth.
  5. Using Kepler’s Third Law, find the orbital radius of a satellite with a period of 12 hours.
  6. Compute centripetal acceleration of a satellite at 800 km above Earth.
  7. Determine the speed required for a rocket to escape Mars’ gravity.
  8. Compare the gravitational force on satellites in low Earth orbit and medium Earth orbit.
  9. A 500 kg satellite moves in a circular orbit of radius 7000 km. Find the centripetal force.
  10. Explain why a geostationary satellite must have an orbital period of 24 h and orbit above the equator.
