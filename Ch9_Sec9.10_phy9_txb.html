Section 9.10: Chapter 9 Test

This test contains 20 questions covering calculations, diagrams, and conceptual understanding of light, mirrors, lenses, and the electromagnetic spectrum.

Questions

  1. Define light and state two of its properties.
  2. List all regions of the electromagnetic spectrum from shortest to longest wavelength.
  3. Explain the difference between specular and diffuse reflection.
  4. State the laws of reflection.
  5. Why is the image in a plane mirror always virtual and upright?
  6. An object is placed 15 cm from a concave mirror with focal length 10 cm. Find the image distance (\(v\)) using the mirror equation: \[ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} \]
  7. A convex mirror has a focal length of 20 cm. An object is placed 30 cm from the mirror. Find the image distance and magnification.
  8. A concave mirror forms an image twice the size of an object placed 15 cm from the mirror. Determine the image distance and focal length.
  9. Calculate the magnification of an image formed by a plane mirror placed 2 m from an object.
  10. An object is placed 12 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 8 cm. Calculate the image distance and state whether it is real or virtual.
  11. Define focal length and radius of curvature of a mirror.
  12. Differentiate between concave and convex mirrors in terms of image formation.
  13. Explain why a convex mirror always produces a diminished image.
  14. State two applications of concave mirrors in daily life.
  15. State two applications of convex mirrors in daily life.
  16. Draw a ray diagram showing the formation of a real image by a concave mirror.
  17. Draw a ray diagram showing the formation of a virtual image by a convex mirror.
  18. An object of height 5 cm is placed 10 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the height of the image.
  19. An object is placed 25 cm from a convex mirror with focal length 20 cm. Find the image distance and magnification.
  20. Calculate the image distance for a concave mirror when the object is at infinity. What type of image is formed?
