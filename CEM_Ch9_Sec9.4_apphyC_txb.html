Section 9.4: Energy in Magnetic Fields

Inductors store energy in their magnetic fields. The energy is proportional to the square of the current and the inductance.

Energy Stored in an Inductor:

\[ U = \frac{1}{2} L I^2 \]

Where \( U \) is the magnetic energy, \( L \) is the inductance, and \( I \) is the current.

Energy Density in Magnetic Field:

\[ u = \frac{B^2}{2\mu_0} \]

Where \( B \) is the magnetic flux density and \( \mu_0 \) the permeability of free space.

Example 1

An inductor of 0.3 H carries a current of 4 A. Find the energy stored in its magnetic field.

Energy stored: \( U = \frac{1}{2} L I^2 = 0.5 \cdot 0.3 \cdot 4^2 = 2.4 \, \text{J} \)

Practice Problems

  1. An inductor of 0.5 H carries a current of 2 A. Calculate the energy stored.
  2. A solenoid of length 0.2 m and cross-sectional area 0.01 m² has 200 turns. Current is 3 A. Calculate magnetic energy and energy density.
  3. Explain why energy in an inductor increases as current increases.
  4. Given \( B = 0.1 \, \text{T} \), find energy density in free space.
  5. Compare energy stored in two inductors of same inductance but different currents: I₁ = 2 A, I₂ = 4 A.
