Section 9.4: Energy in Magnetic Fields

The energy stored in an inductor or magnetic field arises from the work done to establish the current against the induced EMF. This is analogous to energy stored in a capacitor.

Energy Formula:

\[ U = \frac{1}{2} L I^2 \] where \( L \) is the inductance and \( I \) is the current.

Magnetic Energy Density:

\[ u = \frac{B^2}{2\mu_0} \] where \( B \) is the magnetic flux density, \( \mu_0 \) is the permeability of free space, and \( u \) is energy per unit volume.

Example 1

A 3 H inductor carries a current of 2 A. Calculate the energy stored.

\[ U = \frac{1}{2} L I^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3 \cdot 2^2 = 6 \text{ J} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the energy stored in a 0.5 H inductor carrying 4 A.
  2. Derive the energy density formula \( u = B^2 / 2 \mu_0 \) from first principles.
  3. Explain how energy in magnetic fields is analogous to energy in electric fields.
  4. For an RL circuit, determine the work done to establish steady current in the inductor.
  5. Provide a real-life example where magnetic energy storage is important.
