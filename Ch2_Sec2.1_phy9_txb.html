2.1 The Metric System

The metric system is a standardized system of measurement used worldwide in science, engineering, and daily life. It is based on powers of ten, making conversions straightforward. Units are related by factors of 10, 100, 1,000, and so on. Prefixes allow expressing very large or very small quantities efficiently.

Metric Prefixes

Metric prefixes denote multiples or fractions of base units. Here are the common ones:

PrefixSymbolMultiplierExample
TeraT10121 terameter (Tm) = 1,000,000,000,000 meters
GigaG1091 gigagram (Gg) = 1,000,000,000 grams
MegaM1061 megameter (Mm) = 1,000,000 meters
Kilok1031 kilogram (kg) = 1,000 grams
Hectoh1021 hectometer (hm) = 100 meters

Base Unit-1001 meter, 1 gram, 1 liter
Decid10-11 decimeter (dm) = 0.1 meters

Millim10-31 milligram (mg) = 0.001 grams
Microμ10-61 micrometer (µm) = 0.000001 meters
Nanon10-91 nanogram (ng) = 0.000000001 grams
Picop10-121 picometer (pm) = 0.000000000001 meters

Mass measures the amount of matter in an object. Common metric units include:

GramgEveryday small objects like a paperclip

Length Units

Length measures distance between two points. Common metric units:

UnitSymbolUse
MillimetermmVery small objects like screws

MetermHuman-scale distances like a room
KilometerkmLarge distances like city streets

TerameterTmInterstellar distances
Note: Conversion between metric units is simple because each step is a power of ten. For example, 1 kilometer = 1,000 meters, and 1 gram = 1,000 milligrams. Detailed conversions will be discussed in a later section.
