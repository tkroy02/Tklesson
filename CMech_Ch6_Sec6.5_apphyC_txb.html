Section 6.5: Driven Oscillations & Resonance

When a periodic driving force acts on an oscillator, the system undergoes driven oscillations. Resonance occurs when the driving frequency matches the natural frequency.

Driven Harmonic Motion:

Equation: \( m\ddot{x} + b\dot{x} + kx = F_0 \cos(\omega t) \)

Amplitude response: \( A(\omega) = \frac{F_0/m}{\sqrt{(\omega_0^2 - \omega^2)^2 + (2\gamma\omega)^2}} \)

Resonance condition: \( \omega = \omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m} \)

Example 1

A 1 kg oscillator with k = 100 N/m, b = 2 kg/s, driven by F0 = 10 N at frequency ω = 10 rad/s. Find amplitude.

\( \omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m} = \sqrt{100/1} = 10 \text{ rad/s} \)

\( \gamma = b/(2m) = 2/2 = 1 \text{ s}^{-1} \)

\( A = \frac{F_0/m}{\sqrt{(\omega_0^2 - \omega^2)^2 + (2\gamma\omega)^2}} = \frac{10/1}{\sqrt{(10^2-10^2)^2 + (2*1*10)^2}} = 0.5 \text{ m} \)

Practice Problems

  1. A 0.5 kg oscillator with k = 50 N/m, b = 0.5 kg/s, F0 = 5 N, ω = 5 rad/s. Compute amplitude.
  2. Find resonance frequency of a 2 kg spring-mass system with k = 200 N/m.
  3. A driven oscillator has γ = 0.2 s⁻¹, F0 = 8 N. Compute amplitude at ω = ω0.
  4. Determine amplitude of a damped driven oscillator with m = 1 kg, k = 80 N/m, b = 1 kg/s, F0 = 10 N, ω = 9 rad/s.
  5. For a system with m = 1.5 kg, k = 90 N/m, b = 0.3 kg/s, calculate ωd and amplitude at driving frequency ω = 7 rad/s.
