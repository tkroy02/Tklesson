Section 8.7: Atomic Models Review

This section reviews the key atomic models developed to explain experimental observations, including Thomson's, Rutherford's, Bohr's, and Quantum Mechanical models.

Key Models:
  • Thomson Model: Atom is a "plum pudding" with electrons in positive charge.
  • Rutherford Model: Atom has a dense nucleus with electrons orbiting around it.
  • Bohr Model: Electrons occupy quantized orbits; energy emitted/absorbed in transitions.
  • Quantum Mechanical Model: Electron cloud represents probability distribution; incorporates wave mechanics.

Example 1

Explain why Rutherford’s model could not explain atomic spectra.

Rutherford’s model predicted that electrons orbiting the nucleus would radiate energy continuously and spiral into the nucleus, which does not match the observed discrete spectral lines. Bohr’s quantization resolved this.
