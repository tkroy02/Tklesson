Section 1.4: Complex Numbers & Rational Expressions

This section introduces complex numbers, their arithmetic, and how to simplify and operate with rational expressions.

Example 1: Complex Number Arithmetic

Simplify \( (3 + 2i) + (1 - 4i) \).

Step 1: Add real parts: 3 + 1 = 4

Step 2: Add imaginary parts: 2i - 4i = -2i

Result: \( 4 - 2i \)

Example 2: Simplifying a Rational Expression

Simplify \( \frac{x^2 - 9}{x^2 - 6x + 9} \).

Step 1: Factor numerator and denominator: \( \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x-3)^2} \)

Step 2: Cancel common factor: \( \frac{x+3}{x-3} \)
