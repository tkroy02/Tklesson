Section 12.4: Applications & Review

In this section, we bring together concepts from systems of nonlinear equations, inequalities, and optimization to solve real-world application problems. These problems often combine algebraic modeling with graphical or computational methods.

Example 1: Business Application

A bakery produces cakes and pies. Each cake requires 3 hours of labor, each pie requires 2 hours. The bakery has at most 60 labor hours available. Cakes bring \$25 profit, pies \$15. If the bakery wants to maximize profit, how many cakes and pies should they produce?

Let \( x \) = cakes, \( y \) = pies.

Constraint: \( 3x + 2y \leq 60 \), \( x,y \geq 0 \).

Profit: \( P = 25x + 15y \).

Vertices: (0,0), (20,0), (0,30), (10,15).

Check: (20,0)=500, (0,30)=450, (10,15)=475. → Max profit = \$500 at (20,0).

Example 2: Science Application

A biologist wants to build a rectangular enclosure for animals with 300 m of fencing. One side is along a river, so only 3 sides need fencing. What dimensions maximize the area?

Let width = \( x \), length = \( y \). Constraint: \( 2x + y = 300 \).

Area: \( A = xy = x(300 - 2x) = 300x - 2x^2 \).

Max at vertex: \( x = 75 \), \( y = 150 \). Max area = 11,250 m².

Practice & Review Problems

  1. A factory makes two types of toys, each requiring labor and material. Write and solve an optimization problem if toy A requires 4 hours, toy B requires 3 hours, and labor is limited to 120 hours. Profits: \$10 for A, \$8 for B.
  2. A rectangle has perimeter 100 m. Find the dimensions that maximize its area.
  3. Solve the system: \( y = x^2 + 2 \) and \( y = 2x + 3 \). Interpret the intersection.
  4. Maximize \( P = 6x + 4y \) subject to: \( x + 2y \leq 16 \), \( 2x + y \leq 18 \), \( x,y \geq 0 \).
  5. A shipping company can load at most 500 kg. Boxes A weigh 20 kg, boxes B weigh 50 kg. Profits: \$30 for A, \$60 for B. Formulate and solve the optimization problem.
