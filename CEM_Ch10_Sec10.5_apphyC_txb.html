Section 10.5: Maxwell’s Equations in Free Space

Maxwell’s equations in free space summarize all classical electromagnetic phenomena, relating electric and magnetic fields to their sources and to each other.

Gauss’s Law for Electricity:

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} \]

Gauss’s Law for Magnetism:

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0 \]

Faraday’s Law:

\[ \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = - \frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \]

Ampère-Maxwell Law:

\[ \nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \mathbf{J} + \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t} \]

Implications:
  • Electric and magnetic fields can propagate as waves in free space.
  • Displacement current term allows changing electric fields to generate magnetic fields.
  • Forms the foundation of classical electromagnetism and electromagnetic wave theory.

Example 1

Show that Maxwell’s equations predict the existence of electromagnetic waves in free space and derive their speed.

Take curl of Faraday’s Law: \( \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf{E}) = - \frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla \times \mathbf{B}) \).

Use Ampère-Maxwell Law in free space (\( \mathbf{J}=0 \)):

\( \nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t} \)

Then wave equation: \( \nabla^2 \mathbf{E} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2} \)

Wave speed: \( c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}} \)

  4. Explain why displacement current is essential for wave propagation in vacuum.
  5. Use Maxwell’s equations to determine the relation between E and B in a plane electromagnetic wave.
