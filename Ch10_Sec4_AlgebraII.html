Section 10.4: Regression & Correlation

This section covers linear regression, correlation coefficients, and interpreting the strength and direction of relationships between variables.

Example 1: Linear Regression Equation

Given the data points (1,2), (2,3), (3,5), find the regression line equation.

Step 1: Compute slope: m = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁) for best fit → here m ≈ 1.5

Step 2: Compute intercept using mean values: b = ȳ - m x̄ → b ≈ 0.5

Step 3: Regression line: y ≈ 1.5x + 0.5

Example 2: Correlation Coefficient

Interpret r = 0.85 for a dataset of students’ study hours vs. test scores.

Step 1: r = 0.85 indicates a strong positive correlation

Step 2: As study hours increase, test scores tend to increase
