Section 7.1: Angular Displacement, Velocity & Acceleration

This section introduces rotational kinematics. We describe motion of rotating bodies using angular displacement, angular velocity, and angular acceleration.

Angular Displacement (\(\theta\)): The angle through which a point or line has been rotated in a specified sense about a specified axis.
Angular Velocity (\(\omega\)): Rate of change of angular displacement: \(\omega = \frac{d\theta}{dt}\)
Angular Acceleration (\(\alpha\)): Rate of change of angular velocity: \(\alpha = \frac{d\omega}{dt}\)

Example 1

A wheel rotates from rest to 10 rad/s in 5 s. Find its angular acceleration.

\(\alpha = \frac{\omega - \omega_0}{t} = \frac{10-0}{5} = 2 \text{ rad/s²}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A wheel rotates 30 rad in 10 s from rest. Find average angular velocity.
  2. A fan accelerates from 100 rpm to 300 rpm in 6 s. Find angular acceleration in rad/s².
  3. A disc rotates with constant angular acceleration 4 rad/s². Initial velocity 2 rad/s. Find angular velocity after 3 s.
