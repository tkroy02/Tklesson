Section 7.9: Mixed Practice

This section combines multiple concepts from quantum physics, including energy levels, wavefunctions, and transitions, allowing practice in solving integrated problems.

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the first three energy levels of a particle in a 2 nm infinite potential well.
  2. Determine the wavelength of light emitted when a particle transitions from level 3 to 1 in a 1D box of length 1 nm.
  3. Normalize the wavefunction \( \psi(x) = A \sin(2\pi x/L) \) in a box of length L.
  4. Sketch the wavefunction and probability density for n=2 in a box of length 1 nm.
  5. Explain the significance of nodes in wavefunctions.
  6. Compute the energy difference for a particle transitioning from n=4 to n=2 in a 3 nm box.
  7. Apply boundary conditions to a particle encountering a finite potential step and discuss reflection probabilities.
  8. Use the Schr√∂dinger equation to find energy eigenvalues for a 1D harmonic oscillator (conceptual).
  9. Describe qualitatively how energy levels change when a finite well replaces an infinite well.
  10. Solve an integrated problem involving energy levels, transitions, and photon emission.
