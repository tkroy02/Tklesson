4.10 Chapter 4 Test

This test covers all key concepts from Chapter 4: Newton’s Laws, net force, friction, free-body diagrams, weight and gravity, and real-world applications. Answer all questions. Show your calculations where required.

  1. A ball continues to roll on a smooth, level surface without slowing down. Which of Newton’s laws explains this behavior?
    A. First Law
    B. Second Law
    C. Third Law
    D. Law of gravitation
  2. A 10 kg box is pushed with a force of 40 N to the right. The frictional force opposing the motion is 10 N.
    Calculate the net force acting on the box and the acceleration.
  3. A rocket expels gas downward with a force of 5000 N. If the total mass of the rocket is 1000 kg, determine its upward acceleration.
    \( F = ma \)
  4. Identify the action-reaction pair in the case of a swimmer pushing against the water to move forward.
  5. A 60 kg student is standing on Earth.
    Calculate their weight in newtons. (Take \( g = 9.8 \, m/s^2 \))
  6. A 50 N horizontal force is applied to a sled, but it remains stationary. Explain what must be happening in terms of friction and net force.
  7. Draw a free-body diagram for a block sliding down an inclined plane with friction.
  8. A car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. If its mass is 1000 kg, find the net force acting on the car.
  9. Explain why astronauts appear weightless while orbiting the Earth, even though gravity is still acting on them.
  10. A box is being pushed to the right with 100 N, while a frictional force of 30 N acts to the left. The box moves at a constant speed. What is the net force, and what does this tell you about the motion?
  11. A block is on an inclined plane. Explain how you would resolve the forces acting on the block to determine whether it will slide down.
  12. A 25 kg cart is pulled with a force of 50 N. Ignore friction.
    Find the acceleration of the cart.
  13. A motorcyclist riding at high speed spots a sinkhole 5 seconds before reaching it but still rides into it.
    Could this accident have been avoided the moment the motorcyclist first saw the hole? Explain using concepts of reaction time, braking force, and Newton’s laws.
  14. Two people push a stalled car. One exerts a force of 400 N east, the other exerts 300 N east. The friction opposing motion is 200 N west.
    Find the net force on the car and its acceleration if the mass of the car is 800 kg.
  15. A rocket launches vertically upward. List all forces acting on it and explain which is greater at lift-off for it to move upward.
  16. A 5 kg object is dropped from rest. Ignore air resistance.
    Find its velocity after 3 seconds.
  17. Describe the combined effects of friction and air resistance on a cyclist moving at high speed.
  18. A block is pushed across a rough surface with a constant velocity.
    What does this tell you about the relationship between the applied force and the frictional force?
  19. A person standing on a scale in an elevator notices the reading increase as the elevator starts moving upward.
    Explain why this happens using Newton’s second law.
  20. A truck of mass 2000 kg experiences a net force of 6000 N.
    Calculate its acceleration.
