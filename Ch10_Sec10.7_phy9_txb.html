Section 10.7: Series Circuit

A series circuit is a circuit in which electrical components are connected end-to-end so that there is only one path for current to flow. If any component is disconnected or fails, the entire circuit is broken and the current stops.

Properties of Series Circuits

  • The same current flows through all components.
  • The total voltage supplied by the source is divided among the components.
  • The total resistance is the sum of individual resistances: \[ R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + \cdots \]
  • If one component is removed, the entire circuit stops working.

Example 1: Current in a Series Circuit

Problem: A 12 V battery is connected in series with three resistors: \(R_1 = 2 \, \Omega\), \(R_2 = 3 \, \Omega\), and \(R_3 = 5 \, \Omega\). Find the total resistance, the current in the circuit, and the voltage drop across each resistor.

Solution:

  1. Total resistance: \[ R_{\text{total}} = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 \, \Omega \]
  2. Current in the circuit: \[ I = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{12}{10} = 1.2 \, \text{A} \]
  3. Voltage across each resistor: \[ V_1 = I \times R_1 = 1.2 \times 2 = 2.4 \, \text{V} \] \[ V_2 = I \times R_2 = 1.2 \times 3 = 3.6 \, \text{V} \] \[ V_3 = I \times R_3 = 1.2 \times 5 = 6.0 \, \text{V} \]

Answer: Total resistance = 10 Ω, current = 1.2 A, voltage drops = 2.4 V, 3.6 V, and 6.0 V respectively.

Example 2: Equal Resistors in Series

Problem: A 9 V battery is connected across three identical resistors of 6 Ω each in series. Find the total resistance, the current, and the voltage drop across each resistor.

Solution:

  1. Total resistance: \[ R_{\text{total}} = 6 + 6 + 6 = 18 \, \Omega \]
  2. Current in the circuit: \[ I = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{9}{18} = 0.5 \, \text{A} \]
  3. Each resistor has the same voltage drop: \[ V = I \times R = 0.5 \times 6 = 3 \, \text{V} \]

Answer: Total resistance = 18 Ω, current = 0.5 A, voltage across each resistor = 3 V.

Example 3: Series Lamps — Find the Unknown Lamp Resistance and the EMF

Problem: Three lamps are connected in series to a battery. Lamp A has resistance \(R_A = 8\,\Omega\), Lamp B has resistance \(R_B = 12\,\Omega\), and Lamp C has unknown resistance \(R_C\). The current measured in the circuit is \(I = 0.50\ \text{A}\). The power dissipated by Lamp C is measured to be \(P_C = 4.50\ \text{W}\).

Find: (a) the resistance of Lamp C, \(R_C\), and (b) the total source voltage (EMF) of the battery, \( \mathcal{E} \).

Figure 10.7.2: Three lamps connected in series to a battery.

Given:

  • \(R_A = 8\,\Omega\)
  • \(R_B = 12\,\Omega\)
  • \(R_C = ?\)
  • Current \(I = 0.50\ \text{A}\)
  • Power in lamp C: \(P_C = 4.50\ \text{W}\)

Solution:

  1. Use the power relation for lamp C: \[ P_C = I^2 R_C \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_C = \frac{P_C}{I^2}. \] Substitute values: \[ R_C = \frac{4.50}{(0.50)^2} = \frac{4.50}{0.25} = 18\ \Omega. \]
  2. Now compute the total resistance in the series circuit: \[ R_{\text{total}} = R_A + R_B + R_C = 8 + 12 + 18 = 38\ \Omega. \]
  3. The total source voltage (EMF) is given by Ohm's law: \[ \mathcal{E} = I\,R_{\text{total}} = 0.50 \times 38 = 19.0\ \text{V}. \]

Answer: \(R_C = 18\ \Omega\). The total source voltage (EMF) is \(\mathcal{E} = 19.0\ \text{V}\).

Notes: Because the lamps are in series the same current flows through each lamp. We used the power measured for lamp C together with the known current to determine its resistance, then applied Ohm's law to find the battery's EMF.

Example 4: Find Unknown Resistance and Voltage Drops

Problem: Three lamps are connected in series to a 45 V battery. Two lamps have resistances \(R_1 = 10\,\Omega\) and \(R_2 = 15\,\Omega\). The total current in the circuit is \(I = 1.0\ \text{A}\). Determine:

  • (a) the unknown resistance \(R_3\)
  • (b) the voltage drop across each lamp

Figure 10.7.4: Three lamps connected in series with one unknown resistance.

Given:

  • Battery voltage \(V_{\text{total}} = 45\ \text{V}\)
  • Resistors/lamps: \(R_1 = 10\,\Omega\), \(R_2 = 15\,\Omega\), \(R_3 = ?\)
  • Current: \(I = 1.0\ \text{A}\)

Solution:

  1. Find total resistance using Ohm's law: \[ R_{\text{total}} = \frac{V_{\text{total}}}{I} = \frac{45}{1.0} = 45\,\Omega \]
  2. Compute the unknown resistor: \[ R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_3 = R_{\text{total}} - R_1 - R_2 \] \[ R_3 = 45 - 10 - 15 = 20\,\Omega \]
  3. Voltage drop across each lamp: \[ V_1 = I \times R_1 = 1.0 \times 10 = 10\ \text{V} \] \[ V_2 = I \times R_2 = 1.0 \times 15 = 15\ \text{V} \] \[ V_3 = I \times R_3 = 1.0 \times 20 = 20\ \text{V} \]

Answer: \(R_3 = 20\,\Omega\), voltage drops: \(V_1 = 10\ \text{V}\), \(V_2 = 15\ \text{V}\), \(V_3 = 20\ \text{V}\).

Check: Sum of voltage drops equals battery voltage: \[ V_1 + V_2 + V_3 = 10 + 15 + 20 = 45\ \text{V}. \]

Key Points

  • Series circuits allow only one path for current.
  • Current is the same through all components.
  • Voltage divides according to resistance values.
  • Total resistance increases as more resistors are added.
  • If one component fails, the entire circuit stops working.
