Section 1.3: Polynomial Functions

This section introduces polynomial functions, including their standard form, degree, leading coefficient, end behavior, and graphing techniques.

Example 1: Identifying Degree and Leading Coefficient

Determine the degree and leading coefficient of \( f(x) = 4x^3 - 2x^2 + x - 7 \).

Step 1: Highest power of x is 3 → degree = 3

Step 2: Coefficient of \( x^3 \) is 4 → leading coefficient = 4

Example 2: Graphing a Cubic Function

Sketch the graph of \( g(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 2x \).

Step 1: Factor: \( g(x) = x(x-1)(x-2) \)

Step 2: Identify zeros: x = 0, 1, 2

Step 3: Analyze end behavior: degree 3 (odd), leading coefficient 1 → falls left, rises right
