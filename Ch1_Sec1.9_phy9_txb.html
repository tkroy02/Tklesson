Section 1.9: Review Questions

This section provides a variety of questions to test your understanding of Chapter 1: Introduction & Scientific Method. Questions are categorized by difficulty levels.

Level 1: Basic Understanding

  1. Define physics in your own words.
  2. List three real-world applications of physics.
  3. Explain the difference between a theory and a law.
  4. What is the importance of the scientific method in physics?
  5. Give an example of a dependent and independent variable in a physics experiment.
  6. Express the number 0.000567 in scientific notation (standard form).
  7. Round 0.0047852 to 3 significant figures.

Level 2: Application

  1. A car travels 120 km in 2 hours. Calculate its average speed.
  2. If a box has a mass of 8 kg and accelerates at 3 m/sÂ², find the force acting on it.
  3. Explain why constants are not considered variables in an experiment.
  4. By following the scientific method, work on the project "A football floating on water".
  5. Add the following numbers in standard form: \(3.2 \times 10^4\), \(4.5 \times 10^5\), and \(2.1 \times 10^3\). Show proper steps.
  6. Multiply the following numbers in standard form: \(2.0 \times 10^3\), \(5.0 \times 10^4\), and \(1.5 \times 10^2\). Show proper steps.
  7. Identify the coefficient, constant, dependent and independent variables in the equation \(y = 8x^2 + 5\).

Level 3: Challenge

  1. A student drops two objects from the same height. Object A is heavier than object B. Neglect air resistance. Explain what happens and why.
  2. Analyze a scenario where a car accelerates, decelerates, and turns. Identify the forces acting and the direction of acceleration at each stage.
  3. Create a table showing at least five examples of dependent and independent variables in everyday physics experiments.
  4. Explain the relationship between theories and laws using a real-world example.

Key Takeaways

  • Regular review helps reinforce concepts learned in Chapter 1.
  • Understanding the difference between theories, laws, constants, and variables is crucial for solving problems and conducting experiments.
  • Practice applying formulas, reasoning, and standard form calculations to gain mastery of basic physics concepts.
