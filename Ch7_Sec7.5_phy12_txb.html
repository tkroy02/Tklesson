Section 7.5: Energy in Oscillations

Oscillating systems store energy in two main forms: kinetic and potential. For a simple harmonic oscillator, the total mechanical energy remains constant in the absence of damping.

Energy Expressions:
  • Kinetic Energy: \( KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 (A^2 - x^2) \)
  • Potential Energy: \( PE = \frac{1}{2} k x^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 x^2 \)
  • Total Energy: \( E = KE + PE = \frac{1}{2} k A^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 A^2 \)
  • Energy oscillates between KE and PE as the mass moves.

Example 1

A 0.5 kg mass attached to a spring with \( k = 200 \text{ N/m} \) oscillates with amplitude 0.1 m. Find the total energy, kinetic energy at \( x = 0.05 \) m, and potential energy at that position.

Total energy: \( E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2 = 0.5 * 200 * 0.1^2 = 1 \text{ J} \)

Potential energy at \( x = 0.05 \): \( PE = 0.5 * 200 * 0.05^2 = 0.25 \text{ J} \)

Kinetic energy at \( x = 0.05 \): \( KE = E - PE = 1 - 0.25 = 0.75 \text{ J} \)
