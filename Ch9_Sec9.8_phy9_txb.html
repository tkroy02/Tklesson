Section 9.8: Mirror Equation

The mirror equation relates the object distance (\(u\)), image distance (\(v\)), and focal length (\(f\)) of a spherical mirror:

Mirror Equation:

\[ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} \]

Where:

  • \(f\) = focal length of the mirror
  • \(u\) = object distance from mirror (positive if in front)
  • \(v\) = image distance from mirror (positive for real images, negative for virtual)

Example 1: Concave Mirror

Problem: An object is placed 10 cm from a concave mirror with focal length 15 cm. Find the image distance.

Given:

  • \(u = 10 \, \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(f = 15 \, \mathrm{cm}\) (concave, positive)

Solution:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{10} = \frac{2 - 3}{30} = -\frac{1}{30} \] \[ v = -30 \, \mathrm{cm} \]

Answer: Image forms 30 cm behind the mirror (virtual and upright).

Example 2: Convex Mirror

Problem: An object is placed 20 cm from a convex mirror with focal length 25 cm. Find the image distance.

Given:

  • \(u = 20 \, \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(f = -25 \, \mathrm{cm}\) (convex, negative)

Solution:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{-25} - \frac{1}{20} = -\frac{4}{100} - \frac{5}{100} = -\frac{9}{100} \] \[ v = -11.11 \, \mathrm{cm} \]

Answer: Image forms 11.11 cm behind the mirror (virtual, upright, diminished).

Example 3: Magnification

Problem: For the convex mirror in Example 2, find the magnification.

Solution:

\[ m = -\frac{v}{u} = -\frac{-11.11}{20} = 0.556 \]

Answer: Image is virtual, upright, and about 0.56 times the size of the object.

Key Points

  • Concave mirrors: focal length positive, can produce real or virtual images.
  • Convex mirrors: focal length negative, always produce virtual, upright, diminished images.
  • Mirror equation works for all spherical mirrors.
  • Magnification \(m = -v/u\) indicates size and orientation.
