Section 6.9: Chapter Review Questions

Answer the following questions to consolidate your understanding of circular motion, forces, and orbital mechanics.

  1. Define centripetal and centrifugal forces, and explain how they differ in inertial and rotating frames.
  2. A car of mass 1200 kg travels over a hill of radius 30 m at 20 m/s. Calculate the normal force at the top.
  3. A rollercoaster car of mass 500 kg passes through a dip of radius 15 m at 10 m/s. Find the apparent weight at the bottom.
  4. Derive the expression for the minimum speed at the top of a vertical loop to maintain contact.
  5. A satellite of mass 1000 kg orbits the Earth at a radius of 7000 km. Write the expression for the gravitational force and calculate it using \(G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\) and \(M_\text{Earth} = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\) kg.
  6. Explain why centrifugal force is considered a fictitious force.
  7. Using the car-turning analogy, describe the role of inertia and centripetal force.
  8. A 2 kg ball is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1.5 m at 3 m/s. Calculate the tension in the string.
  9. Compare normal forces at the top and bottom of a vertical circular motion path for the same speed and radius.
  10. Explain how the radius of curvature affects the normal force in vertical circular motion.
  11. Derive the formula \(v_\text{min} = \sqrt{g r}\) for a rollercoaster loop from Newton's second law.
  12. Compute the centrifugal force experienced by a 70 kg person on a carousel of radius 3 m rotating at 2 m/s.
  13. Why do engineers include safety margins when designing rollercoaster loops and hills?
  14. Explain apparent weight and how it varies through a crest and dip.
  15. Calculate tension at the top and bottom of a vertical circle for a 0.5 kg mass, r = 1 m, v = 4 m/s.
  16. Discuss limitations of ideal circular motion formulas (assumptions: rigid body, no air resistance, perfect circular path).
  17. A satellite orbits Earth at speed 7.8 km/s. Calculate the centripetal acceleration and gravitational force acting on it.
  18. Explain why astronauts feel weightless in orbit despite gravity still acting on them.
  19. How does increasing speed affect centrifugal force and tension in circular motion?
  20. Summarize the relationship between centripetal force, centrifugal force, inertia, normal force, and apparent weight in circular motion.
