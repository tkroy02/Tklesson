Section 4.5: Energy Stored in Capacitors

A charged capacitor stores potential energy in the electric field between its plates. This energy comes from the work done to move charge onto the plates.

Energy in a Capacitor:

\[ U = \tfrac{1}{2} C V^2 = \tfrac{1}{2} \frac{Q^2}{C} = \tfrac{1}{2} QV \]

These equivalent forms allow flexibility depending on known quantities \(Q, V,\) or \(C\).

Energy Density of the Electric Field:

\[ u = \frac{U}{\text{Volume}} = \tfrac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 E^2 \]

This represents energy stored per unit volume in the electric field.

Example 1

A \(20 \, \mu\text{F}\) capacitor is charged to \(200 \, \text{V}\). Find the stored energy.

Using \(U = \tfrac{1}{2} C V^2\):

\[ U = \tfrac{1}{2} (20 \times 10^{-6})(200^2) \]

\[ U = 0.4 \, \text{J} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Derive the expression \(U = \tfrac{1}{2} C V^2\) from work done in charging.
  2. A \(50 \, \mu\text{F}\) capacitor is charged to \(100 \, \text{V}\). Find stored energy.
  3. Show that the energy density in a capacitor can be written as \(\tfrac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 E^2\).
  4. If a capacitor is disconnected from a battery and dielectric inserted, what happens to stored energy?
  5. Explain in words where the energy of a capacitor is physically stored.
