Section 8.1: Faraday’s Law of Induction

Electromagnetic induction occurs when a changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in a conductor. Faraday's Law quantifies this phenomenon.

Faraday’s Law:

\[ \mathcal{E} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \]

Where \(\mathcal{E}\) is the induced EMF and \(\Phi_B\) is the magnetic flux through the loop.

Lenz’s Law:

The induced current flows in a direction that opposes the change in flux.

Magnetic Flux:

\[ \Phi_B = \int \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} \]

It measures the total magnetic field passing through a surface.

Example 1

A coil of 50 turns with area 0.02 m² is placed in a magnetic field that changes from 0 to 0.3 T in 0.1 s. Calculate the induced EMF.

Magnetic flux change: \(\Delta \Phi_B = B \cdot A = 0.3 \times 0.02 = 0.006 \text{ Wb}\)

EMF: \(\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t} = -50 \times \frac{0.006}{0.1} = -3 \text{ V}\)

Practice Problems

  1. A single-turn loop of area 0.05 m² is in a magnetic field decreasing from 0.4 T to 0.1 T in 0.2 s. Find the induced EMF.
  2. Explain why the negative sign appears in Faraday’s Law.
  3. A 100-turn coil has a changing flux of 0.01 Wb in 0.05 s. Calculate the EMF induced.
  4. State and explain Lenz's Law in your own words.
  5. A rectangular coil rotates in a uniform magnetic field. Describe how the flux and EMF vary with time.
