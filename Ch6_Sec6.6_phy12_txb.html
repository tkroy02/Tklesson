Section 6.6: Power in AC Circuits

In AC circuits, the instantaneous power delivered is:

\( p(t) = v(t) \cdot i(t) \)

With sinusoidal sources, average power is expressed as:

\( P_{avg} = VI \cos \phi \)

  • \(V\) and \(I\) are rms voltage and current.
  • \(\phi\) is the phase angle between current and voltage.
  • \(\cos \phi\) is called the power factor.

A purely resistive circuit has \(\phi = 0\), so all power is real. In purely reactive circuits (\(L\) or \(C\)), \(\phi = \pm 90^\circ\) and average power is zero.

Example

An AC circuit has \(V = 120 \,\text{V (rms)}\), \(I = 5 \,\text{A (rms)}\), and a phase angle of \(\phi = 30^\circ\). Find the average power delivered.

Solution:

\[ P_{avg} = VI \cos \phi = (120)(5)(\cos 30^\circ) \]

\(= 600 \times 0.866 \approx 520 \,\text{W}\).

The average power delivered to the circuit is approximately 520 W.
