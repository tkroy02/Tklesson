Section 8.9: RLC Series Circuits

An RLC series circuit contains a resistor (R), inductor (L), and capacitor (C) connected in series with an AC source. The voltage, current, and phase relationships are influenced by resistance, inductive reactance, and capacitive reactance.

Total Impedance:

\[ Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \]

Where \( X_L = 2 \pi f L \) and \( X_C = \frac{1}{2 \pi f C} \).

Current in RLC Series Circuit:

\[ I = \frac{V_0}{Z} \]

Voltage and current may not be in phase; the phase angle is:

\[ \phi = \arctan\left(\frac{X_L - X_C}{R}\right) \]

Resonance:

Occurs when \( X_L = X_C \). At resonance, impedance is minimum (\( Z = R \)) and current is maximum.

Example 1

An RLC series circuit has R = 10 Ω, L = 0.1 H, C = 100 μF, connected to 50 Hz AC, 12 V. Find the total impedance and peak current.

Inductive reactance: \( X_L = 2 \pi f L = 2 \pi \cdot 50 \cdot 0.1 \approx 31.42 \, \Omega \)

Capacitive reactance: \( X_C = \frac{1}{2 \pi f C} = \frac{1}{2 \pi \cdot 50 \cdot 100 \times 10^{-6}} \approx 31.83 \, \Omega \)

Impedance: \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} = \sqrt{10^2 + (31.42 - 31.83)^2} \approx 10 \, \Omega \)

Peak current: \( I_0 = \frac{V_0}{Z} = \frac{12}{10} = 1.2 \, \text{A} \)

Practice Problems

  1. An RLC series circuit has R = 20 Ω, L = 0.05 H, C = 50 μF, connected to 60 Hz AC, 24 V. Find total impedance and peak current.
  2. Explain the condition for resonance in an RLC series circuit and its significance.
  3. A circuit has X_L = 40 Ω, X_C = 30 Ω, R = 10 Ω. Calculate total impedance and phase angle.
  4. Describe what happens to the current if frequency increases in an RLC series circuit.
  5. Determine the resonance frequency for an RLC series circuit with L = 0.2 H and C = 50 μF.
