Section 8.9: Energy in Magnetic Fields

The energy stored in a magnetic field is given by:

\[ U_B = \frac{1}{2\mu_0} \int B^2 \, d\tau \]

Here, \(B\) is the magnetic field and \(\mu_0\) is the permeability of free space. The integral is over the volume containing the field.

Key Concept:

Magnetic energy can be visualized as stored in the field itself, analogous to the energy stored in capacitors for electric fields.

Example 1

Find the energy stored in a solenoid with magnetic field \(B\), cross-sectional area \(A\), and length \(L\).

Volume of solenoid: \(V = AL\)

\[ U_B = \frac{1}{2\mu_0} B^2 AL \]

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate energy stored in a toroid of given dimensions and current.
  2. Compare magnetic energy for two solenoids of different lengths but same current density.
  3. Conceptual: Explain how magnetic energy density relates to field intensity.
  4. Compute energy in a region with superposed fields from two sources.
  5. Demonstrate conservation of energy in an RL circuit using magnetic energy.
