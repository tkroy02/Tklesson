Section 10.5: Applications & Review

This section combines all previous statistics concepts—data analysis, measures of central tendency, standard deviation, regression, and correlation—for real-world problem solving and review exercises.

Example 1: Real-World Regression

A company tracks advertising spend vs. sales revenue. Use the regression line y = 3x + 50 to predict sales if they spend $20k on advertising.

Step 1: Substitute x = 20 → y = 3(20) + 50

Step 2: y = 60 + 50 = 110

Step 3: Predicted sales = $110k

Example 2: Correlation Interpretation

Given a dataset of hours studied vs. exam score, r = 0.92. Explain the relationship and predict if a student studies 8 hours.

Step 1: r = 0.92 → very strong positive correlation

Step 2: More study hours → higher exam score

Step 3: Use regression line (if available) to predict score for 8 hours
