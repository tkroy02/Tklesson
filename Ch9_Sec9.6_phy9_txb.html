Section 9.6: Concave Mirrors

A concave mirror is a spherical mirror that curves inward like the inside of a bowl. It can converge light rays to a focal point.

Mirror Formula

The mirror formula relates focal length (\(f\)), object distance (\(u\)), and image distance (\(v\)):

\[ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} \]

Rearranging to find image distance:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} \]

Sign conventions:

  • Distances measured in the same direction as the incident light are positive.
  • Concave mirror focal length is positive.

Example 1: Object Beyond Center of Curvature

Problem: An object is placed 10 cm from a concave mirror with focal length 5 cm. Find the image distance.

Given:

  • \(u = 10 \, \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(f = 5 \, \mathrm{cm}\)

Solution:

Use the mirror formula:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} \]

Substitute values:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{10} = \frac{2-1}{10} = \frac{1}{10} \]

Invert to get image distance:

\[ v = 10 \, \mathrm{cm} \]

Answer: The image forms 10 cm from the mirror, real and inverted.

Example 2: Object at Focal Point

Problem: An object is placed at the focal point of a concave mirror with \(f = 8 \, \mathrm{cm}\). Find the image distance.

Given:

  • \(u = 8 \, \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(f = 8 \, \mathrm{cm}\)

Solution:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{8} - \frac{1}{8} = 0 \]

So, the image distance:

\[ v = \infty \]

Answer: The image forms at infinity; rays are parallel after reflection.

Example 3: Object Between Focal Point and Mirror

Problem: An object is 3 cm in front of a concave mirror with \(f = 5 \, \mathrm{cm}\). Find the image distance and nature of the image.

Given:

  • \(u = 3 \, \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(f = 5 \, \mathrm{cm}\)

Solution:

\[ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3-5}{15} = -\frac{2}{15} \]

Invert:

\[ v = -7.5 \, \mathrm{cm} \]

Answer: The image is virtual, upright, and formed 7.5 cm behind the mirror.

Key Points

  • Concave mirrors converge light to a focal point.
  • The mirror formula is \(\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v}\).
  • Real images: formed on the same side as reflected rays, inverted.
  • Virtual images: formed on opposite side of mirror, upright.
  • Magnification \(m = -\frac{v}{u}\).
