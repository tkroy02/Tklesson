Section 7.8: Magnetic Dipole Moment

A current loop behaves like a magnetic dipole. The magnetic dipole moment is a vector quantity that determines the torque and potential energy of the loop in an external magnetic field.

Magnetic Dipole Moment:

\[ \vec{\mu} = n I \vec{A} \]

where \( n \) is the number of turns, \( I \) the current, and \( \vec{A} \) the vector area of the loop.

Torque on a Magnetic Dipole:

\[ \vec{\tau} = \vec{\mu} \times \vec{B} \]

The loop experiences torque that tends to align it with the magnetic field.

Potential Energy of a Magnetic Dipole:

\[ U = -\vec{\mu} \cdot \vec{B} = -\mu B \cos\theta \]

Minimum energy occurs when the magnetic dipole aligns with the field.

Example 1

A single rectangular loop of area \(0.05\,\text{m}^2\) carrying a current of \(3\,\text{A}\) is placed in a uniform magnetic field of \(0.2\,\text{T}\). If the plane of the loop makes 60° with the field, calculate the torque on the loop.

Magnetic dipole moment: \[ \mu = nIA = 1 \times 3 \times 0.05 = 0.15\,\text{A·m²} \]

Torque: \[ \tau = \mu B \sin\theta = 0.15 \times 0.2 \times \sin 60^\circ \approx 0.026\,\text{N·m} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate the magnetic dipole moment for a 4-turn circular coil of radius 0.1 m carrying 2 A current.
  2. A rectangular coil of area 0.02 m² carrying 5 A is placed in a 0.1 T field at 45°. Find the torque.
  3. Explain why a current loop behaves like a magnetic dipole.
  4. Compute the potential energy of a 3-turn coil of area 0.01 m² in a 0.3 T field at 30° angle.
  5. Discuss how increasing the number of turns affects torque and potential energy for a given magnetic field.
