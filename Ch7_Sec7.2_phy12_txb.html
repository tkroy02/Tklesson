Section 7.2: Photoelectric Effect

The photoelectric effect occurs when light strikes a metal surface and ejects electrons. It provided key evidence for the particle nature of light, leading to the concept of photons.

Key Equations:
  • Photon energy: \( E = h\nu \)
  • Maximum kinetic energy of ejected electron: \( K_{\text{max}} = h\nu - \phi \)
  • Work function: \( \phi \) is the minimum energy to release an electron.

Example 1

A light of wavelength 400 nm falls on a metal with work function \( 2.5 \text{ eV} \). Find the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons.

\( E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{6.626\times10^{-34} \cdot 3\times10^8}{400\times10^{-9}} = 4.97\times10^{-19} \text{ J} = 3.1 \text{ eV} \)

\( K_{\text{max}} = E - \phi = 3.1 - 2.5 = 0.6 \text{ eV} \)
