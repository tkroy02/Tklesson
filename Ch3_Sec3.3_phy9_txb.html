3.3 Speed and Velocity

Speed and velocity are related concepts that describe how fast an object moves. The key difference is that velocity also considers the direction of motion.

Key Concepts:
  • Speed (scalar): Distance traveled per unit time. It does not include direction.
  • Velocity (vector): Displacement per unit time. It includes both magnitude and direction.

Formulas

  • Average speed: \(v_{avg} = \frac{\text{Total distance}}{\text{Total time}}\)
  • Average velocity: \(\vec{v}_{avg} = \frac{\text{Displacement}}{\text{Total time}}\)
  • Instantaneous speed/velocity: Speed or velocity at a particular instant in time

Examples

  1. A car travels 120 km in 2 hours. Speed: \(v = \frac{120}{2} = 60 \text{ km/h}\)
  2. A runner moves 100 m north in 20 s. Velocity: \(\vec{v} = \frac{100 \text{ m north}}{20 \text{ s}} = 5 \text{ m/s north}\)
  3. A cyclist goes 50 m east then 50 m west in 30 s. Speed: \(\frac{50+50}{30} = 3.33 \text{ m/s}\) Velocity: \(\frac{0}{30} = 0 \text{ m/s}\)
