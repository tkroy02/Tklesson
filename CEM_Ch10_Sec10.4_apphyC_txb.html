Section 10.4: Ampère-Maxwell Law

The Ampère-Maxwell Law extends Ampère’s Law to include displacement current, allowing consistency with a time-varying electric field and forming one of Maxwell’s equations.

Ampère-Maxwell Law (Integral Form):

\[ \oint_C \mathbf{B} \cdot d\mathbf{l} = \mu_0 \left( I_\text{enc} + \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt} \right) \]

Here, \( I_\text{enc} \) is the conduction current, \( \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt} \) is the displacement current contribution, and \( \mu_0 \) is the vacuum permeability.

Differential Form:

\[ \nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \mathbf{J} + \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t} \]

Shows that the curl of the magnetic field is generated by conduction currents and time-varying electric fields.

Applications:
  • Magnetic field around a charging capacitor (displacement current important)
  • Electromagnetic wave propagation in free space
  • Current in conductors and time-varying electric fields in circuits

Example 1

A parallel-plate capacitor with area A and plate separation d is being charged with current I. Find the magnetic field at a distance r from the axis inside the plates.

Choose a circular Amperian loop of radius r concentric with the plates. The displacement current: \( I_d = \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt} = \epsilon_0 \frac{d}{dt}(E A) = I \) (same as conduction current).

By Ampère-Maxwell Law:

\[ B (2 \pi r) = \mu_0 \frac{I r^2}{R^2} \implies B = \frac{\mu_0 I r}{2 \pi R^2} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Use Ampère-Maxwell Law to find the magnetic field between the plates of a charging capacitor.
  2. Compare the contributions of conduction and displacement currents in a capacitor circuit.
  3. Show that the magnetic field around a long straight wire is consistent with the Ampère-Maxwell Law.
  4. Explain how the displacement current allows Maxwell’s equations to predict electromagnetic waves.
  5. Derive the magnetic field at a distance r from the axis of a solenoid carrying a time-varying current.
