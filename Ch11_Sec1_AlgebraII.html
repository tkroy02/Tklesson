Section 11.1: Trig Ratios & Right Triangles

This section introduces sine, cosine, and tangent ratios and how to apply them to right triangles.

Example 1: Finding a Side Using a Trig Ratio

In a right triangle, if θ = 30° and the hypotenuse = 10, find the length of the opposite side.

Step 1: Use sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse

Step 2: sin 30° = opposite / 10 → 0.5 = opposite / 10

Step 3: opposite = 10 × 0.5 = 5

Example 2: Finding an Angle

Given a right triangle with opposite side = 6 and adjacent side = 8, find θ using tan θ.

Step 1: tan θ = opposite / adjacent = 6 / 8 = 0.75

Step 2: θ = arctan(0.75) ≈ 36.87°
