Section 8.7: Differential Form ∇×B = μ₀J

The differential form of Ampère’s Law connects the curl of the magnetic field to the current density:

\[ \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 \vec{J} \]

Where \(\vec{B}\) is the magnetic field, \(\vec{J}\) is the current density, and \(\mu_0\) is the permeability of free space.

Interpretation:

This form allows applying Ampère’s Law locally and is foundational for Maxwell’s equations.

Example 1

Show that the curl of the magnetic field around a long straight wire yields the expected field.

Assuming cylindrical coordinates and \(\vec{J}\) along the z-axis, the curl gives:

\[ \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 J \hat{z} \quad \Rightarrow \quad B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Compute ∇×B for a solenoid with uniform current density.
  2. Derive the magnetic field inside a toroid using differential form.
  3. Compare results of differential and integral forms for a straight wire.
  4. Explain why ∇·B = 0 complements ∇×B = μ₀J.
  5. Conceptual: Can differential form be applied to non-uniform current distributions?
