Section 8.7: AC Circuits with Inductance

In AC circuits, inductors oppose changes in current through self-induction. This introduces reactance, which affects the amplitude and phase of current and voltage.

Inductive Reactance:

\[ X_L = 2 \pi f L \]

Where \( X_L \) is the reactance, \( f \) is the AC frequency, and \( L \) is the inductance.

Current in AC Inductor:

\[ I(t) = \frac{V_0}{X_L} \sin\left(\omega t - \frac{\pi}{2}\right) \]

Current lags voltage by \(90^\circ\) in a purely inductive AC circuit.

Applications:
  • AC transformers
  • Chokes for filtering AC signals
  • Inductive energy storage and tuning circuits

Example 1

An AC circuit contains a 0.1 H inductor connected to a 50 Hz supply of 12 V. Find the inductive reactance and the peak current.

Inductive reactance: \( X_L = 2 \pi f L = 2 \pi \cdot 50 \cdot 0.1 \approx 31.42 \, \Omega \)

Peak current: \( I_0 = \frac{V_0}{X_L} = \frac{12}{31.42} \approx 0.382 \, \text{A} \)
