Section 8.1: Density and Pressure

Density is defined as mass per unit volume:

\[ \rho = \frac{m}{V} \] where \( \rho \) is density, \( m \) is mass, and \( V \) is volume.

Pressure is force applied per unit area:

\[ P = \frac{F}{A} \] where \( P \) is pressure, \( F \) is force, and \( A \) is area.

Units: \(\text{Density: kg/m³}\), \(\text{Pressure: Pa (N/m²)}\).

Applications

  • Determining buoyant forces on objects in fluids.
  • Calculating stresses in engineering structures.
  • Understanding fluid statics and dynamics.

Example 1

A cube of metal has a mass of 2 kg and side length 0.1 m. Find its density.

Volume: \( V = 0.1^3 = 0.001 \, \text{m³} \)

Density: \( \rho = \frac{2}{0.001} = 2000 \, \text{kg/m³} \)

Example 2

A force of 50 N is applied over an area of 0.02 m². Find the pressure.

\( P = \frac{F}{A} = \frac{50}{0.02} = 2500 \, \text{Pa} \)

Practice Problems

  2. A metal rod of volume 0.003 m³ has mass 15 kg. Find density.
  3. Pressure exerted by a 100 N force on area 0.1 m².
  4. Determine density of a liquid if 500 g occupies 0.00025 m³.
  5. Force applied 200 N on area 0.5 m². Calculate pressure.
  6. Mass 3 kg, volume 0.002 m³. Find density in kg/m³.
  7. Pressure under 400 N force on 0.04 m² surface.
  8. A block of 2 kg has volume 0.0025 m³. Find density.
  9. Pressure on a floor from 600 N applied over 0.3 m².
  10. Density of a 0.8 kg liquid occupying 0.001 m³.
