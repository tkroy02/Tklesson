Section 7.9: Chapter 7 Review Questions

Answer the following questions to review all concepts covered in Chapter 7:

  1. Define temperature and thermal energy. How do they differ?
  2. Convert 25°C to Kelvin and Fahrenheit.
  3. A 0.5 kg cup of water is heated from 20°C to 80°C. Calculate the thermal energy absorbed (\(c = 4186 \, \mathrm{J/kg \cdot K}\)).
  4. Explain the difference between specific heat capacity (\(c\)) and heat capacity (\(C\)).
  5. What is latent heat? Distinguish between latent heat of fusion (\(L_f\)) and vaporization (\(L_v\)).
  6. Calculate the energy required to melt 0.2 kg of ice at 0°C (\(L_f = 334{,}000 \, \mathrm{J/kg}\)).
  7. Calculate the energy needed to vaporize 0.1 kg of water at 100°C (\(L_v = 2.26 \times 10^6 \, \mathrm{J/kg}\)).
  8. Explain how specific heat capacity (\(c\)) affects heating of different substances.
  9. Explain the three modes of heat transfer: conduction, convection, and radiation.
  10. Give five examples each of conduction, convection, and radiation in daily life.
  11. Explain why thermal radiation depends on temperature (\(T\)) and wavelength (\(\lambda\)). Distinguish between infrared, visible, and ultraviolet radiation.
  12. State the first law of thermodynamics: \(\Delta U = Q - W\) and provide an example.
  13. A system absorbs 500 J of heat (\(Q\)) and does 200 J of work (\(W\)). Calculate the change in internal energy (\(\Delta U\)).
  14. Explain the second law of thermodynamics in simple terms.
  15. Define linear (\(\Delta L = \alpha L_0 \Delta T\)), area (\(\Delta A = 2 \alpha A_0 \Delta T\)), and volume (\(\Delta V = \beta V_0 \Delta T\)) expansion.
  16. A 2 m aluminum rod (\(\alpha = 2.3 \times 10^{-5} \, \mathrm{K^{-1}}\)) is heated by 50 K. Find the change in length (\(\Delta L\)).
  17. A 1 m² metal plate (\(\alpha = 1.2 \times 10^{-5} \, \mathrm{K^{-1}}\)) is heated by 100 K. Find the change in area (\(\Delta A\)).
  18. Explain why \(\beta \approx 3 \alpha\) for solids.
  19. Summarize the key postulates of the kinetic theory of matter.
  20. Explain how kinetic theory accounts for pressure (\(P\)) in gases.
  21. Define a heat engine and write the formula for its efficiency: \(\eta = \frac{W}{Q_H} \times 100\%\).
  22. If a heat engine absorbs \(Q_H = 1200 \, \mathrm{J}\) from a hot reservoir and expels \(Q_C = 800 \, \mathrm{J}\) to a cold reservoir, calculate the work done (\(W\)) and efficiency (\(\eta\)).
  23. Explain the concept of a Carnot engine and calculate its maximum efficiency if \(T_H = 600 \, \mathrm{K}\) and \(T_C = 300 \, \mathrm{K}\): \(\eta_\text{max} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}\).
  24. Give two real-life examples of heat engines.
