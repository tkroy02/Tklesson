Section 8.5: Ampère’s Law

Ampère’s Law relates the magnetic field around a closed loop to the total current passing through the loop.

Mathematical Form:

\[ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{\ell} = \mu_0 I_{\text{enc}} \] where \( I_{\text{enc}} \) is the net current enclosed by the path.

Key Concept:

For high-symmetry geometries, Ampère’s Law simplifies calculation of the magnetic field, such as for long straight wires, solenoids, and toroids.

Example 1

Find the magnetic field at a distance \( r \) from a long straight wire carrying current \( I \).

Choose an Ampèrian loop as a circle of radius \( r \) around the wire. By symmetry:

\[ B (2 \pi r) = \mu_0 I \quad \Rightarrow \quad B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r} \]

Practice Problems

  1. Derive the magnetic field inside a solenoid using Ampère’s Law.
  2. Compute the field inside a toroid using Ampère’s Law.
  3. Explain why the field outside an ideal solenoid is negligible.
  4. Apply Ampère’s Law to a coaxial cable and find the field in each region.
  5. Discuss the limitations of Ampère’s Law in non-symmetric situations.
