Section 10.6: Electric Power

Electric power is the rate at which electrical energy is converted into another form (heat, light, motion, etc.). The SI unit of power is the watt (W): \(1\ \mathrm{W} = 1\ \mathrm{J/s}\).

Key Definitions

  • Power (P): Rate of energy conversion. Unit: watt (W).
  • Energy (E): Capacity to do work. Unit: joule (J).
  • Time (t): Duration of operation. Unit: second (s) — often converted to hours for practical billing.

Power Formulas

Basic relation:

\[ P = V I \]

Using Ohm's law \( V = IR \) we get two alternative useful forms:

\[ P = I^2 R \quad\text{and}\quad P = \dfrac{V^2}{R} \]

Energy related to power and time:

\[ E = P t \]

Practical unit: kilowatt-hour (kWh) used for household energy billing:

\(1\ \mathrm{kWh} = 1000\ \mathrm{W} \times 3600\ \mathrm{s} = 3.6 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{J}\).

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Power from Voltage & Current

Problem: A lamp is connected to \(120\ \mathrm{V}\) and draws \(0.5\ \mathrm{A}\). Find the power.

Given: \(V=120\ \mathrm{V},\; I=0.5\ \mathrm{A}\).

Solution:

\[ P = VI = 120 \times 0.5 = 60\ \mathrm{W} \]

Answer: \(60\ \mathrm{W}\).

Example 2 — Power using Resistance

Problem: A resistor \(R=20\ \Omega\) carries current \(I=3\ \mathrm{A}\). Find power.

Given: \(R=20\ \Omega,\; I=3\ \mathrm{A}\).

Solution:

\[ P = I^2 R = 3^2 \times 20 = 9 \times 20 = 180\ \mathrm{W} \]

Answer: \(180\ \mathrm{W}\).

Example 3 — Power using Voltage & Resistance

Problem: A device connected to \(V=12\ \mathrm{V}\) has \(R=6\ \Omega\). Find power.

Given: \(V=12\ \mathrm{V},\; R=6\ \Omega\).

Solution:

\[ P = \dfrac{V^2}{R} = \dfrac{12^2}{6} = \dfrac{144}{6} = 24\ \mathrm{W} \]

Answer: \(24\ \mathrm{W}\).

Example 4 — Energy & kWh

Problem: A 1500 W kettle runs for 5 minutes. Find energy used in joules and kWh.

Given: \(P=1500\ \mathrm{W},\; t=5\ \mathrm{min}=300\ \mathrm{s}\).

Solution:

\[ E = Pt = 1500 \times 300 = 450\,000\ \mathrm{J} = 4.5 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{J} \]

Convert to kWh:

\[ E = \dfrac{450\,000}{3.6 \times 10^6}\ \mathrm{kWh} = 0.125\ \mathrm{kWh} \]

Answer: \(4.5 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{J}\) or \(0.125\ \mathrm{kWh}\).

Summary

  • Electrical power is the rate of energy conversion, measured in watts (W).
  • Main formulas: \(P = VI\), \(P = I^2R\), \(P = \dfrac{V^2}{R}\).
  • Energy consumed over time: \(E = Pt\) (convert to kWh for billing).
  • Remember units: \(1\ \mathrm{kWh} = 3.6 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{J}\).

Practice Questions

  1. A heater operates at \(240\ \mathrm{V}\) and draws \(10\ \mathrm{A}\). Find the power consumed.
  2. A resistor of \(15\ \Omega\) carries a current of \(4\ \mathrm{A}\). How much power is dissipated?
  3. A device connected to a \(9\ \mathrm{V}\) battery has \(R=3\ \Omega\). Calculate the power used.
  4. An electric kettle is rated at \(1500\ \mathrm{W}\) and operates on \(250\ \mathrm{V}\). Find the current drawn.
  5. A \(60\ \mathrm{W}\) bulb is connected to \(120\ \mathrm{V}\). What is its resistance?
  6. A \(2\ \mathrm{kW}\) heater runs for 3 hours. How much energy does it consume (in J and kWh)?
