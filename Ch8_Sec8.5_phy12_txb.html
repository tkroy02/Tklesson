Section 8.5: Binding Energy

Binding energy is the energy required to disassemble a nucleus into its constituent protons and neutrons. It reflects the stability of a nucleus and is crucial for understanding nuclear reactions.

Key Concepts:
  • Mass Defect: \( \Delta m = \text{(sum of nucleon masses)} - \text{(nuclear mass)} \)
  • Binding Energy: \( E_b = \Delta m c^2 \)
  • High binding energy per nucleon indicates a stable nucleus.
  • Binding energy differences drive energy release in fission and fusion.

Example 1

Calculate the binding energy of a helium-4 nucleus, given the mass of nucleus = 4.0015 u, proton = 1.0073 u, neutron = 1.0087 u.

Mass defect: \( \Delta m = (2*1.0073 + 2*1.0087) - 4.0015 = 0.0305 \text{ u} \)

Convert to kg: \( 0.0305 * 1.6605*10^{-27} \text{ kg} = 5.06*10^{-29} \text{ kg} \)

Binding energy: \( E = \Delta m c^2 = 5.06*10^{-29} * (3*10^8)^2 \approx 4.55*10^{-12} \text{ J} \)
